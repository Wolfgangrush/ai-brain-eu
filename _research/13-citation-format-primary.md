## Currency Warning
The ECLI (European Case-Law Identifier) was fully implemented by the CJEU in 2014. Since 2023, the Court has moved to using fictitious names for preliminary ruling cases involving natural persons.

## Key Facts
1. **ECLI (European Case-Law Identifier):** The uniform identifier for all EU case law. Format: `ECLI:[jurisdiction]:[court]:[year]:[number]`.
2. **ECJ ECLI:** `ECLI:EU:C:[year]:[number]` (e.g., ECLI:EU:C:2005:446).
3. **General Court ECLI:** `ECLI:EU:T:[year]:[number]` (e.g., ECLI:EU:T:2010:450).
4. **Case Numbers:** Prefixed by 'C-' for Court of Justice and 'T-' for General Court (e.g., Case C-403/03).
5. **ELI (European Legislation Identifier):** Used for citing legislation. It uses a persistent URI. Format: `http://data.europa.eu/eli/[type]/[year]/[number]/oj`.
6. **CELEX Number:** A unique ID for every document in EUR-Lex (e.g., 32016R0679 for the GDPR). It is structured: [sector][year][type][number].
7. **Official Journal (OJ):** The official repository of EU law. Cited by Year, Series (L or C), Issue Number, and Page (e.g., OJ L 119, 4.5.2016, p. 1).
8. **Neutral Citation Requirement:** The CJEU requires the use of ECLI in all pleadings and documents submitted to the court.
9. **Fictitious Names:** Since 2023, for privacy, cases like *C-286/25* would be cited as *C-286/25 BRANDL* where "BRANDL" is a randomly generated name.
10. **Secondary Sources:** Academic journals (e.g., *Common Market Law Review*) or textbooks are cited using standard Bluebook or OSCOLA (International) styles, but primary EU identifiers take precedence.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The ECLI... is a uniform identifier that has the same format for all Member States and EU courts. It is composed of five mandatory components." — CURIA Guide.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
When drafting submissions for a national court or the CJEU, the advocate MUST use the ECLI to ensure the judge and opposing counsel can immediately locate the primary source on CURIA or EUR-Lex. Relying on old volume/page citations (e.g., [2005] ECR I-06421) is deprecated.
