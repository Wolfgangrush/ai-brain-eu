# Bar Rules: Publicity and Solicitation — European Union (CCBE)

## Source URL
https://www.ccbe.eu/fileadmin/speciality_distribution/public/documents/DEONTOLOGY/DEON_CoC/EN_DEON_CoC.pdf

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule (CCBE Code of Conduct)

## Currency Warning
The CCBE Code of Conduct is primarily binding for **cross-border** activities. However, it serves as the model for most national Bar rules within the EU. National variations (e.g., stricter bans on advertising in some countries) still apply for purely domestic practice.

## Key Facts
1. **Personal Publicity:** A lawyer is entitled to inform the public about their services, provided the information is accurate, not misleading, and respectful of confidentiality and other core values (Rule 2.6.1).
2. **Professional Secrecy:** Confidentiality is a primary and fundamental right and duty of the lawyer. It is not just a client privilege but a core tenet of the administration of justice (Rule 2.3.1).
3. **Independence:** A lawyer must be free from all influence, especially such as may arise from his/her personal interests or external pressure (Rule 2.1.1).
4. **Conflict of Interest:** A lawyer may not advise, represent, or act on behalf of two or more clients in the same matter if there is a conflict, or a significant risk of conflict, between the interests of those clients (Rule 3.2.1).
5. **Incompatible Occupations:** A lawyer may not engage in an occupation that would impair their independence or dignity (Rule 2.5).
6. **Fee Sharing:** A lawyer may not share fees with persons who are not lawyers (with specific exceptions for certain multi-disciplinary practices in some jurisdictions) (Rule 3.6.1).
7. **Relations with Other Lawyers:** Correspondence between lawyers from different Member States is generally confidential unless stated otherwise (Rule 5.3).
8. **Client Funds:** All funds of clients must be deposited in an account of a bank or similar institution subject to supervision by a public authority (Rule 3.8.1).
9. **Professional Indemnity Insurance:** Lawyers must be insured against liability for professional negligence to an extent which is reasonable (Rule 3.9).
10. **Sanctions:** Violations of the Code are subject to disciplinary proceedings by the lawyer’s home Bar or the host Bar where they are practicing.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Personal publicity... shall be allowed in so far as it provides information to the public which is truthful, not misleading and does not violate the lawyer's professional secrecy and other core values." — Rule 2.6.1.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates operating across EU borders (e.g., a Polish lawyer taking a case in Ireland) must adhere to the CCBE Code. They can advertise their services online, but must avoid "aggressive" solicitation or claims that guarantee results, as these often violate the requirement of "dignity" and "integrity" common to all European legal professions.
