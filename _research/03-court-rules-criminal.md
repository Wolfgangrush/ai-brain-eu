## Currency Warning
The EPPO became operational on June 1, 2021. The European Arrest Warrant (EAW) framework has been refined by several CJEU judgments regarding judicial independence (e.g., the 'L and P' cases).

## Key Facts
1. **European Public Prosecutor's Office (EPPO):** An independent EU body empowered to investigate and prosecute crimes against the EU's financial interests (e.g., VAT fraud >€10m, corruption, money laundering).
2. **EPPO Structure:** Operates at two levels: a Central Office (Luxembourg) and a decentralized level consisting of European Delegated Prosecutors (EDPs) located in each participating Member State.
3. **European Arrest Warrant (EAW):** A judicial decision issued by a Member State for the arrest and surrender by another Member State of a requested person for the purposes of conducting a criminal prosecution or executing a custodial sentence.
4. **Mutual Recognition:** The EAW is based on high trust between Member States; it replaced traditional extradition and removed the "double criminality" requirement for 32 categories of serious crimes.
5. **Time Limits for EAW:** A decision on surrender must be made within 10 days (if the person consents) or 60 days (if they do not) after arrest.
6. **Procedural Rights Directives:** The EU has established minimum standards for: Right to interpretation/translation (2010/64/EU), Right to information (2012/13/EU), and Right of access to a lawyer (2013/48/EU).
7. **European Evidence Warrant (EEW) / European Investigation Order (EIO):** Allows judicial authorities to request evidence (documents, testimony) from another Member State as if it were a domestic request.
8. **EPPO Jurisdiction:** The EPPO has priority (evocation) over national authorities for crimes within its scope (PIF crimes).
9. **Rights of Suspects:** Suspects in EPPO investigations have all rights granted by national law and the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights.
10. **Judicial Review:** Procedural acts of the EPPO are generally subject to judicial review by national courts, while certain decisions may be challenged before the CJEU.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The European Arrest Warrant... is the first concrete measure in the field of criminal law implementing the principle of mutual recognition." — Preamble, Framework Decision 2002/584/JHA.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Advocates defending clients against an EAW must act within extremely tight deadlines (often days). They should focus on grounds for non-execution, such as systemic deficiencies in the issuing State's judicial independence or potential violations of the *ne bis in idem* principle. In EPPO cases, the advocate deals with a "European" prosecutor but within the framework of national procedural law.
