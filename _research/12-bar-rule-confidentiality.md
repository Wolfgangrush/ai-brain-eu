## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule (CCBE Code of Conduct)

## Key Facts
1. **Fundamental Value:** Confidentiality is a primary and fundamental right and duty of the lawyer. It is of the essence of a lawyer's function that they should be told things that the client would not tell to others (Rule 2.3.1).
2. **Scope of Duty:** The obligation of confidentiality is not limited in time; it continues after the lawyer has ceased to act for the client (Rule 2.3.2).
3. **Protection of Secrets:** A lawyer must require their partners and staff and any person engaged by them in the course of providing professional services to observe the same obligation of confidentiality (Rule 2.3.3).
4. **Professional Secrecy vs. Legal Privilege:** While often used interchangeably, "Professional Secrecy" is the lawyer's duty, while "Legal Professional Privilege" (LPP) is the legal protection against the disclosure of communications in court or to authorities.
5. **Exceptions:** In most EU jurisdictions, the duty is absolute unless the client waives it. However, narrow exceptions exist for preventing a serious crime or in the lawyer's own defense in a fee dispute or malpractice claim.
6. **AML Overriding:** AML regulations (see File 27) create a limited "carve-out" where lawyers must report suspicions of money laundering, though this generally excludes information obtained in the course of "ascertaining the legal position" of the client.
7. **Correspondence between Lawyers:** In some jurisdictions (e.g., France), correspondence between lawyers is "confidential" by default and cannot be produced in court. In others (e.g., UK), it is "without prejudice" only if it is a genuine attempt to settle. The CCBE seeks to harmonize this for cross-border work.
8. **Digital Security:** The duty of confidentiality extends to the security of digital communications. Lawyers must use encryption and secure storage to protect client data from hackers.
9. **Inadvertent Disclosure:** If a lawyer receives a document that clearly appears to have been sent in error and is confidential, they should inform the sender and follow national rules on whether it can be used.
10. **Sanctions:** Breach of confidentiality is a criminal offense in some EU countries (e.g., France, Art. 226-13 Penal Code) and a severe disciplinary offense in all.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The lawyer's duty of confidentiality serves the interest of the administration of justice as well as the interest of the client. It is entitled to particular protection by the State." — Rule 2.3.1.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must use secure email (e.g., ProtonMail or encrypted attachments) and avoid public Wi-Fi when handling client files. They must also ensure that any cloud storage provider used (e.g., Dropbox, OneDrive) is GDPR compliant and that the lawyer maintains the "keys" to the data.
