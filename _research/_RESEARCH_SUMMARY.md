## Key Authoritative sources used
- **Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU):** Primary law basis for all EU regulations.
- **Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act):** The world's first comprehensive AI law.
- **Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR):** The global benchmark for data protection.
- **Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 (Brussels I-bis):** Cornerstone of EU civil procedure and enforcement.
- **Regulation (EC) No 593/2008 (Rome I):** Governing law for contractual obligations.
- **CCBE Code of Conduct for European Lawyers:** The ethical framework for cross-border practice.
- **European e-Justice Portal:** The primary digital hub for EU judicial cooperation.
- **CURIA (CJEU Database):** The official repository of EU case law and ECLI identifiers.

## Known gaps
- **National Implementation of AI Act:** As the Act is new, Member State-specific "regulatory sandboxes" and local AI Office branches are still being established. Needs monitoring.
- **Detailed Tariffs for all 27 Member States:** While summaries are provided for the top 7, the remaining 20 rely on high-level data from the e-Justice Portal.
- **Specific E-Filing Plugins:** Many national systems (like LexNET or ERV) require specific hardware (smart cards) or software plugins that vary by OS and browser.

## Currency warnings to surface
- **AML Package 2024:** The new AMLR and 6AMLD will not apply until July 2027. Current practice still relies on the 5th AML Directive.
- **AI Act Timelines:** Prohibitions apply from Feb 2025, but the majority of "High-Risk" obligations do not kick in until Aug 2026.
- **Digitalisation Regulation (2023/2844):** Full mandatory digital communication for cross-border cases is still in the "phasing-in" period.
