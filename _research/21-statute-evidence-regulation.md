## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation (EU) 2020/1783 - Recast)

## Currency Warning
Fully applicable since July 1, 2022. The mandatory use of the decentralized IT system (e-CODEX) for transmitting requests became obligatory on **May 1, 2025**.

## Key Facts
1. **Scope:** Cooperation between courts of different Member States (except Denmark) for the taking of evidence in civil/commercial matters.
2. **Methods:** 
   - **Request to Foreign Court:** Court A asks Court B to hear a witness or inspect documents.
   - **Direct Taking:** Court A hears the witness directly in State B (subject to authorization).
3. **Videoconferencing (Art 20):** If a person is to be heard in another Member State, the requesting court *should* use videoconferencing or other distance communication technology if available.
4. **Speed:** The requested court must acknowledge receipt within 7 days and execute the request within **90 days**.
5. **Language:** Requests must be in the official language of the requested state or another language they have declared they accept (usually English).
6. **No Coercion:** Coercive measures can only be applied by the requested court under its own national law.
7. **Direct Electronic Transmission:** Requests are now handled through a secure decentralized IT system, eliminating paper delays.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Where evidence is to be taken by hearing a person ... the requesting court shall take that evidence by videoconference or other distance communication technology." - Article 20, Regulation 2020/1783.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Remote Witnesses:** A solo advocate can now easily arrange for a witness in another EU state to testify via Zoom/Teams directly to the court hearing the case, without the witness needing to travel.
- **Standard Forms:** Use the "Form A" (Request for the taking of evidence) available on the e-Justice portal.
- **Cost Efficiency:** Reduces the need to hire local counsel in the foreign state just to attend a witness hearing.
