# 22-statute-service-regulation.md

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32020R1784

## Last Verified
2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation (EU) 2020/1784 - Recast)

## Currency Warning
Fully applicable since July 1, 2022. Mandatory e-CODEX transmission for agencies effective **May 1, 2025**.

## Key Facts
1. **Scope:** Service of judicial/extrajudicial documents between Member States (including Denmark).
2. **Direct Electronic Service (Art 19):** Documents can be served directly by email or other electronic means if the addressee has given **prior express consent**.
3. **Right of Refusal:** The addressee can refuse the document if it's not in a language they understand or the official language of the place of service. They now have **2 weeks** to exercise this right (using Form L).
4. **Postal Service:** Service by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt is a valid alternative to agency-to-agency transmission.
5. **Assistance in Address Search:** Member States must provide a service to help find the address of the person to be served (e.g., via population registers).
6. **Central IT System:** Agency-to-agency service is now digital and instantaneous via e-CODEX.
7. **No Legalisation:** Documents served under this Regulation are exempt from legalisation or any equivalent formality (Apostille).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Service of judicial documents may be effected directly by electronic means ... provided that the addressee has given prior express consent." - Article 19, Regulation 2020/1784.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Consent in Contracts:** Advocates should include a clause in contracts where parties "expressly consent to service of documents via the following email address" to trigger Article 19 and avoid slow postal service.
- **Translation Check:** Always verify the language of the addressee before serving, to prevent a "Refusal" that resets the timeline.
- **e-Justice Portal:** Use the portal to generate the mandatory multilingual forms (Forms A-L).
