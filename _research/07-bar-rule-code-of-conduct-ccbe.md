# 07-bar-rule-code-of-conduct-ccbe.md

## Source URL
https://www.ccbe.eu/documents/professional-regulations/

## Last Verified
2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Bar Rule (CCBE Code of Conduct for European Lawyers)

## Currency Warning
The Code applies to "cross-border activities." For purely domestic activities, the national bar rules of the Member State apply. However, most national rules are heavily aligned with the CCBE Code.

## Key Facts
1. **Independence:** A lawyer must be free from all influence, especially such as may arise from their personal interests or external pressure.
2. **Confidentiality (Professional Privilege):** It is of the essence of a lawyer’s function that the lawyer should be told by the client things which the client would not tell to others. Confidentiality is a primary and fundamental right and duty of the lawyer.
3. **Conflict of Interest:** A lawyer may not advise, represent or act on behalf of two or more clients in the same matter if there is a conflict, or a significant risk of a conflict, between the interests of those clients.
4. **Publicity/Advertising:** A lawyer is entitled to inform the public about their services, provided that the information is accurate, not misleading, and respectful of the obligation of confidentiality and other core values. (Note: Much more liberal than India's Rule 36).
5. **Fees:** Must be fully disclosed to the client and be fair and reasonable. "Pactum de quota litis" (contingency fees where the lawyer takes a share of the result) is generally prohibited, though some exceptions apply in certain jurisdictions.
6. **Professional Indemnity Insurance:** Lawyers must be insured against liability for professional negligence to an extent which is reasonable having regard to the nature and extent of the risks.
7. **Relations with Other Lawyers:** A lawyer who is approached by a colleague from another Member State to handle a matter must not accept it if they are not competent to handle it.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A lawyer’s duty of confidentiality serves the interest of the administration of justice as well as that of the client. It is therefore entitled to special protection by the State." — *Article 2.3.1, CCBE Code of Conduct*.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Cross-Border Cooperation:** If a solo advocate in France needs to serve a document in Germany, they can contact a German colleague. The CCBE Code governs their professional relationship (e.g., promptness, communications "without prejudice").
- **Client Onboarding:** Must check for conflicts of interest across all previous matters.
- **Insurance:** The solo advocate must maintain professional indemnity insurance as a condition of their license.
- **Advertising:** They can have a website and use LinkedIn to acquire clients, provided they don't make false claims.
