## Key Facts
1. **Civil Law System:** Based on the *Burgerlijk Wetboek* (Civil Code). Known for being pragmatic and internationally oriented.
2. **Court Structure:** 11 District Courts (*Rechtbanken*), 4 Courts of Appeal (*Gerechtshoven*), and the Supreme Court (*Hoge Raad*).
3. **Cantonal Judges (*Kantonrechter*):** Handle cases up to €25,000, labor, and rent disputes. Lawyers are not mandatory here.
4. **Administrative Law:** Handled by the District Courts with appeals to specialized bodies like the Council of State (*Raad van State*).
5. **No Jury:** All cases are decided by professional judges.
6. **Mandatory Representation:** Required in the civil chambers of District Courts and in all appeals.
7. **NCC (Netherlands Commercial Court):** Offers proceedings in English for international commercial disputes, based in Amsterdam.
8. **Advocatenorde:** The *Nederlandse Orde van Advocaten* (NOvA) is the mandatory bar for all Dutch lawyers.
9. **Cassation Bar:** Only a specialized group of lawyers can litigate civil cases before the Supreme Court.
10. **Legal Aid:** The Netherlands has a highly developed "Council for Legal Aid" (*Raad voor Rechtsbijstand*) system.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Supreme Court... guards the legal unity and legal development in the Netherlands." — Hoge Raad website.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
The Cantonal sector allows solo advocates to represent clients with less procedural formality. For larger commercial cases, the NCC's English-language option is a significant advantage for advocates serving international clients.
