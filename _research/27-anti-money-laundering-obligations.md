## Currency Warning
The 2024 "Single Rulebook" package (AMLR and 6AMLD) was published in June 2024. The Regulation (AMLR) will be directly applicable from **July 10, 2027**. Until then, the 5th AML Directive (2018/843) remains the operative framework in most national laws.

## Key Facts
1. **Obliged Entities:** Lawyers and other independent legal professionals are "obliged entities" when they participate in financial or real estate transactions for their clients (Art. 3 AMLR).
2. **Customer Due Diligence (CDD):** Lawyers must verify the identity of their clients and the beneficial owners (BO) before establishing a business relationship.
3. **Beneficial Ownership (BO):** A BO is any natural person who ultimately owns or controls more than 25% of a legal entity.
4. **Reporting Suspicious Transactions (STR):** Lawyers must report suspicions of money laundering or terrorist financing to their national Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).
5. **Legal Professional Privilege (LPP):** Lawyers are exempt from reporting information obtained in the course of ascertaining the legal position of a client or defending them in judicial proceedings (Art. 70 AMLR).
6. **Prohibition of Tipping-Off:** Lawyers must not inform the client that a report has been filed or that an investigation is underway.
7. **AMLA (New Authority):** A new EU-level Authority for Anti-Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism (AMLA) will oversee the system from Frankfurt.
8. **Cash Limit:** A new EU-wide limit of €10,000 for large cash payments is introduced to curb illicit flows.
9. **High-Risk Third Countries:** Enhanced due diligence is required for transactions involving jurisdictions identified by the Commission as having strategic AML deficiencies.
10. **Sanctions:** Member States must provide for effective, proportionate, and dissuasive sanctions for breaches of AML obligations.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Legal professional privilege is a fundamental part of the right to a fair trial... However, it should not cover cases where the legal professional is taking part in money laundering or terrorist financing." — Recital 113, AMLR.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must implement internal AML procedures, even if they only handle a few real estate or corporate transactions. This includes maintaining a "Know Your Customer" (KYC) file for every client and being prepared to justify the "source of funds" in significant transactions. The 2024 rules tighten the definition of beneficial ownership, requiring more rigorous checks than in the past.
