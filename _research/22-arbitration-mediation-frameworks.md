Statute (Directive)

## Currency Warning
The Mediation Directive (2008/52/EC) applies to cross-border disputes. Domestic mediation is governed by national laws, though most Member States have aligned their domestic rules with the Directive.

## Key Facts
1. **Mediation Directive Purpose:** To facilitate access to alternative dispute resolution (ADR) and promote the amicable settlement of disputes by encouraging the use of mediation.
2. **Quality of Mediation:** Member States must encourage the training of mediators and the development of voluntary codes of conduct.
3. **Suspension of Limitation Periods:** Parties who choose mediation should not be prevented from subsequently starting judicial proceedings by the expiry of limitation periods during the mediation process (Art. 8).
4. **Enforceability of Agreements:** Content of written agreements resulting from mediation can be made enforceable (e.g., by a court or notary) in a similar way to a judgment (Art. 6).
5. **Confidentiality:** Mediators and those involved in the administration of the mediation process cannot be compelled to give evidence in civil/commercial judicial proceedings regarding information arising from the mediation (Art. 7).
6. **Arbitration (Exclusion):** Arbitration is explicitly excluded from the scope of most EU civil procedure regulations (like Brussels I-bis) and is primarily governed by the **1958 New York Convention** and national laws based on the UNCITRAL Model Law.
7. **ADR for Consumers:** Directive 2013/11/EU ensures consumers have access to high-quality ADR entities for all types of contractual disputes with traders.
8. **ODR Platform:** The EU provides an Online Dispute Resolution platform for disputes arising from online transactions (Regulation 524/2013).
9. **Mediator Selection:** The e-Justice portal provides a tool to find mediators across the EU.
10. **Judicial Encouragement:** Courts may invite parties to use mediation and can require them to attend an information session on the use of mediation (Art. 5).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Mediation can provide a cost-effective and quick extrajudicial resolution of disputes in civil and commercial matters through processes tailored to the needs of the parties." — Recital 6.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Advocates should always consider mediation as a first step, especially in cross-border cases where court costs and delays can be extreme. The "stay" on limitation periods under Art. 8 is a critical protection that allows the advocate to attempt settlement without losing the right to sue.
