# Statute: Contract Law Overview (Rome I) — European Union

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2008/593/oj

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation)

## Currency Warning
Regulation (EC) No 593/2008 (Rome I) applies to contracts concluded after December 17, 2009. It does not apply in Denmark.

## Key Facts
1. **Freedom of Choice:** The fundamental principle is that a contract is governed by the law chosen by the parties (Art. 3). The choice can be express or implied.
2. **Applicable Law in Absence of Choice:** If no law is chosen, Art. 4 provides default rules: Sale of goods (seller's residence), Provision of services (service provider's residence), Real estate (location of property).
3. **Characteristic Performance:** For contracts not listed in Art. 4(1), the law of the country where the party required to effect the "characteristic performance" has their residence applies.
4. **Consumer Protection:** Contracts between consumers and professionals are generally governed by the law of the consumer's residence, provided the professional directs activities to that country (Art. 6).
5. **Employment Contracts:** Generally governed by the law of the country where the employee habitually carries out their work (Art. 8).
6. **Mandatory Rules:** A choice of law cannot override the "overriding mandatory provisions" of the law of the forum (Art. 9).
7. **Public Policy:** Application of a foreign law may be refused if it is "manifestly incompatible" with the public policy (*ordre public*) of the forum (Art. 21).
8. **Universal Application:** The law specified by the Regulation shall be applied whether or not it is the law of a Member State (Art. 2).
9. **Form of Contract:** A contract is formally valid if it satisfies the requirements of the law which governs it in substance or the law of the country where it was concluded (Art. 11).
10. **Assignment of Claims:** The relationship between assignor and assignee is governed by the law that applies to the contract between them (Art. 14).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A contract shall be governed by the law chosen by the parties. The choice shall be made expressly or clearly demonstrated by the terms of the contract or the circumstances of the case." — Article 3(1).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
When drafting or reviewing a cross-border contract, the advocate MUST include a clear "Governing Law" clause. Without it, the default rules of Rome I might apply a foreign law (e.g., the law of a foreign service provider) that the advocate is not familiar with, creating significant legal risk.
