## Currency Warning
NIS2 implementation deadline was October 2024. While "legal services" are not a primary sector, the "supply chain" requirements are now being felt by law firms.

## Key Facts
1. **GDPR Mandate (Art 32):** Lawyers must implement "Technical and Organisational Measures" (TOMs) to protect client data. Failure is a GDPR breach.
2. **NIS2 Supply Chain:** Law firms serving "Essential" or "Important" entities (banks, energy, etc.) are now required by contract to meet NIS2-level cybersecurity standards.
3. **MFA and Encryption:** Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and end-to-end encryption for client files are considered "state of the art" and effectively mandatory for lawyers.
4. **Incident Reporting:** Firms may have to report cyber incidents to their client within 24-72 hours if the client is regulated under NIS2.
5. **Business Continuity:** Must have offline, immutable backups to ensure resilience against ransomware.
6. **Professional Ethics:** Many national bars (e.g., France, Germany) now include cybersecurity competence as part of the professional "duty of confidentiality."
7. **Ransomware:** Paying a ransom is highly discouraged and may violate AML or sanctions rules if the attacker is a listed entity.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The controller ... shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk." - Article 32, GDPR.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Secure Portals:** Avoid sending sensitive documents via unencrypted email; use secure client portals or encrypted attachments.
- **Hardware Security:** Full-disk encryption must be enabled on all laptops and mobile devices used for work.
- **Supply Chain Vetting:** When using a cloud provider, the advocate must ensure the provider meets high security standards and has a DPA in place.
