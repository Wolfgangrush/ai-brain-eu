## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation (EU) 2016/679)

## Currency Warning
While the GDPR is the primary text, it is supplemented by national "Data Protection Acts" in each Member State that fill in gaps (e.g., age of consent, processing in employment). Also, check for "Adequacy Decisions" for cross-border transfers.

## Key Facts
1. **Direct Applicability:** As a Regulation, it is directly applicable in all EU Member States without needing national implementation.
2. **Principles:** Processing must be lawful, fair, transparent, limited to purpose, data minimized, accurate, storage limited, and processed with integrity and confidentiality.
3. **Lawfulness:** Processing is only lawful if based on: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests.
4. **Data Subject Rights:** Includes rights to access, rectification, erasure ("right to be forgotten"), restriction, data portability, and to object.
5. **DPO Requirement:** Law firms (even solo advocates) must appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) if their "core activities" involve "regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale" or processing of "special categories of data" (e.g., criminal records) on a large scale. Solo practitioners usually do NOT meet the "large scale" threshold but must still comply with other GDPR obligations.
6. **Breach Notification:** Personal data breaches must be notified to the Supervisory Authority within 72 hours, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to individuals.
7. **Fines:** Administrative fines can reach up to €20 million or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The protection of natural persons in relation to the processing of personal data is a fundamental right." — *Recital 1, GDPR*.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Privacy Notice:** A solo advocate must provide a clear Privacy Notice to clients explaining how their data is handled.
- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA):** If the advocate uses cloud storage (e.g., Dropbox, Google Drive), they must ensure a DPA is in place with the provider.
- **Client Records:** Legal professional privilege applies, but the advocate must still respect the client's right to access their own data (subject to privilege exceptions).
- **Security:** Use of encryption for emails and client files is a practical necessity to meet the "security of processing" requirement (Art 32).
