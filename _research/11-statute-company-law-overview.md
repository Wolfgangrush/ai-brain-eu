## Authority Tier
Statute (Directive (EU) 2017/1132 - Codified)

## Currency Warning
Significantly amended by Directive (EU) 2019/1151 (Digital Tools) and Directive (EU) 2019/2121 (Cross-border conversions/divisions).

## Key Facts
1. **Formation:** Harmonized rules for the instrument of constitution and disclosure in national registers.
2. **Capital Maintenance:** Public limited liability companies (PLCs/AGs/SAs) must have a minimum capital of 25,000 EUR. Rules prevent unauthorized reduction of capital.
3. **Disclosure (BRIS):** All Member States must maintain an electronic register interconnected via the Business Registers Interconnection System (BRIS).
4. **Online Incorporation:** Since 2019, Member States must allow the fully online formation of limited liability companies and online registration of branches.
5. **Cross-border Mergers:** Detailed procedure for companies from different EU states to merge, including protection for employees and creditors.
6. **Conversions and Divisions:** Since 2023 (Mobility Directive), companies have a clear path to change their legal seat from one Member State to another without losing their legal personality.
7. **Disqualified Directors:** Registers must exchange info to prevent a person disqualified in one Member State from becoming a director in another.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"For the formation of a public limited liability company, the laws of the Member States shall require a minimum capital of not less than EUR 25 000." - Article 45, Directive 2017/1132.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Company Secretarial:** Use BRIS (via the e-Justice portal) to perform due diligence on foreign EU companies for clients.
- **Cross-border Advice:** The 2023 Mobility Directive creates a massive opportunity for advocates to help clients move their business seat to more favorable jurisdictions within the EU.
- **Online Filings:** Advocates can now handle the entire incorporation process for clients remotely using digital signatures and online registers.
