## Key Facts
1. **Regulatory Overload:** Compliance with AML, GDPR, AI Act, and national bar rules consumes an "excessive" amount of unbillable time.
2. **Cybersecurity Threats:** Law firms are prime targets for phishing and ransomware; solo practitioners often have the weakest defenses.
3. **Rising Aggression:** 45% of European lawyers report an increase in threats and harassment from opposing parties or disgruntled clients.
4. **Isolation:** Lack of peer support and "brainstorming" partners leads to higher rates of burnout and mental health issues.
5. **Technology Complexity:** Choosing and maintaining a secure, compliant "tech stack" without an IT department is a major stressor.
6. **Fee Pressure:** Competition from Alternative Legal Service Providers (ALSPs) and "LegalZoom-style" platforms for routine work.
7. **Late Payments:** Especially from legal aid authorities and corporate clients in certain jurisdictions (e.g., Italy, Greece).
8. **Court Inefficiencies:** Delays in judicial proceedings (some cases taking 5+ years) impact the lawyer's ability to deliver results and collect success fees.
9. **Work-Life Balance:** The "always-on" nature of email and court portals makes it difficult for solo practitioners to disconnect.
10. **Succession Planning:** Difficulty in valuing and selling a solo practice or finding a successor when retiring.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The primary challenge is no longer just the law itself, but the environment of threats, bureaucracy, and rapid technological change in which it must be practiced." — FBE (European Bars Federation) Survey.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate should prioritize tools that provide "security by default" and "compliance by design." Joining local small-firm networks can help mitigate the "isolation" pain point and provide a framework for sharing the cost of specialized tech.
