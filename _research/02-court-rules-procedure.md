## Authority Tier
Court Rule (Rules of Procedure)

## Currency Warning
The Rules of Procedure for both the Court of Justice and the General Court were significantly amended in **2024** (effective October 1, 2024) to implement the reform of the Statute of the CJEU. Older versions of the Rules do not contain the provisions for the General Court's new preliminary ruling jurisdiction.

## Key Facts
1. **Written Phase:** Direct actions begin with a written application (pleading). The defendant (usually an EU institution) has a set time (usually 2 months + 10 days for distance) to file a defense.
2. **Oral Phase:** Consists of a public hearing where lawyers present oral arguments and answer questions from Judges and the Advocate General.
3. **Electronic Filing (e-Curia):** The use of the **e-Curia** application for lodging and serving procedural documents is mandatory for representatives (lawyers) since 2018, unless technical issues occur.
4. **Representation:** Parties (other than Member States/Institutions) must be represented by a "lawyer authorised to practise before a court of a Member State." A solo advocate must be a member of a national bar.
5. **Language:** The "language of the case" is determined at the start. All documents must be translated into this language.
6. **Extension of Time Limits:** Time limits are strict. However, there is a fixed "extension on account of distance" of **10 days** added to all procedural time limits (except for the time limit for bringing an action in certain cases).
7. **Anonymity:** Parties can request that their identity or certain information be omitted from public documents (omission of data).
8. **Expedited Procedure:** Available in cases of extreme urgency, significantly shortening the written and oral phases.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The use of the e-Curia application ... shall be the only means of exchanging procedural documents between the parties’ representatives and the General Court." — *Article 72, Rules of Procedure of the General Court*.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Mandatory e-Curia:** A solo advocate *must* register for an e-Curia account before they can file any application.
- **Strict Deadlines:** Missing a deadline is usually fatal to the case. The 2-month limit for actions for annulment is interpreted strictly by the Court.
- **Bar Membership:** The advocate must have a valid certificate of membership from their national bar to be recognized by the CJEU Registrar.
