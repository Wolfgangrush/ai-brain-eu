# Mandatory Continuing Education (CPE/CPD) — European Union

## Source URL
https://www.ccbe.eu/fileadmin/speciality_distribution/public/documents/TRAINING/TR_Position_papers/EN_TR_2024_CPE_Recommendations.pdf

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule (CCBE Recommendations)

## Key Facts
1. **Common Requirement:** Almost all EU Member States require lawyers to engage in Mandatory Continuing Professional Development (CPD) or Continuing Professional Education (CPE).
2. **Average Hours:** Most Bars require between 10 and 20 hours (or "credits") per year (e.g., 15 hours in France, 15 credits in Germany).
3. **Content:** Courses typically cover new legislation, ethics, digital skills, and practice management.
4. **Self-Certification:** Lawyers must usually keep a record of their training and report it to their local Bar annually or every few years.
5. **AI Training:** Many Bars are now introducing mandatory or highly recommended modules on "AI Ethics and Literacy" in response to the AI Act.
6. **Cross-Border Recognition:** The CCBE promotes the "mutual recognition" of CPE credits earned in other Member States to facilitate mobility.
7. **Sanctions:** Failure to complete mandatory hours can lead to disciplinary action, including fines or temporary suspension of the right to practice.
8. **Accredited Providers:** Only courses from "accredited" providers (Bars, Universities, approved private vendors) count toward the requirement.
9. **Exemptions:** New lawyers (during their first year) or senior lawyers (over a certain age) may have different or reduced requirements.
10. **Digital Delivery:** Since the pandemic, over 80% of CPE is now delivered or available via webinars and online platforms.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Continuing training is a duty and a right of the lawyer. It is essential for maintaining the quality of legal services in an increasingly complex environment." — CCBE Training Guide.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate must budget both time (approx. 2 days/year) and money for CPE. They should look for courses that provide practical skills (e.g., "AI for Litigators") rather than just theoretical updates to maximize the return on their "unbillable" time.
