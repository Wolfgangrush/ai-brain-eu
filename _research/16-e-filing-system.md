## Currency Warning
The Digitalisation Regulation (EU) 2023/2844 is currently being implemented. Full mandatory digital communication for cross-border cases is expected to be phased in over the next 2-5 years.

## Key Facts
1. **European e-Justice Portal:** The central digital hub for legal information, finding a lawyer across borders, and accessing online forms for EU-wide procedures.
2. **e-CODEX:** The technical "gold standard" for cross-border electronic exchange of judicial data. It is a decentralized network of access points.
3. **European Small Claims Procedure:** Claims up to €5,000 can be filed electronically via the portal using standardized multilingual forms.
4. **European Payment Order (EPO):** A procedure for uncontested monetary claims that can be initiated via online forms on the portal.
5. **e-Curia:** The specific e-filing application for the Court of Justice and General Court. It allows for the exchange of judicial documents between parties and the Courts.
6. **eIDAS Regulation:** Sets the standard for electronic identification (eID) and electronic signatures (e-signatures) used in e-filing (Regulation EU No 910/2014).
7. **Find a Lawyer:** A tool on the e-Justice portal that allows citizens to find qualified legal professionals in any Member State.
8. **Interoperability:** The EU does not have a single "EU Court System" for domestic cases; instead, it focuses on making national e-filing systems (like LexNET or ERV) talk to each other via e-CODEX.
9. **Online Dispute Resolution (ODR):** A platform for consumers to resolve disputes with traders regarding online purchases.
10. **Mandatory Digitality:** Regulation 2023/2844 establishes that communication between authorities in different Member States in civil/commercial/criminal matters must be digital by default.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The European e-Justice Portal should be developed as a one-stop shop for citizens, businesses and legal practitioners." — Recital 4, Regulation 2023/2844.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates engaged in cross-border litigation (e.g., a German lawyer suing a French company for a small debt) should use the e-Justice Portal's forms to minimize translation costs and ensure procedural compliance. For cases before the CJEU, registration for an **e-Curia** account is mandatory for efficient document management.
