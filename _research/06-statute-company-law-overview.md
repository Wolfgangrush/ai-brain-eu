# Statute: Company Law Overview — European Union

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2017/1132/oj

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Directive)

## Currency Warning
Directive (EU) 2017/1132 codifies several earlier directives. It was significantly updated by Directive (EU) 2019/1151 (digital tools) and Directive (EU) 2019/2121 (cross-border operations).

## Key Facts
1. **Codification:** Directive 2017/1132 brings together rules on company formation, capital maintenance, disclosure, and mergers/divisions for public limited liability companies.
2. **Disclosure (BRIS):** Member States must ensure company information is available via the Business Registers Interconnection System (BRIS), accessible through the e-Justice Portal.
3. **Formation & Validity:** Sets rules on the minimum content of the instrument of constitution and the cases where a company can be declared null and void (which are very limited).
4. **Capital Maintenance:** For public limited companies, minimum capital is generally €25,000. Rules prevent the erosion of capital through improper dividends or share buy-backs.
5. **Digital Tools (2019/1151):** Mandates that companies can be registered online and that documents can be submitted digitally to business registers without needing to appear in person.
6. **Cross-Border Conversions (2019/2121):** Established a clear legal framework for a company to move its registered office from one Member State to another while maintaining its legal personality.
7. **Single-Member Companies:** Directive 2009/102/EC (now part of 2017/1132) allows for the creation of private limited liability companies with a single shareholder.
8. **Shareholders' Rights:** Directive 2007/36/EC (amended by 2017/828) regulates the exercise of certain rights of shareholders in listed companies (proxy voting, "say on pay").
9. **Corporate Governance:** EU law emphasizes transparency in executive remuneration and board diversity, though much of this is handled via "Comply or Explain" codes at the national level.
10. **Sustainable Due Diligence (CSDDD):** New obligations (2024) for large companies to identify and prevent adverse human rights and environmental impacts in their value chains.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The coordination of national provisions concerning the formation of public limited liability companies and the maintenance, increase or reduction of their capital is particularly important." — Recital 40.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate assisting a startup or SME with cross-border ambitions should leverage BRIS to verify the status of foreign counterparties. They should also be aware of the "Digital Tools" directive which significantly simplifies the process of setting up a branch or subsidiary in another Member State online.
