# Statute: Non-Contractual Obligations (Rome II) — European Union

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2007/864/oj

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation)

## Currency Warning
Regulation (EC) No 864/2007 (Rome II) applies to events giving rise to damage occurring after January 11, 2009.

## Key Facts
1. **General Rule:** The law applicable to a non-contractual obligation arising out of a tort/delict shall be the law of the country in which the **damage occurs** (Art. 4(1)), regardless of where the event giving rise to the damage occurred.
2. **Common Habitual Residence:** If the person claimed to be liable and the person sustaining damage both have their habitual residence in the same country at the time the damage occurs, the law of that country shall apply (Art. 4(2)).
3. **Manifestly Closer Connection:** If the tort is manifestly more closely connected with another country (e.g., a pre-existing relationship like a contract), the law of that other country applies (Art. 4(3)).
4. **Product Liability:** Special rules for damage caused by a product (Art. 5), favoring the law of the victim's residence if the product was marketed there.
5. **Unfair Competition:** The law of the country where competitive relations or the collective interests of consumers are, or are likely to be, affected (Art. 6).
6. **Environmental Damage:** The victim can choose to base their claim on the law of the country where the damage occurred or the law of the country in which the event giving rise to the damage occurred (Art. 7).
7. **Infringement of Intellectual Property:** For EU-wide rights, the law of the country for which protection is claimed (Art. 8).
8. **Freedom of Choice:** Parties can agree to submit non-contractual obligations to a law of their choice, but only after the event giving rise to the damage occurred (Art. 14).
9. **Rules of Safety and Conduct:** Regardless of the applicable law, account must be taken of the rules of safety and conduct in force at the place and time of the event (Art. 17).
10. **Direct Action Against Insurer:** The victim may bring a claim directly against the insurer of the person liable if the law applicable to the non-contractual obligation or the law applicable to the insurance contract so provides (Art. 18).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The law applicable to a non-contractual obligation... shall be the law of the country in which the damage occurs irrespective of the country in which the event giving rise to the damage occurred." — Article 4(1).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
In a cross-border accident (e.g., a Spanish tourist injured in a car crash in Italy caused by a French driver), the advocate must look to Italian law for the substance of the claim (damages, liability), as that is where the damage occurred, unless both parties reside in the same country.
