# Court Rules: Civil — European Union (Brussels I-bis)

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2012/1215/oj

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation)

## Currency Warning
Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 (Brussels I-bis) applies to proceedings instituted on or after January 10, 2015. It replaced Regulation (EC) No 44/2001.

## Key Facts
1. **Scope:** Applies to "civil and commercial matters" regardless of the nature of the court or tribunal. It excludes revenue, customs, administrative matters, and specific areas like family law, bankruptcy, and arbitration.
2. **General Jurisdiction:** Persons domiciled in a Member State shall be sued in the courts of that Member State (Art. 4).
3. **Special Jurisdiction:** In contract matters, a person may be sued in the courts for the place of performance of the obligation (Art. 7(1)). In tort, in the courts for the place where the harmful event occurred (Art. 7(2)).
4. **Exclusive Jurisdiction:** Certain matters (e.g., real estate, company validity, IP registration) have exclusive jurisdiction in specific Member States regardless of domicile (Art. 24).
5. **Prorogation of Jurisdiction:** Parties can agree that a specific Member State's court has jurisdiction (Art. 25).
6. **Abolition of Exequatur:** A judgment given in one Member State is recognized and enforceable in other Member States without any special procedure or declaration of enforceability (Art. 36 & 39).
7. **Certificate for Enforcement:** To enforce a judgment, the applicant must provide a copy of the judgment and a certificate issued by the court of origin (Annex I).
8. **Lis Pendens:** If parallel proceedings are brought in different Member States, any court other than the court first seized must stay its proceedings (Art. 29).
9. **Grounds for Refusal:** Recognition/enforcement can be refused if it is manifestly contrary to public policy, or if there was a lack of proper service in default judgments (Art. 45).
10. **Provisional Measures:** Application may be made to the courts of a Member State for provisional or protective measures available under the law of that State, even if another State has jurisdiction over the substance (Art. 35).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A judgment given in a Member State which is enforceable in that Member State shall be enforceable in the other Member States without any declaration of enforceability being required." — Article 39.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
When representing a client in a cross-border dispute (e.g., a French client suing a German supplier), the advocate must first determine jurisdiction under Brussels I-bis. If a judgment is obtained in France, the advocate can proceed directly to enforcement in Germany using the Annex I certificate, bypassing the old, costly 'exequatur' process.
