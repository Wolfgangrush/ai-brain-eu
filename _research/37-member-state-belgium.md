## Key Facts
1. **Civil Law System:** Influenced by the French Napoleonic Code; highly formalized and multilingual (Dutch, French, German).
2. **Judicial Hierarchy:** Justice of the Peace (minor), Court of First Instance, Court of Appeal, and Court of Cassation (Supreme).
3. **Business Court (*Ondernemingsrechtbank*):** Handles disputes between companies, using a mix of professional and lay business judges.
4. **Labor Courts:** Specialized hierarchy for employment and social security matters.
5. **Constitutional Court:** *Grondwettelijk Hof* rules on conflicts between federal and regional laws and constitutional violations.
6. **Council of State:** The supreme administrative court and advisor to the government.
7. **Assize Court:** The only court with a jury, reserved for the most serious crimes.
8. **Languages:** Proceedings must be conducted in the official language of the judicial area (Dutch in Flanders, French in Wallonia, both in Brussels).
9. **Cassation Bar:** Only a very limited number of "Advocaten bij het Hof van Cassatie" can practice civil cassation.
10. **Diplomas:** Requires a 5-year Master of Laws followed by a 3-year traineeship (*stage*) at the bar.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Court of Cassation ensures the legality of judicial decisions and the unity of case-law." — Justitie.belgium.be.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Language is a critical barrier; a solo advocate in Brussels must be fluent in both Dutch and French or specialize in one section of the court. The specialized Business Courts provide a relatively fast track for commercial disputes.
