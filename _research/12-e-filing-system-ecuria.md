## Authority Tier
Court Rule / Administrative Guide (CJEU Decisions)

## Currency Warning
Mandatory for all filings in the General Court since 2018. New 2024 Decisions (effective Sep 2024) update the "Conditions of Use."

## Key Facts
1. **Mandatory Use:** Lawyers MUST use e-Curia to lodge and receive documents in General Court cases. In the Court of Justice, it is the preferred method.
2. **Account Requirement:** To open a "Representative" account, an advocate must submit a paper application signed and accompanied by a Bar Membership Certificate.
3. **Electronic Signature:** No handwritten signature is needed on documents uploaded to e-Curia; the login credentials serve as the legal signature.
4. **Receipt of Service:** A document is deemed served when the lawyer "accesses" it in e-Curia, or 7 days after the notification email, whichever is earlier.
5. **Technical Limits:** PDF format only, max 30 MB per file. If a document is larger, it must be split and numbered.
6. **Assistants:** A lawyer can create sub-accounts for assistants to prepare filings, but the lawyer must perform the final "lodging" action.
7. **Proof of Lodging:** The system generates an automatic "Lodging Receipt" with a timestamp (Luxembourg time).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The lodging of a procedural document by means of e-Curia ... shall be characterized as an electronic signature for the purposes of the Rules of Procedure." - Decision of the Court of Justice (2024).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Setup Time:** A solo advocate should apply for an e-Curia account *before* they actually have a case, as validation can take several days.
- **No Postage Costs:** e-Curia eliminates the need for registered mail to Luxembourg.
- **7-Day Buffer:** The "deemed receipt" rule after 7 days provides a small buffer for lawyers who may miss a notification email while on leave.
