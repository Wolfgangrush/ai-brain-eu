## Key Facts
1. **Civil Law System:** Based on the 1964 Civil Code and the 1997 Constitution.
2. **Common Courts Hierarchy:** Sąd Rejonowy (District), Sąd Okręgowy (Regional), Sąd Apelacyjny (Appellate), and Sąd Najwyższy (Supreme Court).
3. **Administrative Justice:** Two-tier system: *Wojewódzki Sąd Administracyjny* (WSA) and *Naczelny Sąd Administracyjny* (NSA).
4. **Constitutional Tribunal:** *Trybunał Konstytucyjny* rules on the constitutionality of statutes.
5. **Legal Professions:** Divided between Advocates (*Adwokaci*) and Legal Advisors (*Radcowie Prawni*), though their powers are now largely identical.
6. **E-Court (*e-Sąd*):** A specialized branch of the Lublin-West District Court that handles uncontested monetary claims across Poland electronically.
7. **KRS (National Court Register):** Centralized electronic register for companies and associations.
8. **KW (Land and Mortgage Register):** Publicly accessible electronic register for real estate.
9. **Code of Civil Procedure (KPC):** The primary procedural text, which has undergone significant digitalization reforms recently.
10. **Legal Aid:** Provided by the state through local bar associations to persons in a difficult financial situation.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Justice in the Republic of Poland is administered by the Supreme Court, common courts, administrative courts and military courts." — Article 175, Constitution of Poland.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
The **e-Sąd** is a powerful tool for solo advocates to handle debt collection cases efficiently for clients. Being proficient in the electronic KRS and KW systems is essential for corporate and real estate practice.
