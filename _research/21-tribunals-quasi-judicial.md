## Key Facts
1. **European Ombudsman:** An independent and impartial body that holds the EU institutions to account. Investigates complaints about "maladministration" (e.g., unfairness, discrimination, abuse of power, lack of transparency).
2. **Right to Complain:** Any EU citizen or resident, and any business or association in the EU, can lodge a complaint with the Ombudsman.
3. **Non-Judicial Remedy:** The Ombudsman cannot force an institution to act, but its recommendations carry significant political and public weight.
4. **EUIPO Boards of Appeal:** The quasi-judicial body within the European Union Intellectual Property Office. It hears appeals against decisions on EU trademarks and registered community designs.
5. **Appeal to General Court:** Decisions of the EUIPO Boards of Appeal can be further appealed to the General Court of the EU on points of law.
6. **CPVO Board of Appeal:** Similar body for the Community Plant Variety Office, dealing with plant variety rights.
7. **ECHA Board of Appeal:** Handles appeals against certain decisions of the European Chemicals Agency (REACH).
8. **ACER Board of Appeal:** Deals with disputes in the energy sector (Agency for the Cooperation of Energy Regulators).
9. **ESMA / EBA / EIOPA Boards of Appeal:** Supervisory bodies for the financial sector (Securities, Banking, and Insurance) have internal appeal mechanisms for regulated entities.
10. **European Data Protection Supervisor (EDPS):** The independent authority responsible for ensuring that EU institutions respect the right to privacy and data protection.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Any citizen of the Union... shall have the right to complain to the European Ombudsman about instances of maladministration in the activities of the Union institutions." — Article 228 TFEU.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
If a solo advocate's client is struggling with "bureaucratic wall" from the European Commission (e.g., failure to provide documents under access-to-info rules), the Ombudsman is a free and effective alternative to a costly lawsuit. For IP specialists, the EUIPO Boards of Appeal are a frequent and necessary step before reaching the General Court.
