# 05-statute-ai-act.md

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689

## Last Verified
2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)

## Currency Warning
The AI Act entered into force on August 1, 2024. However, most provisions (including obligations for high-risk systems) apply from **August 2, 2026**. Prohibitions on "unacceptable risk" AI apply from February 2, 2025.

## Key Facts
1. **Risk-Based Approach:** Categorizes AI into: Unacceptable Risk (prohibited), High Risk (strictly regulated), Limited Risk (transparency obligations), and Minimal Risk (no regulation).
2. **Prohibited AI:** Includes biometric categorization (in certain contexts), emotion recognition in workplaces/schools, social scoring, and AI used to manipulate human behavior.
3. **High-Risk AI (Annex III):** Crucially for lawyers, AI used in the **administration of justice and democratic processes** (e.g., tools to assist a judicial authority in researching/interpreting facts and the law) is classified as **High-Risk**.
4. **Obligations for High-Risk AI:** Providers must ensure data quality, documentation, logging, transparency, human oversight, and robustness/accuracy.
5. **General Purpose AI (GPAI):** Specific rules for large models (like GPT-4), including transparency and copyright compliance.
6. **AI Definition:** Aligned with OECD definition: "a machine-based system that, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers, from the input it receives, how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments."
7. **Annex III, Point 8:** Specifically targets AI systems intended to be used by a judicial authority or on their behalf to assist in researching and interpreting facts and the law.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"AI systems intended to be used by a judicial authority or on their behalf to assist a judicial authority in researching and interpreting facts and the law and in applying the law to a concrete set of facts ... shall be classified as high-risk." — *Annex III, Point 8(a), AI Act*.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Tool Selection:** A solo advocate using AI for drafting or research must verify if the tool is "High-Risk" and if the provider complies with the AI Act.
- **Human Oversight:** The advocate *must* remain the "human in the loop." Relying solely on AI output for a court filing could violate professional ethics and AI Act oversight principles.
- **Compliance Agent Warning:** Software tools for advocates (like the one being built) must fire explicit warnings when users attempt to use AI for tasks that overlap with the "high-risk" categories in the administration of justice.
- **Transparency:** If the advocate uses AI to generate content for clients, they should disclose this where appropriate.
