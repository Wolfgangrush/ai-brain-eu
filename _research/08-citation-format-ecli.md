## Currency Warning
ECLI is now the standard for all CJEU decisions and is being progressively adopted by national courts (France, Spain, Netherlands, Germany, etc.). Always check if a national decision has an ECLI before using older citation styles.

## Key Facts
1. **Structure:** ECLI:[Country Code]:[Court Code]:[Year]:[Unique ID]
2. **CJEU Court Codes:**
   - C: Court of Justice (ECJ)
   - T: General Court
   - F: Civil Service Tribunal (pre-2016)
3. **Example Breakdown:** ECLI:EU:C:2014:2133
   - ECLI: Prefix
   - EU: European Union (for CJEU)
   - C: Court of Justice
   - 2014: Year of decision
   - 2133: Unique serial number
4. **Metadata:** ECLI citations are often linked to metadata like CELEX numbers (for the legislative database) and summaries in the Official Journal.
5. **Purpose:** To solve the problem of multiple citation formats for the same case (e.g., by different law reports) and to make case law machine-readable.
6. **Implementation:** Not all EU countries use ECLI for all courts yet. The e-Justice portal provides a search engine that works across participating countries.
7. **OSCOLA / Bluebook:** Modern legal citation styles (like OSCOLA) now prioritize ECLI for EU cases.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The ECLI is a uniform identifier that has the same structural format for all Member States and EU courts." - European e-Justice Portal.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Search Precision:** Using an ECLI in the Curia or EUR-Lex search box will take the advocate directly to the judgment.
- **Drafting Pleadings:** Advocates should cite CJEU cases using their ECLI to ensure the Court can easily identify the precedent.
- **Automation:** Software tools can use ECLI to automatically link citations to the full text of the judgment.
