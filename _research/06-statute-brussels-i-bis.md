## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012)

## Currency Warning
Applies to legal proceedings instituted on or after 10 January 2015. Note that for UK-related matters, this regulation no longer applies (post-Brexit), and the Lugano Convention or Hague Convention may apply instead.

## Key Facts
1. **Scope:** Civil and commercial matters. Excludes: revenue, customs, administrative matters, status of natural persons, succession, bankruptcy, and arbitration.
2. **General Rule (Art 4):** Persons domiciled in a Member State shall be sued in the courts of that Member State.
3. **Special Jurisdiction (Art 7):** In contract cases, the court of the place of performance of the obligation. In tort cases, the court of the place where the harmful event occurred.
4. **Consumer/Employment Protection:** Specific rules allow consumers or employees to sue in their own domicile, overriding general rules.
5. **Prorogation of Jurisdiction (Art 25):** Parties can agree (in writing) that a specific Member State court has jurisdiction (Choice of Court).
6. **Recognition and Enforcement:** A judgment given in a Member State shall be recognized in the other Member States without any special procedure being required.
7. **Abolition of Exequatur:** No need for a "declaration of enforceability" to enforce a judgment from another EU state. You directly use the "Certificate concerning a judgment in civil and commercial matters" (Annex I).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A judgment given in a Member State which is enforceable in that Member State shall be enforceable in the other Member States without any declaration of enforceability being required." — *Article 39, Brussels I-bis*.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Cross-Border Debt Recovery:** A solo advocate representing a small business can enforce a local judgment against a debtor in another EU state simply by obtaining a certificate from the local court.
- **Drafting Contracts:** Always include a jurisdiction clause that complies with Article 25 to avoid disputes over where a lawsuit should happen.
- **Defensive Strategy:** If a client is sued in another Member State, the first step is to check if that court has jurisdiction under Brussels I-bis.
