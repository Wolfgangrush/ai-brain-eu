## Currency Warning
The jurisdiction of the General Court to hear appeals from these bodies is subject to the "filtering" mechanism (Art 58a of the Statute), meaning the Court of Justice only hears further appeals if they raise an issue significant to the unity of EU law.

## Key Facts
1. **EUIPO (Intellectual Property):** Based in Alicante. Handles EU Trade Marks and Designs. Decisions of examiners can be appealed to the **Boards of Appeal** (quasi-judicial).
2. **CPVO (Plant Varieties):** Based in Angers. Protects new plant varieties. Has its own **Board of Appeal**.
3. **ECHA (Chemicals):** Based in Helsinki. Manages REACH compliance. Has a **Board of Appeal** for technical decisions on chemical registration.
4. **SRB (Banking Union):** Based in Brussels. Handles bank resolutions. The **Appeal Panel** reviews decisions on resolution plans and fines.
5. **ACER (Energy):** Based in Ljubljana. Regulates cross-border energy markets. Has a **Board of Appeal**.
6. **EASA (Aviation):** Based in Cologne. Safety regulations. Has a **Board of Appeal**.
7. **Judicial Review:** Decisions of these Boards of Appeal are not final; they can be challenged in the **General Court of the EU** via an action for annulment (Art 263 TFEU).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Boards of Appeal shall be responsible for deciding on appeals against decisions of the Office." - Article 165, Regulation (EU) 2017/1001 (EU Trade Mark Regulation).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Administrative First Step:** In IP or regulatory matters, an advocate *must* usually exhaust the internal Board of Appeal remedy before they can file a case in the General Court.
- **Technical Expertise:** These tribunals handle highly technical data. Solo advocates often need to work with technical experts (e.g., patent attorneys, scientists) to build the case.
- **Luxembourg Connection:** If the Board of Appeal rejects the case, the advocate must be prepared to litigate in Luxembourg (General Court).
