## Key Facts
1. **Austria:** Civil law system; very high digitalization with the **ERV** e-filing system. High court is the *Oberster Gerichtshof*.
2. **Sweden:** Civil law with strong ombudsman tradition. Does not have a traditional "Constitutional Court" but judges can set aside unconstitutional laws.
3. **Denmark:** Holds an "opt-out" from EU Justice and Home Affairs (doesn't apply Brussels I-bis or Rome I directly, but has similar agreements).
4. **Ireland:** The only **Common Law** system remaining in the EU. High court is the Superior Courts (High Court, Court of Appeal, Supreme Court).
5. **Greece:** Civil law based on the German/French models. Noted for significant judicial delays.
6. **Portugal:** Civil law; high degree of centralization in Lisbon for apex courts.
7. **Czech Republic:** Civil law; strong emphasis on the protection of fundamental rights via the *Ústavní soud* (Constitutional Court).
8. **Hungary:** Civil law; legal system has undergone significant recent changes regarding the structure of the judiciary.
9. **Romania:** Civil law; specialized "Anti-Corruption Directorate" (DNA) is a key feature of the criminal justice system.
10. **Bulgaria:** Civil law; judicial system consists of three tiers of courts and a separate administrative branch.
11. **Finland:** Civil law; known for high transparency and a very efficient, digital-first judiciary.
12. **Slovakia:** Civil law; similar structure to the Czech Republic with a strong Constitutional Court.
13. **Croatia:** Civil law; newest EU member (2013), has aligned its judiciary closely with EU standards.
14. **Lithuania:** Civil law; has a highly regarded electronic case management system.
15. **Slovenia:** Civil law; small, efficient system with a three-tier court structure.
16. **Latvia:** Civil law; three-tier court system with a separate Constitutional Court.
17. **Estonia:** The "most digital" legal system; almost all proceedings can be handled via the **e-File** system.
18. **Cyprus:** Common law background (British colonial influence) but now a mixed system heavily influenced by EU civil law.
19. **Luxembourg:** Civil law; host to the CJEU. Its national system is closely modeled on the French system.
20. **Malta:** Mixed system (Common law and Civil law influence) with English as an official legal language alongside Maltese.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The diversity of legal traditions in Europe is a strength, but the 'Area of Freedom, Security and Justice' ensures they all speak the same language of fundamental rights." — European Commission.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
When dealing with these jurisdictions, the advocate should start at the **e-Justice Portal**, which provides the primary link to each country's "Find a Lawyer" and "Find a Case" tools. In Estonia and Austria, e-filing is the default and often mandatory for all practitioners.
