# Data Localization & Free Flow of Data — European Union

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2018/1807/oj

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation)

## Currency Warning
Regulation (EU) 2018/1807 covers **non-personal** data. Personal data is covered by the GDPR (see File 04).

## Key Facts
1. **Free Flow of Non-Personal Data:** Prohibits Member States from imposing data localization requirements (i.e., requiring data to be stored or processed in a specific territory) unless justified on grounds of public security (Art. 4).
2. **"Non-Personal" Data:** Data other than personal data as defined in the GDPR (e.g., aggregate datasets used for AI training, maintenance logs, meteorological data).
3. **Mixed Datasets:** In the case of datasets containing both personal and non-personal data, the GDPR applies to the personal data part, and the Free Flow Regulation applies to the non-personal part (Art. 2).
4. **Data Availability for Authorities:** Ensuring that the removal of localization requirements does not affect the powers of competent authorities to access data for regulatory or supervisory purposes.
5. **Data Porting for Professional Users:** Encourages the development of codes of conduct to facilitate switching service providers (e.g., cloud providers) and the porting of data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format (Art. 6).
6. **Single Point of Contact:** Each Member State must designate a single point of contact to liaise with other States and the Commission on the application of the Regulation.
7. **Public Security Exception:** Any new localization requirement must be notified to the Commission and is subject to strict scrutiny.
8. **Interoperability:** The Regulation aims to prevent "vendor lock-in" in the cloud market.
9. **Relationship with GDPR:** The Regulation explicitly states that it does not prejudice the application of the GDPR.
10. **SMEs and Solo Practitioners:** By removing localization barriers, the Regulation makes it easier and cheaper for small firms to use cloud services based anywhere in the EU.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Data localisation requirements... constitute a clear barrier to the free provision of services and to the free movement of data within the Union." — Recital 10.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate can host their non-personal business data (e.g., accounting records, library of templates) on a server in any EU Member State without fear of national laws requiring the data to be "kept in the country." For "mixed" client files, the GDPR's stricter rules on personal data location (within the EEA) effectively dictate the choice of provider.
