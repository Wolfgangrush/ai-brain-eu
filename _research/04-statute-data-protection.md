# Statute: Data Protection (GDPR) — European Union

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation)

## Currency Warning
Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) remains the primary framework. However, it must be read alongside national "Data Protection Acts" of each Member State, which may exercise "opening clauses" for specific areas (e.g., employment data, age of consent for children).

## Key Facts
1. **Direct Effect:** As a Regulation, the GDPR is directly applicable in all EU Member States without the need for national implementing legislation.
2. **Territorial Scope:** Applies to controllers/processors in the EU, and those outside the EU offering goods/services to or monitoring behavior of individuals in the EU (Art. 3).
3. **Core Principles:** Lawfulness, fairness, transparency; purpose limitation; data minimization; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity and confidentiality; and accountability (Art. 5).
4. **Lawful Bases for Processing:** Consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests (Art. 6).
5. **Data Subject Rights:** Right to access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure/to be forgotten (Art. 17), restriction (Art. 18), data portability (Art. 20), and right to object (Art. 21).
6. **Breach Notification:** Mandatory notification to the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach (Art. 33).
7. **Data Protection Officer (DPO):** Mandatory for public authorities and certain large-scale monitoring or processing of sensitive data (Art. 37).
8. **Sanctions:** Administrative fines up to €20 million or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher (Art. 83).
9. **Supervisory Authorities:** Each Member State has an independent data protection authority (DPA) to enforce the GDPR.
10. **Cross-Border Transfers:** Transfers outside the EEA require an adequacy decision, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or other safeguards (Chapter V).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The protection of natural persons in relation to the processing of personal data is a fundamental right." — Recital 1.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must ensure their own practice is GDPR-compliant (acting as a data controller). This includes maintaining a Record of Processing Activities (ROPA), providing privacy notices to clients, and ensuring secure storage of sensitive legal files. Lawyers often process "special categories of data" (Art. 9) which requires higher security and a specific lawful basis (often "establishment, exercise or defense of legal claims").
