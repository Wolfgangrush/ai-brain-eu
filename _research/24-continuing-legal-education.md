## Currency Warning
While CCBE provides a framework, the *mandatory* nature and number of hours required for Continuing Professional Development (CPD) vary by national bar.

## Key Facts
1. **Duty to Maintain Competence:** CCBE Code (Art 3.1.3) requires lawyers to be competent. Ongoing training is the primary means to fulfill this duty.
2. **Mutual Recognition:** Under a 2017 CCBE Memorandum of Understanding, many national bars recognize CPD hours completed in other EU states.
3. **EU Law Focus:** The European Commission and CCBE strongly encourage training in EU law, specifically the Charter of Fundamental Rights and CJEU procedure.
4. **European Training Platform (ETP):** A central hub on the e-Justice portal that provides access to training courses and materials for legal professionals across the EU.
5. **Common Subjects:** Ethics (Deontology), Digital Skills/AI, and specialized substantive law.
6. **Hours/Credits:** Most bars require 10-20 hours of CPD per year.
7. **Sanctions:** Non-compliance can lead to disciplinary action or, in some states, suspension of the license to practice.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A lawyer shall not handle a matter which he or she knows or ought to know he or she is not competent to handle." - Article 3.1.3, CCBE Code of Conduct.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Log Your Hours:** Keep a detailed record of all seminars, webinars, and self-study. Many bars require an annual "CPD Return."
- **Use Free Resources:** The e-Justice portal offers free high-quality training on EU regulations (GDPR, Brussels I-bis, etc.).
- **Cross-Border Recognition:** If attending a conference in another EU state, get a certificate of attendance to claim credits with your home bar.
