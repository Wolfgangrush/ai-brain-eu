## Currency Warning
Member States are increasingly allowing corporate forms for law firms, but rules on "non-lawyer ownership" (ABS) remain highly restrictive in most of the EU (except Denmark/Spain/Netherlands to a limited extent).

## Key Facts
1. **Sole Proprietorship:** The default for most solo advocates. Unlimited personal liability for all debts and professional negligence.
2. **Partnership (General):** Joint and several liability. Common for small groupings.
3. **Limited Liability Forms:**
   - **LLP-equivalents:** e.g., PartG mbB in Germany, SEL in France. Limits liability for professional errors to the firm's assets (provided insurance is in place).
   - **Professional Corporations:** e.g., SLP in Spain, STP in Italy. Separate legal personality.
4. **Right to Joint Practice (Art 11):** If a host Member State allows its own lawyers to practice in a grouping, it must allow EU lawyers from other states to do the same (e.g., a French lawyer can join a German PartG).
5. **Ownership Restrictions:** Most EU states require that the majority of shares and voting rights be held by qualified lawyers to ensure professional independence.
6. **Multi-Disciplinary Practices (MDPs):** Partnerships with non-lawyers (e.g., accountants) are permitted in some states (Germany, Netherlands) but prohibited in others (France).
7. **Registered European Lawyer (REL):** A lawyer can practice under their home-country title (e.g., "Advokat", "Avocat") in another EU state for 3 years, after which they can gain full admission to the host bar.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"One or more lawyers ... may practise in the form of a branch or agency of their grouping in the host Member State." - Article 11, Directive 98/5/EC.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Limiting Liability:** Solo advocates should consider "incorporating" as a professional society (where national law allows) to protect personal assets.
- **Cross-Border Scaling:** A solo advocate in one country can form a partnership with a solo advocate in another, creating a "virtual" cross-border firm under Article 11.
- **Insurance:** Regardless of structure, professional indemnity insurance is mandatory and must be verified by the bar.
