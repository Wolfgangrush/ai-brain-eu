## Key Facts
1. **Consumer Rights (B2C):**
   - **14-day Withdrawal:** Mandatory "cooling-off" period for distance/online contracts.
   - **Pre-contractual Transparency:** Comprehensive list of info (price, characteristics, identity) must be provided before the sale.
   - **2-Year Guarantee:** Minimum 2-year legal guarantee for conformity of goods across the EU.
2. **Unfair Terms:** Standard contract terms are non-binding on consumers if they cause a "significant imbalance" in rights (e.g., hidden fees, one-sided termination).
3. **Late Payments (B2B/B2G):**
   - **Statutory Interest:** At least 8% above ECB base rate.
   - **Fixed Compensation:** Minimum 40 EUR for recovery costs per unpaid invoice.
   - **Deadlines:** 30 days for public authorities, 60 days for B2B (unless otherwise agreed and not grossly unfair).
4. **Digital Content:** Special rules (Directive 2019/770) for apps and streaming, including cases where consumers provide "data" instead of "money."
5. **Electronic Signatures (eIDAS):** Regulation 910/2014 ensures that a "Qualified Electronic Signature" (QES) has the same legal effect as a handwritten one across the EU.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Member States shall ensure that, in commercial transactions between undertakings, the creditor is entitled to interest for late payment without the necessity of a reminder." - Article 3(1), Directive 2011/7/EU.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Reviewing Terms:** When drafting for a small business, ensure their T&Cs don't contain "unfair terms" that could be struck down.
- **Debt Collection:** Use the 40 EUR fixed compensation and statutory interest as leverage in demand letters for unpaid fees.
- **Online Practice:** If the advocate offers online consultations, they must comply with the Consumer Rights Directive info requirements on their website.
