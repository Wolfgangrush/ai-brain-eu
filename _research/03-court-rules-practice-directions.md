## Authority Tier
Court Rule (Practice Directions / Recommendations)

## Currency Warning
Updated in 2024 to account for the new "pilot case" procedure and expanded use of videoconferencing.

## Key Facts
1. **Formatting Requirements:** Applications must follow strict formatting (page limits, paragraph numbering, font size). The General Court has a 30-50 page limit for most applications.
2. **Structure of Application:** Must contain: name/address of applicant, name of party against whom action is brought, subject matter, forms of order sought, and a brief summary of the pleas in law.
3. **Mandatory Annexes:** Must include proof of the lawyer’s authority to act (Power of Attorney) and proof of the lawyer’s bar membership.
4. **Summary of Pleas:** A concise summary (usually 2 pages) of the application must be provided for publication in the Official Journal.
5. **Videoconferencing:** Practice directions now provide a framework for lawyers to participate in oral hearings via videoconference if they cannot travel to Luxembourg due to "health, security or other serious reasons."
6. **Preliminary Ruling Recommendations:** Provides a template for national judges on how to draft a request for a preliminary ruling (clear statement of facts, relevant national law, and the necessity of the EU law interpretation).
7. **Pilot Cases:** The General Court can stay proceedings if a "pilot case" is pending that addresses the same legal issue, to ensure judicial economy.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The application shall be accompanied by ... a certificate that the lawyer is authorised to practise before a court of a Member State." — *Article 51(2), Rules of Procedure of the Court of Justice*.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- **Page Limits:** A solo advocate must be concise. If an application exceeds page limits without prior authorization, the Registrar may reject it or require it to be shortened.
- **Checklist Approach:** Advocates should use the "Aide-mémoire" (checklist) provided by the Registrar to ensure no mandatory annexes are missing.
- **e-Curia Proficiency:** The advocate is responsible for ensuring documents are uploaded correctly in PDF format and within file size limits.
