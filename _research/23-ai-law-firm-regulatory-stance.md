# AI Regulatory Stance (EU AI Act) — European Union

## Source URL
https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Regulation)

## Currency Warning
Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 was published July 12, 2024. Most provisions apply from August 2, 2026. Prohibitions apply from February 2, 2025. Rules for General-Purpose AI (GPAI) apply from August 2, 2025.

## Key Facts
1. **Risk-Based Approach:** The Act categorizes AI systems into four levels: Unacceptable Risk, High Risk, Limited Risk, and Minimal Risk.
2. **Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited):** Systems like social scoring, biometric identification in public spaces (with exceptions), and manipulative AI are banned (Art. 5).
3. **High-Risk AI Systems:** Systems used in critical sectors (justice, employment, education, critical infrastructure). These face strict obligations including risk management, data governance, and human oversight (Title III).
4. **AI in Justice:** AI systems intended to be used by a judicial authority or on their behalf to assist a judicial authority in researching and interpreting facts and the law and in applying the law to a concrete set of facts are classified as **High-Risk** (Annex III, point 8).
5. **General-Purpose AI (GPAI):** Specific rules for models like LLMs, including transparency requirements and systemic risk assessments for very large models (Title V).
6. **Transparency Obligations:** Users must be informed when interacting with AI (e.g., chatbots) or when AI generates deepfakes/content (Art. 50).
7. **AI Office:** A central European AI Office is established within the Commission to oversee the Act's implementation and enforcement.
8. **Sanctions:** Fines up to €35 million or 7% of global turnover for prohibited practices; lower tiers for other violations.
9. **AI Regulatory Sandboxes:** Member States must establish at least one AI regulatory sandbox at the national level to foster innovation (Art. 57).
10. **Exemptions:** AI systems used exclusively for military, defense, or national security purposes are excluded from the scope.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"AI systems intended to be used by a judicial authority... to assist a judicial authority in researching and interpreting facts and the law and in applying the law to a concrete set of facts... should be classified as high-risk." — Recital 56.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates using AI tools for "legal research and interpretation" must verify if their tool providers comply with "High-Risk" requirements if the tool is intended to assist in judicial decision-making. While "standard" administrative tools (scheduling, billing) are likely "Minimal Risk," any tool that mimics legal reasoning or drafting for court use falls into a regulated category requiring high levels of transparency and human-in-the-loop oversight.
