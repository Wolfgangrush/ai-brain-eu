# Bar Rules: Conflict of Interest — European Union (CCBE)

## Source URL
https://www.ccbe.eu/fileadmin/speciality_distribution/public/documents/DEONTOLOGY/DEON_CoC/EN_DEON_CoC.pdf

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule (CCBE Code of Conduct)

## Key Facts
1. **Basic Prohibition:** A lawyer may not advise, represent or act on behalf of two or more clients in the same matter if there is a conflict, or a significant risk of a conflict, between the interests of those clients (Rule 3.2.1).
2. **Duty to Cease Acting:** A lawyer must cease to act for both (or all) clients when a conflict of interest arises between those clients and also whenever there is a risk of a breach of confidence or where the lawyer's independence may be impaired (Rule 3.2.2).
3. **New Clients:** A lawyer must also refrain from acting for a new client if there is a risk of a breach of a confidence entrusted to the lawyer by a former client or if the knowledge which the lawyer possesses of the affairs of the former client would give an unfair advantage to the new client (Rule 3.2.3).
4. **Group Practice:** Where lawyers are practicing in a group, the rules on conflict of interest apply to the group as a whole and all its members (Rule 3.2.4).
5. **Incompatible Occupations:** Independence is paramount; a lawyer must avoid any occupation that would compromise their ability to provide unbiased advice (Rule 2.5).
6. **Financial Interest:** A lawyer should not have a financial interest in the subject matter of the litigation that could influence their professional judgment.
7. **Personal Relationships:** Conflicts can also arise from personal relationships with the opposing party or their counsel, which must be disclosed or lead to withdrawal.
8. **Informed Consent:** In some jurisdictions, a conflict might be waived by the clients if they give informed consent in writing, but the CCBE rules are generally strict about "significant risk" leading to a duty to cease acting.
9. **Confidentiality vs. Conflict:** The duty of confidentiality often triggers the conflict rule; if you can't be 100% loyal to one client without using secrets of another, you must withdraw.
10. **Sanctions:** Disciplinary action by national Bars for breach of conflict rules is often severe, including suspension or disbarment.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A lawyer must cease to act for both or all of the clients concerned when a conflict of interest arises between those clients." — Rule 3.2.2.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
A solo advocate must maintain a robust "Conflict Check" system, even for small matters. Before taking a new instruction, they must search their database for any previous involvement with the opposing party or related entities. In a small community or niche practice, this is the most common cause of professional liability.
