# Statute-Text Digest: Digital Services Act (DSA)

## Citation
Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 19 October 2022 on a Single Market For Digital Services and amending Directive 2000/31/EC (Digital Services Act) (Text with EEA relevance).

## Authority Tier
EU Regulation (Primary Legislative Act - Directly Applicable in all Member States).

## Currency
*   **Adopted:** 19 October 2022.
*   **In Force:** 16 November 2022.
*   **Fully Applicable:** 17 February 2024 (with earlier application for Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) and Very Large Online Search Engines (VLOSEs)).

## Scope (Core Operative Articles)
Focus on Chapter II (Liability of providers of intermediary services), Chapter III (Due diligence obligations for a safe and transparent online environment), and Chapter IV (Additional obligations for VLOPs and VLOSEs to manage systemic risks).

---

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Article 4: ‘Mere conduit’
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Providers of "conduit" services (like ISPs) aren't responsible for the data they transmit as long as they don't start the transmission, pick the receiver, or change the data.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Provider is not liable for information transmitted, provided it does not initiate the transmission; does not select the receiver; and does not select or modify the information.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 4, Reg. (EU) 2022/2065.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 5 (Caching), Art. 6 (Hosting), Art. 8 (No general monitoring).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Defense for infrastructure providers against claims of illegal content dissemination.

### Article 6: Hosting
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Websites that host user content (like social media or forums) aren't liable for illegal stuff users post UNLESS they know about it and don't remove it quickly.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Provider is not liable for information stored at the request of a recipient, on condition that the provider: (a) does not have actual knowledge of illegal activity or illegal content; (b) upon obtaining such knowledge, acts expeditiously to remove or disable access.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 6, Reg. (EU) 2022/2065.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 16 (Notice and action), Art. 17 (Statement of reasons).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Primary liability shield for platforms; critical for understanding "Notice and Takedown" triggers.

### Article 8: No general monitoring or active fact-finding obligations
*   **Plain-English Summary:** EU countries can't force platforms to spy on everyone to find illegal activity. Platforms only have to act on specific knowledge.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** No general obligation on providers to monitor the information which they transmit or store, nor a general obligation actively to seek facts or circumstances indicating illegal activity.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 8, Reg. (EU) 2022/2065.
*   **Cross-references:** Recital 30, Art. 9 (Orders to act).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Use to challenge overly broad injunctions or national laws requiring proactive automated filtering.

### Article 16: Notice and action mechanisms
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Platforms must give people an easy way to report illegal content.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Providers shall put mechanisms in place to allow any individual or entity to notify them of the presence of specific items of information that the individual or entity considers to be illegal content.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 16, Reg. (EU) 2022/2065.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 6 (Hosting), Art. 17 (Statement of reasons).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Procedural basis for filing formal complaints against platforms for failing to remove illegal content.

### Article 25: Online interface design and organisation (Dark Patterns)
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Platforms can't use "dark patterns" or manipulative designs that trick users into making choices they might not want to make.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Providers shall not design, organise or operate their online interfaces in a way that deceives or manipulates the recipients of their service or in a way that otherwise materially distorts or impairs the ability of the recipients of their service to make free and informed decisions.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 25, Reg. (EU) 2022/2065.
*   **Cross-references:** GDPR Art. 4(11) (Consent), Unfair Commercial Practices Directive.
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Basis for consumer protection litigation regarding deceptive UI/UX practices.

### Article 34: Risk assessment (VLOPs/VLOSEs)
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Huge platforms must regularly check if their services are causing major problems like spreading hate, harming kids, or messing with elections.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** VLOPs and VLOSEs shall identify, analyse and assess any systemic risks in the Union stemming from the design or functioning of their service and its related systems, including algorithmic systems.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 34, Reg. (EU) 2022/2065.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 35 (Mitigation), Art. 37 (Independent audit).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Use to demand transparency or evidence of risk management in high-stakes public interest cases.
