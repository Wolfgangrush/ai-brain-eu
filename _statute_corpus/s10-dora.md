# DORA - Digital Operational Resilience Act (EU) 2022/2554

- **Citation:** Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 14 December 2022 on digital operational resilience for the financial sector
- **Authority Tier:** Tier 2 (EU Regulation)
- **Currency:** Current (Fully applicable as of 17 January 2025)
- **Scope:** Creates a comprehensive framework for managing ICT (Information and Communication Technology) risks in the financial sector, covering ICT risk management, incident reporting, resilience testing, and third-party risk.

## Section-by-Section Digest (Core Operative Sections)

### Articles 1–2: Subject Matter and Scope
- **Plain-English Summary:** Outlines that the regulation applies to a wide range of financial entities (banks, insurers, investment firms, CASPs) and their ICT third-party service providers.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Lays down uniform requirements concerning the security of network and information systems supporting the business processes of financial entities.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 2, Reg. (EU) 2022/2554.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Verify if a client (e.g., a fintech startup or a SaaS vendor to a bank) falls within the direct or indirect scope of DORA’s compliance requirements.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 3: Definitions
- **Plain-English Summary:** Defines key terms like "ICT risk," "major ICT-related incident," and "critical or important function."
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Provides the technical and legal foundation for interpreting the resilience obligations.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 3, Reg. (EU) 2022/2554.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Use these definitions to draft or review ICT service agreements, ensuring terms align with the statutory standard of care.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 5–16: ICT Risk Management
- **Plain-English Summary:** Requires financial entities to have a robust ICT risk management framework, including governance, detection, protection, and recovery strategies.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Entities shall have internal governance and control frameworks that ensure effective and prudent management of ICT risk.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 5, Reg. (EU) 2022/2554.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Audit a client's internal IT policies or "Disaster Recovery Plans" to ensure they meet the specific requirements of the Management Body’s responsibility (Art. 5).
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 17–23: ICT-Related Incident Management and Reporting
- **Plain-English Summary:** Mandates a process for detecting and managing ICT incidents and requires reporting "major" incidents to regulators within very short windows.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Financial entities shall define, establish and implement an ICT-related incident management process to detect, manage and notify ICT-related incidents.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 17, Reg. (EU) 2022/2554.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Critical during a cyber-incident; advise the client on the legal classification of the breach and the immediate reporting obligations to avoid heavy fines.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 28–30: Management of ICT Third-Party Risk
- **Plain-English Summary:** Rules for how financial firms manage their relationships with tech vendors (e.g., Cloud providers). Mandates specific "minimum" clauses in all ICT contracts.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Financial entities shall manage ICT third-party risk as an integral component of ICT risk within their risk management framework.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 28, Reg. (EU) 2022/2554.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Reviewing and "re-papering" vendor contracts to include mandatory clauses regarding audit rights, service levels, and termination (Art. 30).
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**
