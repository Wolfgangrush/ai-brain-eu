# Statute Digest: ePrivacy Directive

## Citation
Directive 2002/58/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 12 July 2002 concerning the processing of personal data and the protection of privacy in the electronic communications sector (Directive on privacy and electronic communications), as amended by Directive 2009/136/EC.

## Authority Tier
EU Directive (Transposed into national law by Member States). It complements and particularises Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR).

## Currency
Current (last major amendment in 2009; currently awaiting replacement by the ePrivacy Regulation). Consolidates the 2002 original and 2009 "Citizen's Rights" amendments.

## Scope
Focuses on the processing of personal data and the protection of privacy in the electronic communications sector. Specifically covers confidentiality of communications, security of services, and specific data types (traffic, location, cookies).

---

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Article 1: Scope and Aim
- **Plain-English Summary**: Defines the purpose of the directive as harmonizing national laws to ensure an equivalent level of protection for fundamental rights, particularly the right to privacy and confidentiality, in the electronic communications sector.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: This Directive provides for the harmonisation of the national provisions required to ensure an equivalent level of protection of fundamental rights and freedoms, and in particular the right to privacy and confidentiality, with respect to the processing of personal data in the electronic communication sector and to ensure the free movement of such data and of electronic communication equipment and services in the Community.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 1, Dir. 2002/58/EC
- **Cross-references**: Art. 7 & 8 EU Charter; GDPR Art. 95.
- **Practical Use**: Establishing the legal basis for privacy claims specific to telcos and web services.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 5: Confidentiality of Communications
- **Plain-English Summary**: Prohibits the listening, tapping, storage, or other kinds of interception of communications by anyone other than users, without their consent (except where legally authorized).
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: 1. Member States shall ensure the confidentiality of communications and the related traffic data by means of a public communications network and publicly available electronic communications services... they shall prohibit listening, tapping, storage or other kinds of interception or surveillance of communications and the related traffic data by persons other than users, without the consent of the users concerned...
- **Citation Format**: Art. 5(1), Dir. 2002/58/EC
- **Cross-references**: Art. 5(3) (Cookies); Art. 15 (Law enforcement exceptions).
- **Practical Use**: Primary shield against unauthorized wiretapping or metadata harvesting.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 5(3): The "Cookie Provision"
- **Plain-English Summary**: Requires users to give consent before information (like cookies) is stored on or accessed from their devices, unless it is strictly necessary for a service they requested.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: Member States shall ensure that the storing of information, or the gaining of access to information already stored, in the terminal equipment of a subscriber or user is only allowed on condition that the subscriber or user concerned has given his or her consent, having been provided with clear and comprehensive information... This shall not prevent any technical storage or access for the sole purpose of carrying out the transmission of a communication... or as strictly necessary in order for the provider... to provide an information society service explicitly requested by the subscriber or user.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 5(3), Dir. 2002/58/EC
- **Cross-references**: GDPR Art. 4(11) (Consent definition); Art. 7 (Conditions for consent).
- **Practical Use**: Basis for challenging non-compliant cookie banners and tracking scripts.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 6: Traffic Data
- **Plain-English Summary**: Traffic data must be deleted or anonymized once a call or message is finished, unless it's needed for billing or the user has consented to its use for marketing.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: Traffic data relating to subscribers and users processed and stored by the provider of a public communications network or publicly available electronic communications service must be erased or made anonymous when it is no longer needed for the purpose of the transmission of a communication... Processing of traffic data is allowed for billing and interconnection payments... up to the end of the period during which the bill may lawfully be challenged.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 6, Dir. 2002/58/EC
- **Cross-references**: Art. 2 (Definition of traffic data); Art. 15.
- **Practical Use**: Limiting the "data trail" left by metadata; ensuring prompt deletion.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 9: Location Data
- **Plain-English Summary**: Location data (beyond what's needed for the network) can only be processed if anonymized or with user consent, and only for the duration needed for a specific service.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: Where location data other than traffic data... can be processed, such data may only be processed when they are made anonymous, or with the consent of the users or subscribers to the extent and for the duration necessary for the provision of a value-added service.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 9, Dir. 2002/58/EC
- **Cross-references**: Art. 2 (Definition of location data).
- **Practical Use**: Protecting users against persistent GPS/cell-tower tracking by apps and telcos.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 13: Unsolicited Communications (Spam)
- **Plain-English Summary**: Prohibits sending marketing emails, faxes, or automated calls without prior consent (opt-in), except for existing customers for similar products (soft opt-in).
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: 1. The use of automated calling and communication systems without human intervention (automatic calling machines), facsimile machines (fax) or electronic mail for the purposes of direct marketing may be allowed only in respect of subscribers or users who have given their prior consent.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 13, Dir. 2002/58/EC
- **Cross-references**: Art. 13(2) (Soft opt-in exception).
- **Practical Use**: Enforcement against illegal cold-calling and email spam.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED
