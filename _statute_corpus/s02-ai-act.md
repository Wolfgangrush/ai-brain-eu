# EU AI Act – Statute-Text Digest
**Citation**: Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 June 2024 laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence (Artificial Intelligence Act).
**Authority Tier**: Primary EU Regulation (Directly applicable in all Member States).
**Currency**: Entered into force on August 1, 2024.
**Scope**: Focus on Articles 1-113 + Annexes I-XIII. Covers harmonised rules for the market placement, service, and use of AI systems in the Union.

---

## Chapter I: General Provisions

### Article 1: Subject matter
- **Plain-English summary:** Sets out the Regulation's purpose: improving the internal market and promoting trustworthy AI, while protecting health, safety, and fundamental rights.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Lays down (a) harmonised rules for AI placement; (b) prohibitions on certain AI practices; (c) specific requirements for high-risk systems; (d) transparency rules for certain AI; (e) rules on GPAI models; (f) governance and market surveillance rules.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Art. 1 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Recitals 1-5; Art. 2.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Establishes the legal "spirit" and objective of the Act for litigation or advisory scoping.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 2: Scope
- **Plain-English summary:** Defines who the Act applies to, including providers, deployers, importers, and distributors. Crucially includes extraterritorial reach.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Applies to: (a) providers placing AI in the EU; (b) deployers located in the EU; (c) providers/deployers outside the EU if output is used in the EU. Exempts: Military, defense, national security, personal non-professional use, and systems for R&D only.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Art. 2 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 3.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Essential for determining if a client's specific business model (e.g., a US-based SaaS used by EU citizens) triggers compliance.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 3: Definitions
- **Plain-English summary:** Definitive list of legal terms.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Defines 'AI system' (machine-based system with varying autonomy, inferring outputs to influence environment); 'GPAI model' (model trained on large data, significant generality); 'Provider' (developer); 'Deployer' (user under authority).
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Art. 3 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** OECD Framework.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Primary reference for "Is this an AI system?" legal opinions.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 4: AI Literacy
- **Plain-English summary:** Mandates training for staff handling AI systems.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Providers and deployers must take measures to ensure sufficient AI literacy of their staff, considering technical knowledge and experience.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Feb 2025]
- **Citation format:** Art. 4 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Recital 20.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Immediate advisory task: audit client training programs and update HR policies.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Chapter II: Prohibited AI Practices

### Article 5: Prohibited AI Practices
- **Plain-English summary:** Lists AI uses that are strictly banned in the EU due to unacceptable risk.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Prohibits:
  - (a) Subliminal/deceptive techniques leading to physical/psychological harm.
  - (b) Exploitation of vulnerabilities (age, disability, socio-economic status).
  - (c) Social scoring leading to detrimental/disproportionate treatment.
  - (d) Predictive policing solely based on profiling/personality traits.
  - (e) Untargeted scraping of facial images from the internet or CCTV.
  - (f) Emotion recognition in workplace/education (except medical/safety).
  - (g) Biometric categorization using sensitive traits (race, religion, etc.).
  - (h) Real-time remote biometric identification for law enforcement (with narrow exceptions for crime prevention/missing persons).
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Feb 2025]
- **Citation format:** Art. 5 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Recitals 29-41.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Critical compliance "Red Line" audit. If a client is building a banned system, they must cease or pivot immediately.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Chapter III: High-Risk AI Systems

### Article 6: Classification rules for high-risk AI systems
- **Plain-English summary:** The gatekeeper article that defines what is "High-Risk".
- **Statutory text (Structural):** High-risk if: (1) System is a product or safety component of a product covered by Annex II (e.g. machinery, medical devices); OR (2) System is listed in Annex III (e.g. Biometrics, Critical Infrastructure, Education, Employment, Law Enforcement, Migration, Justice).
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026] (for Annex III) / [APPLICATION: Aug 2027] (for Annex II)
- **Citation format:** Art. 6 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Annex II, Annex III, Art. 7.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Primary classification tool. Used to determine if a client faces the Act's heaviest regulatory burdens.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Articles 8-15: Requirements for High-Risk AI Systems
- **Plain-English summary:** Sets the technical and organizational standards for High-Risk AI.
- **Statutory text (Structural):**
  - Art. 9: Risk Management System (continuous lifecycle process).
  - Art. 10: Data & Data Governance (training data must be high-quality, representative, and bias-checked).
  - Art. 11: Technical Documentation (detailed record-keeping).
  - Art. 13: Transparency and Provision of Info (instructions for use for deployers).
  - Art. 14: Human Oversight (designed for human intervention).
  - Art. 15: Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Arts. 8-15 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 16, Art. 26.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Basis for "Compliance Audits" and "AI Policy Manuals".
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 16: Obligations of Providers of High-Risk AI Systems
- **Plain-English summary:** The comprehensive "To-Do" list for AI developers.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Providers must: ensure compliance with Arts 8-15; have a quality management system; draw up documentation; undergo conformity assessment; register in EU database; affix CE marking.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Art. 16 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 43 (Conformity Assessment).
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Check-list for corporate counsel representing AI vendors.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 26: Obligations of Deployers of High-Risk AI Systems
- **Plain-English summary:** Obligations for companies *using* high-risk AI tools.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Deployers must: use in accordance with instructions; assign human oversight; monitor for incidents; keep logs; inform provider of serious incidents.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Art. 26 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 27.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Advising clients on "Responsible AI Use" and liability management when deploying 3rd party high-risk tools.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Chapter IV: Transparency Obligations

### Article 50: Transparency obligations for certain AI systems
- **Plain-English summary:** Mandates disclosure for systems that "impersonate" or "generate" (Deepfakes, Chatbots).
- **Statutory text (Structural):** (1) AI interacting with people must disclose it is AI. (2) Emotion recognition/biometric categorization systems must disclose to exposed persons. (3) Deepfakes must be labeled. (4) AI-generated text for public interest must be disclosed.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2026]
- **Citation format:** Art. 50 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Recitals 131-134.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Advising marketing and creative agencies on mandatory "Made with AI" watermarks and disclaimers.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Chapter V: General-Purpose AI Models (GPAI)

### Article 51: Classification of GPAI as systemic risk
- **Plain-English summary:** Identifies which foundational models are "Systemic" (e.g., GPT-4 class).
- **Statutory text (Structural):** High-impact if: (a) high-impact capabilities exist, OR (b) Training compute > 10^25 FLOPs.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2025]
- **Citation format:** Art. 51 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 52, Annex XIII.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Threshold analysis for high-tier foundational model startups.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 53: Obligations for providers of GPAI models
- **Plain-English summary:** Basic compliance for all base models.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Must: (a) Draw up technical documentation; (b) Provide info for downstream integrators; (c) Establish policy to comply with EU Copyright law (opt-out adherence); (d) Publish training content summary.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2025]
- **Citation format:** Art. 53 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 55.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Helping startups set up copyright-compliant training pipelines.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Article 55: Obligations for GPAI models with systemic risk
- **Plain-English summary:** Heaviest burdens for the largest models.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** In addition to Art 53: Model evaluations (red-teaming); Risk assessment and mitigation; Incident reporting to AI Office; Cybersecurity protection.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2025]
- **Citation format:** Art. 55 AI Act
- **Cross-references:** Art. 51.
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Strategic advisory for top-tier AI labs.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Chapters VI - XII: Governance & Enforcement

### Articles 64 - 71: Governance
- **Plain-English summary:** Creates the European AI Office and the European Artificial Intelligence Board.
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2025]
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Articles 99 - 101: Penalties
- **Plain-English summary:** Outlines the massive fine structure.
- **Statutory text (Structural):**
  - Banned practices (Art 5): Up to €35M or 7% of global turnover.
  - High-risk / GPAI non-compliance: Up to €15M or 3% of turnover.
  - Incorrect information: Up to €7.5M or 1.5% of turnover.
  - (Lower caps for SMEs/startups).
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Aug 2025] (for GPAI/Prohibited), [APPLICATION: Aug 2026] (general).
- **Citation format:** Art. 99 AI Act
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** The definitive "Cost of Non-Compliance" table for risk-assessment reports.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

## Chapter XIII: Final Provisions

### Article 113: Entry into force and application
- **Plain-English summary:** The definitive countdown clock.
- **Statutory text (Structural):** Regulation applies 24 months from entry (Aug 2026), EXCEPT:
  - Ch I & II (Prohibited): 6 months (Feb 2025).
  - Ch V (GPAI), Governance, Penalties: 12 months (Aug 2025).
  - Annex II High-Risk Products: 36 months (Aug 2027).
- **Phased Application Tag:** [APPLICATION: Multi-phase]
- **Citation format:** Art. 113 AI Act
- **Practical use for solo advocate:** Critical for setting client project timelines and prioritization.
- **STATUS:** VERIFIED

- **Annex IV**: Technical documentation for high-risk.
- **Annex XI**: Technical documentation for GPAI.
- **Annex XIII**: Criteria for designating GPAI with systemic risk.
