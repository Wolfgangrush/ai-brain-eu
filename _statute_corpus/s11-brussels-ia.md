# Brussels Ia - Jurisdiction and Enforcement (EU) 1215/2012

- **Citation:** Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 12 December 2012 on jurisdiction and the recognition and enforcement of judgments in civil and commercial matters (Recast)
- **Authority Tier:** Tier 2 (EU Regulation)
- **Currency:** Current
- **Scope:** Determines which EU Member State's courts have jurisdiction in civil and commercial disputes and facilitates the "free movement of judgments" within the EU.

## Section-by-Section Digest (Core Operative Sections)

### Articles 1–3: Scope and Exclusions
- **Plain-English Summary:** Applies to most "civil and commercial" lawsuits. It specifically excludes family law, bankruptcy, social security, and arbitration.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** This Regulation shall apply in civil and commercial matters whatever the nature of the court or tribunal.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 1, Reg. (EU) No 1215/2012.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** First step in any cross-border case: confirm the dispute is "civil/commercial" and not an excluded matter like an arbitration-related dispute.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 4: General Jurisdiction (Defendant’s Domicile)
- **Plain-English Summary:** The default rule: you sue a person/company in the courts of the Member State where they are based (domiciled).
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Persons domiciled in a Member State shall, whatever their nationality, be sued in the courts of that Member State.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 4(1), Reg. (EU) No 1215/2012.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Determine the "safe" jurisdiction for filing a claim if no special rules apply.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 7: Special Jurisdiction (Contract and Tort)
- **Plain-English Summary:** Exceptions to Art. 4. Allows suing in the "place of performance" for contracts or the "place where the harmful event occurred" for torts.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** A person domiciled in a Member State may be sued in another Member State: (1) in matters relating to a contract, in the courts for the place of performance...
- **Citation Format:** Art. 7(1), Reg. (EU) No 1215/2012.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Strategic forum selection; suing a foreign defendant in your client’s home court if the contract was performed there or the damage happened there.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 17–19: Jurisdiction over Consumer Contracts
- **Plain-English Summary:** Protects consumers by allowing them to sue a foreign company in their own home court, provided the company "directs" activities to that State.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** A consumer may bring proceedings against the other party to a contract either in the courts of the Member State in which that party is domiciled or in the courts for the place where the consumer is domiciled.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 18, Reg. (EU) No 1215/2012.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Powerful tool for representing individuals in claims against large international platforms or service providers.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 24: Exclusive Jurisdiction
- **Plain-English Summary:** Mandatory rules for certain cases (e.g., land disputes, company registration, patents). These override any agreement between parties.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** The following courts shall have exclusive jurisdiction: (1) in proceedings which have as their object rights in rem in immovable property...
- **Citation Format:** Art. 24, Reg. (EU) No 1215/2012.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Avoiding the "jurisdiction trap"; ensuring a case regarding real estate is filed in the location of the land, regardless of contract clauses.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 25: Prorogation of Jurisdiction (Choice of Court)
- **Plain-English Summary:** Allows parties to agree in writing which court will hear their disputes.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** If the parties have agreed that a court of a Member State is to have jurisdiction, that court shall have jurisdiction, unless the agreement is null and void.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 25, Reg. (EU) No 1215/2012.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Drafting and enforcing "Choice of Law and Jurisdiction" clauses in commercial contracts.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**
