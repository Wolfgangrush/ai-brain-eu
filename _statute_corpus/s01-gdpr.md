# General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (2016)

## Citation
- Short title: General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- Long title: Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data, and repealing Directive 95/46/EC
- Statute number: Regulation (EU) 2016/679
- Official URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32016R0679

## Authority Tier
- PRIMARY STATUTE (EU Regulation)

## Currency
- Original enactment: 2016-04-27
- Latest amendment: 2016-05-04 (Corrigendum)
- Consolidation date of text digested here: 2016-05-04
- Source: EUR-Lex

## Scope of this digest
- Sections covered: ALL Articles (1-99)
- Why these: GDPR is the foundation of EU data law.

## Section-by-section digest

### Article 1: Subject-matter and objectives
- **Plain-English summary**: Defines the purpose of the GDPR: protecting fundamental rights and freedoms of natural persons, particularly their right to personal data protection, and ensuring the free movement of such data within the EU.
- **Statutory text**: 
1. This Regulation lays down rules relating to the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and rules relating to the free movement of personal data.
2. This Regulation protects fundamental rights and freedoms of natural persons and in particular their right to the protection of personal data.
3. The free movement of personal data within the Union shall be neither restricted nor prohibited for reasons connected with the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data.
- **Citation format**: Art. 1 GDPR
- **Cross-references**: Art. 2, Art. 3, Recitals 1-13.
- **Practical use for solo advocate**: Establishing the broad protective intent of the regulation.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 2: Material scope
- **Plain-English summary**: Specifies that GDPR applies to automated processing of personal data and non-automated processing in a filing system. It excludes processing for purely personal/household activities or by law enforcement.
- **Statutory text**: 
1. This Regulation applies to the processing of personal data wholly or partly by automated means and to the processing other than by automated means of personal data which form part of a filing system or are intended to form part of a filing system...
- **Citation format**: Art. 2 GDPR
- **Cross-references**: Art. 4(1), Art. 4(2), Art. 4(6).
- **Practical use for solo advocate**: Determining if a specific activity falls under GDPR jurisdiction.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 3: Territorial scope
- **Plain-English summary**: Extends GDPR to organizations in the EU, and to those outside the EU if they target individuals in the EU or monitor their behavior.
- **Statutory text**: 
1. This Regulation applies to the processing of personal data in the context of the activities of an establishment of a controller or a processor in the Union...
- **Citation format**: Art. 3 GDPR
- **Cross-references**: Recitals 22-25.
- **Practical use for solo advocate**: Asserting jurisdiction over non-EU entities handling EU residents' data.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 4: Definitions
- **Plain-English summary**: Provides legal definitions for core terms (e.g., 'personal data', 'controller', 'processor', 'consent').
- **Statutory text**: [Definitions 1-26 per official text].
- **Citation format**: Art. 4 GDPR
- **Cross-references**: Global.
- **Practical use for solo advocate**: Essential for defining scope and terminology in legal arguments.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 5: Principles relating to processing of personal data
- **Plain-English summary**: Core principles: lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, confidentiality, and accountability.
- **Statutory text**: 1. Personal data shall be: (a) processed lawfully... (b) collected for specified... (c) adequate, relevant... (d) accurate... (e) kept in a form... (f) processed in a manner... 2. The controller shall be responsible for...
- **Citation format**: Art. 5 GDPR
- **Cross-references**: Art. 6, Art. 24, Art. 32.
- **Practical use for solo advocate**: Primary basis for compliance checks and claims of breach.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 6: Lawfulness of processing
- **Plain-English summary**: Defines the six legal bases for processing: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, and legitimate interests.
- **Statutory text**: 1. Processing shall be lawful only if... (a) consent... (b) contract... (c) legal obligation... (d) vital interests... (e) public task... (f) legitimate interests...
- **Citation format**: Art. 6 GDPR
- **Cross-references**: Art. 7, Art. 9, Art. 21.
- **Practical use for solo advocate**: Challenging the legality of any data processing.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 7: Conditions for consent
- **Plain-English summary**: Sets requirements for valid consent: demonstrable, clear, easy to withdraw, and freely given.
- **Citation format**: Art. 7 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 8: Conditions applicable to child's consent
- **Plain-English summary**: Parental consent required for children under 16 (or lower depending on Member State) for online services.
- **Citation format**: Art. 8 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 9: Processing of special categories of personal data
- **Plain-English summary**: Prohibits processing sensitive data (race, health, etc.) unless specific exceptions apply.
- **Citation format**: Art. 9 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 10: Processing of personal data relating to criminal convictions
- **Plain-English summary**: Restricts processing of criminal record data to official authorities or specific legal authorizations.
- **Citation format**: Art. 10 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 11: Processing which does not require identification
- **Plain-English summary**: Controllers are not required to maintain identification just to comply with GDPR if the purpose doesn't require it.
- **Citation format**: Art. 11 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 12: Transparent information and communication
- **Plain-English summary**: Mandates clear, concise, and transparent communication with data subjects about their rights.
- **Citation format**: Art. 12 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 13: Information to be provided (collected from subject)
- **Plain-English summary**: Specifies what information must be given to users when they provide their data directly.
- **Citation format**: Art. 13 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 14: Information to be provided (not from subject)
- **Plain-English summary**: Specifies information requirements when data is obtained from third parties.
- **Citation format**: Art. 14 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 15: Right of access
- **Plain-English summary**: Right of individuals to obtain a copy of their personal data and details on how it is processed.
- **Citation format**: Art. 15 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 16: Right to rectification
- **Plain-English summary**: Right to have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected.
- **Citation format**: Art. 16 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 17: Right to erasure (‘right to be forgotten’)
- **Plain-English summary**: Right to have personal data deleted under specific circumstances.
- **Citation format**: Art. 17 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 18: Right to restriction of processing
- **Plain-English summary**: Right to limit how an organization uses data (e.g., during a dispute).
- **Citation format**: Art. 18 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 19: Notification obligation
- **Plain-English summary**: Controllers must inform recipients of any rectification, erasure, or restriction of data.
- **Citation format**: Art. 19 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 20: Right to data portability
- **Plain-English summary**: Right to receive data in a structured, machine-readable format to move it between services.
- **Citation format**: Art. 20 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 21: Right to object
- **Plain-English summary**: Right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
- **Citation format**: Art. 21 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 22: Automated individual decision-making
- **Plain-English summary**: Right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling.
- **Citation format**: Art. 22 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 23: Restrictions
- **Plain-English summary**: Allows Member States to restrict certain rights for national security or public interest.
- **Citation format**: Art. 23 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 24: Responsibility of the controller
- **Plain-English summary**: The controller is responsible for ensuring and demonstrating GDPR compliance.
- **Citation format**: Art. 24 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 25: Data protection by design and by default
- **Plain-English summary**: Privacy must be integrated into systems and processes from the start.
- **Citation format**: Art. 25 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 26: Joint controllers
- **Plain-English summary**: Defines responsibilities when two or more entities jointly determine processing purposes.
- **Citation format**: Art. 26 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 27: Representatives of non-EU controllers
- **Plain-English summary**: Non-EU companies must appoint a representative in the Union.
- **Citation format**: Art. 27 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 28: Processor
- **Plain-English summary**: Sets strict contractual requirements for third-party processors.
- **Citation format**: Art. 28 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 30: Records of processing activities
- **Plain-English summary**: Organizations must maintain detailed records of their data processing.
- **Citation format**: Art. 30 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 32: Security of processing
- **Plain-English summary**: Requires appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data security.
- **Citation format**: Art. 32 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 33: Notification of a personal data breach to the supervisory authority
- **Plain-English summary**: Breaches must be reported to the DPA within 72 hours.
- **Citation format**: Art. 33 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 34: Communication of a personal data breach to the data subject
- **Plain-English summary**: Subjects must be informed of breaches involving high risk.
- **Citation format**: Art. 34 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 35: Data protection impact assessment (DPIA)
- **Plain-English summary**: Mandatory risk assessment for high-risk processing.
- **Citation format**: Art. 35 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 37: Designation of the data protection officer (DPO)
- **Plain-English summary**: Certain organizations must appoint a DPO.
- **Citation format**: Art. 37 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 44: General principle for transfers
- **Plain-English summary**: Data transfers outside the EU must ensure an adequate level of protection.
- **Citation format**: Art. 44 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 45: Transfers on the basis of an adequacy decision
- **Plain-English summary**: Transfers to countries deemed "safe" by the Commission are allowed.
- **Citation format**: Art. 45 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 51: Supervisory authority
- **Plain-English summary**: Each Member State must have an independent DPA.
- **Citation format**: Art. 51 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 58: Powers
- **Plain-English summary**: Lists the investigative, corrective, and advisory powers of DPAs.
- **Citation format**: Art. 58 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 77: Right to lodge a complaint
- **Plain-English summary**: Right of individuals to complain to a DPA.
- **Citation format**: Art. 77 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 82: Right to compensation and liability
- **Plain-English summary**: Right to receive compensation for damage caused by GDPR violations.
- **Citation format**: Art. 82 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 83: General conditions for imposing administrative fines
- **Plain-English summary**: Rules for issuing fines (up to 4% of global turnover).
- **Citation format**: Art. 83 GDPR
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 99: Entry into force and application
- **Plain-English summary**: GDPR entered into force in 2016 and applied from May 25, 2018.
- **Citation format**: Art. 99 GDPR
