# Statute Digest: Data Act - Regulation (EU) 2023/2854

## Citation
Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 December 2023 on harmonised rules on fair access to and use of data and amending Regulation (EU) 2017/2394 and Directive (EU) 2020/1828 (Data Act).

## Authority Tier
EU Regulation (Directly Applicable in all Member States).

## Currency
*   **Entered into force:** 11 January 2024.
*   **Date of application:** Generally from 12 September 2025 (with some exceptions for specific articles).

## Scope
The Data Act establishes a harmonized framework for the access to and use of data generated by connected products (IoT) and related services. It focuses on:
*   B2C and B2B data sharing rights.
*   Protection against unfair contractual terms in data sharing.
*   B2G data sharing in cases of exceptional need.
*   Facilitating switching between data processing services (cloud/edge).

---

## Section-by-section digest

### Article 3: Obligation to make data accessible
*   **Plain-English summary:** Manufacturers of connected products and providers of related services must design them so that data generated by their use is accessible to the user by default. Users must be informed about what data is collected and how to access it before they buy or sign up.
*   **Statutory text (Summary):**
    1. Products and services shall be designed/provided such that product and related service data are, by default, easily, securely and directly accessible to the user.
    2. Before contract, users must be informed of the type, format, and volume of data; whether it is generated continuously; storage location/period; and how to access/erase it.
*   **Citation format:** Art. 3 Reg. (EU) 2023/2854.
*   **Cross-references:** Article 4 (Access rights), Article 5 (Sharing with third parties).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Use to challenge manufacturers who lock users out of their own device data or fail to provide transparency during the sales process.
*   **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 4: Rights and obligations of users and data holders
*   **Plain-English summary:** If a user can't access data directly from the device, the "data holder" must provide it for free, without delay, and in a machine-readable format. Data holders can't use non-personal data to spy on the user's economic situation or build a competing product.
*   **Statutory text (Summary):**
    1. Data holders shall make available to the user the data they hold without undue delay, free of charge, and in a structured, commonly used format.
    2. Data holders shall not use non-personal data on the basis of a contract with the user to derive insights about the user's economic situation that could undermine their commercial position.
    3. Neither party may use the data to develop a competing connected product.
*   **Citation format:** Art. 4 Reg. (EU) 2023/2854.
*   **Cross-references:** GDPR (for personal data aspects), Article 5 (Third-party requests).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Foundation for "right to repair" or data portability claims where the data holder is uncooperative.
*   **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 5: Right to share data with third parties
*   **Plain-English summary:** Users have the right to tell a data holder to send their data to a third party (e.g., an independent repair shop or a competing service provider). The data holder must do this under the same quality and format conditions as for the user.
*   **Statutory text (Summary):**
    1. Upon user request, the data holder shall make data available to a third party without undue delay and in a machine-readable format.
    2. Third parties must only process data for agreed purposes and cannot use it to develop competing products or derive commercial insights about the data holder.
*   **Citation format:** Art. 5 Reg. (EU) 2023/2854.
*   **Cross-references:** Article 8 (FRAND terms), Article 9 (Compensation).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Enables independent service providers to access the data they need to compete with "authorized" dealers/providers.
*   **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 8: Conditions for making data available
*   **Plain-English summary:** When a data holder is required to share data, they must do so under "Fair, Reasonable, and Non-Discriminatory" (FRAND) terms. They cannot discriminate between different recipients.
*   **Statutory text (Summary):**
    1. Data sharing under Article 5 must be on FRAND terms and in a transparent manner.
    2. Data holders shall not discriminate between comparable categories of data recipients.
*   **Citation format:** Art. 8 Reg. (EU) 2023/2854.
*   **Cross-references:** Chapter IV (Unfair terms), Article 9 (Compensation).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Prevents "gatekeeping" through prohibitive pricing or discriminatory access terms.
*   **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 13: Unfair contractual terms
*   **Plain-English summary:** Contracts about data access that are "take-it-or-leave-it" (unilaterally imposed) are not binding if they are unfair. A term is unfair if it grossly deviates from good commercial practice or limits liability for gross negligence.
*   **Statutory text (Summary):**
    1. Unilaterally imposed contractual terms regarding data access/use shall not be binding if they are unfair.
    2. A term is unfair if it grossly deviates from good commercial practice, contrary to good faith and fair dealing.
    3. Examples: excluding liability for intentional acts, or giving one party exclusive right to interpret the contract.
*   **Citation format:** Art. 13 Reg. (EU) 2023/2854.
*   **Cross-references:** EU Unfair Contract Terms Directive (Directive 93/13/EEC).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Direct tool to strike down abusive clauses in standard form contracts between SMEs and large tech providers.
*   **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 23: Removing obstacles to effective switching
*   **Plain-English summary:** Cloud and edge service providers must let customers switch to another provider or move their data back to their own servers. They must remove technical and contractual barriers to switching.
*   **Statutory text (Summary):**
    1. Providers shall take measures to enable customers to switch providers or port data.
    2. Obstacles to terminating contracts (max 30 days notice) and porting assets (exportable data/apps) must be removed.
*   **Citation format:** Art. 23 Reg. (EU) 2023/2854.
*   **Cross-references:** Article 25 (Technical aspects of switching).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Defending clients against "vendor lock-in" by cloud infrastructure or software providers.
*   **STATUS: VERIFIED**
