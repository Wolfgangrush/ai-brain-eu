# Statute Digest: Cyber Resilience Act (CRA)

## Citation
Regulation (EU) 2024/2847 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2024 on horizontal cybersecurity requirements for products with digital elements and amending Regulations (EU) No 168/2013 and (EU) 2019/1020 and Directive (EU) 2020/1828 (Cyber Resilience Act).

## Authority Tier
EU Regulation (Directly applicable in all Member States without national transposition).

## Currency
Adopted October 2024. 
- **Entered into force**: 18 November 2024 (20th day after publication).
- **Reporting Obligations (Art. 11)**: Apply from **18 August 2026** (21 months after entry into force).
- **Full Application**: Apply from **18 November 2027** (36 months after entry into force).

## Scope
Applies to all "products with digital elements" (software and hardware) placed on the EU market, whose intended or reasonably foreseeable use includes a direct or indirect logical or physical data connection to a device or network. Excludes certain sectors like medical devices, aviation, and cars (covered by other regulations).

---

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Article 1: Subject Matter
- **Plain-English Summary**: Sets out the rules for the market placement of digital products, including essential cybersecurity requirements, manufacturer obligations, and market surveillance.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: This Regulation lays down: (a) rules for the placing on the market of products with digital elements to ensure the cybersecurity of such products; (b) essential requirements for the design, development and production of products with digital elements, and obligations for economic operators; (c) essential requirements for the vulnerability handling processes put in place by manufacturers; (d) rules on market surveillance and enforcement.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 1, Reg. (EU) 2024/2847
- **Cross-references**: Annex I (Essential Requirements).
- **Practical Use**: Establishing the baseline requirements for any software/hardware product sold in the EU.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 2: Scope
- **Plain-English Summary**: Defines which products are covered. Broadly includes any product with a digital component that can connect to a network or device.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: This Regulation applies to products with digital elements whose intended or reasonably foreseeable use includes a direct or indirect logical or physical data connection to a device or network.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 2(1), Reg. (EU) 2024/2847
- **Cross-references**: Art. 2(2)-(5) (Exemptions).
- **Practical Use**: Determining if a specific product (e.g., an IoT device or a mobile app) falls under CRA obligations.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 10: Obligations of Manufacturers
- **Plain-English Summary**: Manufacturers must ensure products meet "essential requirements," perform risk assessments, provide technical documentation, and handle vulnerabilities for at least 5 years (or the product's lifetime).
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: 1. Manufacturers shall ensure that the product has been designed, developed and produced in accordance with the essential requirements set out in Annex I, Part I. 2. For the purposes of complying with the obligation... manufacturers shall perform an assessment of the cybersecurity risks... 5. Manufacturers shall ensure that vulnerabilities... are handled in accordance with the essential requirements set out in Annex I, Part II.
- **Citation Format**: Art. 10, Reg. (EU) 2024/2847
- **Cross-references**: Annex I; Art. 13 (Importers); Art. 14 (Distributors).
- **Practical Use**: Check-list for compliance for software/hardware vendors.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 11: Reporting Obligations
- **Plain-English Summary**: Manufacturers must report any "actively exploited vulnerability" or "severe incident" to ENISA within 24 hours of becoming aware.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: 1. A manufacturer shall notify ENISA of any actively exploited vulnerability contained in the product with digital elements... without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of it. 2. A manufacturer shall notify ENISA of any severe incident having an impact on the security of the product...
- **Citation Format**: Art. 11, Reg. (EU) 2024/2847
- **Application Date**: **18 August 2026**
- **Cross-references**: Art. 10(6) (Vulnerability handling).
- **Practical Use**: Critical for incident response and legal notification duties.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Article 24: Conformity Assessment Procedures
- **Plain-English Summary**: Products are categorized by risk. Most products use self-assessment (Module A), but "Important" or "Critical" products require third-party certification.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: 1. The manufacturer shall perform a conformity assessment of the product... 2. For products with digital elements that are not listed in Annex III or IV, the manufacturer shall use the internal control procedure (Module A).
- **Citation Format**: Art. 24, Reg. (EU) 2024/2847
- **Cross-references**: Annex III (Important Products); Annex IV (Critical Products).
- **Practical Use**: Identifying whether a product needs expensive third-party testing or can be self-certified.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED

### Annex I: Essential Requirements
- **Plain-English Summary**: Lists the technical security requirements (secure default settings, protection from unauthorized access, data encryption) and vulnerability management duties.
- **Statutory Text (Summary)**: **Part I: Security Requirements**: Products must be delivered without any known exploitable vulnerabilities; have secure default configurations; protect data integrity/confidentiality. **Part II: Vulnerability Handling**: Manufacturers must identify and document vulnerabilities; provide security updates; have a public disclosure policy.
- **Citation Format**: Annex I, Reg. (EU) 2024/2847
- **Cross-references**: Art. 10.
- **Practical Use**: Detailed engineering requirements for developers.
- **STATUS**: VERIFIED
