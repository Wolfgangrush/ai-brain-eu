# MiCA - Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (EU) 2023/1114

- **Citation:** Regulation (EU) 2023/1114 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 31 May 2023 on markets in crypto-assets
- **Authority Tier:** Tier 2 (EU Regulation)
- **Currency:** Current (In force; various application dates through 2024/2025)
- **Scope:** Establishes a harmonized regulatory framework for the issuance, public offering, and admission to trading of crypto-assets (including ARTs and EMTs) and the licensing of crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) within the EU.

## Section-by-Section Digest (Core Operative Sections)

### Articles 1–2: Subject Matter and Scope
- **Plain-English Summary:** Defines what the regulation covers (issuers and service providers) and what it excludes (e.g., NFTs with unique/non-fungible characteristics, CBDCs).
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Applies to persons engaged in the issuance of crypto-assets or the provision of services related to crypto-assets in the Union.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 1, Reg. (EU) 2023/1114.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Determine if a client's digital asset is captured by MiCA or falls under an exemption (like MiFID II instruments or truly unique NFTs).
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Article 3: Definitions
- **Plain-English Summary:** The "dictionary" of the regulation. Defines "crypto-asset," "asset-referenced token" (ART), "electronic money token" (EMT), and "distributed ledger technology" (DLT).
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Sets out exhaustive definitions for 30+ technical and legal terms used throughout the instrument.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 3(1)(x), Reg. (EU) 2023/1114.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Essential for the legal classification of assets in pleadings, crypto-legal opinions, or regulatory filings.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 4–15: Crypto-Assets other than ARTs or EMTs
- **Plain-English Summary:** Rules for "utility tokens" or general crypto-assets. Requires a "Crypto-Asset White Paper," notification to regulators, and strict marketing conduct.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Prohibits offering crypto-assets to the public without a compliant white paper and notification to the competent authority.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 4, Reg. (EU) 2023/1114.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Guide a startup through the "White Paper" drafting process and ensure compliance with the "right of withdrawal" for retail holders (Art. 13).
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 16–26: Authorization and Operating Conditions for ARTs
- **Plain-English Summary:** High-level requirements for issuers of "stablecoins" backed by assets. Requires legal entity status in the EU and prior authorization.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** ART issuers must be authorized by a competent authority and maintain specific capital requirements and governance arrangements.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 16, Reg. (EU) 2023/1114.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Advise on the significant licensing hurdle for issuers of asset-backed tokens; critical for institutional crypto projects.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**

### Articles 27–30: Obligations for Issuers of ARTs
- **Plain-English Summary:** Conduct of business rules. Issuers must act honestly, fairly, and professionally, manage conflicts of interest, and handle complaints.
- **Statutory Text (Summary):** Mandates transparency, professional behavior, and the establishment of a robust complaint-handling procedure.
- **Citation Format:** Art. 27, Reg. (EU) 2023/1114.
- **Practical Use for Solo Advocate:** Basis for litigation against an issuer for breach of statutory duties, misleading marketing, or failure to address consumer complaints.
- **STATUS: VERIFIED**
