# Statute-Text Digest: Digital Markets Act (DMA)

## Citation
Regulation (EU) 2022/1925 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 14 September 2022 on contestable and fair markets in the digital sector and amending Directives (EU) 2019/1937 and (EU) 2020/1828 (Digital Markets Act) (Text with EEA relevance).

## Authority Tier
EU Regulation (Primary Legislative Act - Directly Applicable in all Member States).

## Currency
*   **Adopted:** 14 September 2022.
*   **In Force:** 1 November 2022.
*   **Applicable:** 2 May 2023.

## Scope (Core Operative Articles)
Focus on Chapter II (Designation of Gatekeepers) and Chapter III (Practices of gatekeepers that limit contestability or are unfair), specifically the core obligations for designated gatekeepers (Arts 5, 6, and 7).

---

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Article 3: Designation of gatekeepers
*   **Plain-English Summary:** This article sets the rules for who counts as a "gatekeeper" (the tech giants). If a company has a massive impact on the market, provides a core platform service used by many businesses to reach customers, and has held this position for a while, the Commission labels them a gatekeeper.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** An undertaking shall be designated as a gatekeeper if: (a) it has a significant impact on the internal market; (b) it provides a core platform service which is an important gateway for business users to reach end users; and (c) it enjoys an entrenched and durable position.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 3, Reg. (EU) 2022/1925.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 2 (Definitions), Art. 4 (Review of status).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Identifying whether a client's counterparty is subject to the stringent DMA rules.

### Article 5: Obligations for gatekeepers (Self-executing)
*   **Plain-English Summary:** A list of "do's and don'ts" that gatekeepers must follow immediately. For example, they can't force business users to use their payment system, and they can't mix data from different services without user consent.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Gatekeepers shall comply with obligations including: (a) not combining personal data from core platform services with data from other services without consent; (b) allowing business users to offer same products to end users through third-party services at different prices; (c) allowing business users to promote offers to end users acquired outside the platform.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 5, Reg. (EU) 2022/1925.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 8 (Compliance), Art. 13 (Anti-circumvention).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Direct basis for litigation or complaints when a tech giant engages in "steering" prohibitions or data bundling.

### Article 6: Obligations for gatekeepers susceptible of being further specified
*   **Plain-English Summary:** More complex obligations where the Commission might need to give specific instructions. Includes things like letting users uninstall pre-installed apps, not favoring their own services in rankings (self-preferencing), and allowing third-party app stores.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Gatekeepers shall: (a) not use non-public data of business users to compete against them; (b) allow end users to uninstall any software applications; (c) not treat their own services more favorably in ranking compared to similar services of third parties (anti-self-preferencing).
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 6, Reg. (EU) 2022/1925.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 7 (Interoperability), Art. 8 (Compliance).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Challenging unfair "buy-box" or ranking algorithms that favor the gatekeeper's own products.

### Article 7: Interoperability of number-independent interpersonal communications services
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Big messaging apps (like WhatsApp or Messenger) must, if asked, make their basic features work with smaller messaging apps so users can send messages across different platforms.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** Where a gatekeeper provides number-independent interpersonal communications services, it shall make the basic functionalities of such services interoperable with the services of another provider... upon request and free of charge.
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 7, Reg. (EU) 2022/1925.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 6(7) (Hardware/Software interoperability).
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** Supporting smaller tech startups in gaining access to the network effects of dominant platforms.

### Article 13: Anti-circumvention
*   **Plain-English Summary:** Gatekeepers can't try to get around the rules by using technical tricks, confusing contracts, or making it hard for users to exercise their rights.
*   **Statutory Text (Summary):** An undertaking providing core platform services shall not segment, divide, subdivide, fragment or split those services through contractual, commercial, technical or any other means in order to circumvent the thresholds laid down in Article 3(2).
*   **Citation Format:** Art. 13, Reg. (EU) 2022/1925.
*   **Cross-references:** Art. 5, Art. 6.
*   **Practical use for solo advocate:** "Catch-all" provision to attack creative corporate restructuring or technical obfuscation designed to avoid DMA compliance.
