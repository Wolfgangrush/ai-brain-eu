# GDPR — Data Breach Notification to Supervisory Authority

## Source URL
- [GDPR Article 33](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32016R0679#d1e3424-1-1)
- [EDPB Guidelines 9/2022 on personal data breach notification](https://edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-92022-personal-data-breach-notification-under-gdpr_en)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under GDPR Art 33 and EDPB Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Controllers must notify the Supervisory Authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach, unless it is unlikely to result in a risk to individuals.

## Template Type
Compliance / Emergency Response Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Administrative Information**
   - Controller/Representative details.
   - Data Protection Officer (DPO) contact point.
2. **Nature of the Breach**
   - Description of the incident (e.g., cyberattack, misdelivery, loss of device).
   - Categories and approximate number of data subjects (e.g., 500 customers, 10 employees).
   - Categories and approximate number of personal data records (e.g., 2,000 credit card records).
3. **Timing and Awareness**
   - Date/Time of occurrence and discovery.
   - Justification for any delay beyond the 72-hour window.
4. **Likely Consequences**
   - Risk assessment for the rights and freedoms of individuals (e.g., identity theft, financial loss).
   - Severity level (Low/Medium/High Risk).
5. **Mitigation and Preventative Measures**
   - Measures taken to address the breach and limit its effects.
   - Measures proposed to prevent future recurrence.
6. **Notification to Data Subjects**
   - Confirmation if individuals have been or will be notified (Mandatory if "High Risk" under Art 34).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Phased Notification**: If full details are unavailable within 72 hours, provide the information in phases (Art 33(4))—do not wait for a complete investigation before the first filing.
- **The "Awareness" Trigger**: Awareness is defined as having a "reasonable degree of certainty" that a security incident has compromised data.
- **Processor Duty**: Processors must notify the controller "without undue delay"; the controller's 72-hour clock starts upon receipt of the processor's notice.
- **Non-EU Controllers**: Must notify every Supervisory Authority where affected data subjects reside; the "One-Stop-Shop" does not apply.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"In the case of a personal data breach, the controller shall without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after having become aware of it, notify the personal data breach to the supervisory authority... The notification shall at least: (a) describe the nature of the personal data breach... (b) communicate the name and contact details of the DPO... (c) describe the likely consequences... (d) describe the measures taken." (GDPR Art 33).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The critical "Triage" form for any client experiencing a security incident. Solo advocates use this template to ensure the 72-hour regulatory window is hit, even if only a "Preliminary Notification" is possible, thereby avoiding massive fines for procedural non-compliance.
