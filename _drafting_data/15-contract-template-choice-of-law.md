# Rome I — Choice-of-Law Clause Boilerplate

## Source URL
[Regulation (EC) No 593/2008 (Rome I)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32008R0593)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory regulation governing the law applicable to contractual obligations in the EU.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. This Regulation applies to all Member States (except Denmark, which has a specific opt-out/opt-in arrangement).

## Template Type
Contract / Boilerplate Clause

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Express Choice of Law Statement**
   - Direct statement that the contract is governed by the laws of a specific jurisdiction.
2. **Scope of Application**
   - Explicit inclusion of non-contractual disputes or claims (to align with Rome II).
   - Inclusion of formation, validity, and interpretation.
3. **Reference to Specific Regulation**
   - Optional but common to cite Rome I for legal certainty in cross-border contexts.
4. **Drafting Variation for "Weaker Parties"**
   - Consumer Contracts: Qualification that the choice does not deprive the consumer of mandatory protections in their home country (Art 6).
   - Employment Contracts: Qualification regarding mandatory labor laws of the country where work is habitually performed (Art 8).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Autonomy Principle (Art 3)**: Parties are free to choose the law of any country, even a non-EU one, provided it is an express choice.
- **Combined Scope**: Always include the phrase *"including non-contractual disputes or claims"* to ensure the same law applies to tortious claims related to the contract (e.g., misrepresentation).
- **Mandatory Rules (Art 3(3))**: Be aware that if all elements of the contract are located in one Member State, the choice of a foreign law cannot override that State's mandatory rules.
- **Exclusions (Art 1)**: Rome I does not apply to family law, wills, or company law (e.g., the internal management of a legal person).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"A contract shall be governed by the law chosen by the parties. The choice shall be made expressly or clearly demonstrated by the terms of the contract or the circumstances of the case. By their choice the parties can select the law applicable to the whole or to part only of the contract." (Rome I Regulation, Article 3(1)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The most common clause in any cross-border EU commercial contract. Solo advocates use this template to prevent "forum shopping" and to ensure that any dispute—whether based on the contract or a related tort—is resolved under a single, predictable legal system.
