# Franchise Agreement — VBER 2022/720 Compliance

2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — Commission Regulation + Vertical Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
VBER 2022/720 entered into force 1 June 2022 (transitional period to 31 May 2023 for pre-existing agreements). Key changes from prior VBER 330/2010: dual distribution clarified · online sales restrictions tightened · most favoured nation (MFN) clauses for online platforms restricted · sustainability agreements clarified. Block exemption applies where both supplier's and buyer's market share is below 30%.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Franchisor + Franchisee (legal entities + connected parties).
2. **Recitals** — Franchisor's system, brand, know-how, IP rights.
3. **Definitions** — "System" · "Marks" · "Know-How" · "Territory" · "Initial Fee" · "Royalty" · "Marketing Fund".
4. **Grant of Rights**
   - License to use Marks + Know-How within the Territory.
   - Right to operate the franchised business at the Franchised Location(s).
   - Exclusive / sole / non-exclusive territorial structure.
5. **Franchisee Obligations**
   - Operate strictly within the System Manual.
   - Pay Initial Fee + ongoing Royalty + Marketing Fund contributions.
   - Maintain quality standards · attend training.
   - Confidentiality of Know-How.
6. **Franchisor Obligations**
   - Provide initial and ongoing training.
   - Supply System Manual + updates.
   - Provide marketing materials + national advertising fund deployment.
   - Maintain and enforce the Marks.
7. **Restrictive Clauses — VBER Compliance**
   - **Resale price maintenance**: only recommended / maximum prices (NOT minimum or fixed).
   - **Territorial restrictions**: passive sales restrictions are hardcore (Art 4 VBER); active sales restrictions to exclusive territories are permitted.
   - **Customer restrictions**: comply with Art 4 hardcore list.
   - **Online sales**: cannot prohibit; restrictions on online ads / online platforms must be proportionate.
   - **Cross-supplies between franchisees**: cannot be prohibited.
8. **IP Protection**
   - Marks registered with EUIPO + national offices.
   - Know-How definition (Art 1(g) VBER) — substantial, secret, identified.
9. **Non-Compete (during agreement and post-term)**
   - During: permitted if duration ≤ 5 years (Art 5(1)(a) VBER).
   - Post-term: max 1 year, limited to premises used during franchise, indispensable to protect know-how (Art 5(1)(b)).
10. **Term and Termination**
    - Initial term + renewal mechanism.
    - Termination for cause · convenience (with notice).
    - Effects of termination — return of materials · de-identification of premises.
11. **Goodwill / Compensation on Termination** — Member State law may impose obligations (FR Loi Doubin · DE BGB §89b analogous application).
12. **Dispute Resolution** — Member State court (Brussels Ia) or arbitration · mediation step.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Pre-contractual disclosure** required in many Member States (FR Loi Doubin · ES Real Decreto 201/2010 · IT L. 129/2004 · BE Loi 19 December 2005).
- **Market share threshold** check before relying on VBER (combined or unilateral).
- **Hardcore restrictions** (Art 4) — even one removes block exemption for the entire agreement.
- **Dual distribution** — VBER 2022/720 narrowed dual distribution carve-out.
- **Sustainability clauses** — Guidelines now address; carbon-emission standards can be imposed on franchisees.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Pursuant to Article 101(3) of the Treaty and subject to the provisions of this Regulation, it is hereby declared that Article 101(1) of the Treaty shall not apply to vertical agreements. This exemption shall apply to the extent that such agreements contain vertical restraints." (Regulation 2022/720, Art 2(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Franchise litigation focuses on (a) pre-contractual disclosure failures (especially in FR · IT · ES) and (b) post-termination non-compete enforceability. Solo practitioners drafting franchise agreements must pressure-test every restrictive provision against VBER Art 4 hardcore restrictions — a single non-compliant clause forfeits the block exemption and exposes the whole agreement to Art 101 TFEU scrutiny.
