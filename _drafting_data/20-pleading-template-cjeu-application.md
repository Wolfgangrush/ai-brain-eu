# CJEU Direct Action — Application Template (Art 263 TFEU)

## Source URL
- [Rules of Procedure of the General Court](https://curia.europa.eu/jcms/jcms/Jo2_7040/)
- [Practice Rules for the Implementation of the Rules of Procedure of the General Court](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32015H0520(02))

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Rules of Procedure and Practice Directions of the CJEU.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Note that since 2018, the use of **e-Curia** is mandatory for all exchanges of pleadings between the parties and the General Court.

## Template Type
Pleading / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header**
   - Designation of the Court (e.g., General Court of the European Union).
   - Names and addresses of the parties (Applicant vs. Defendant Institution).
   - Name and Bar status of the Applicant's lawyer.
2. **Subject-Matter of the Dispute**
   - Concise statement identifying the contested act (e.g., "Application for annulment of Commission Decision...").
3. **The Parties**
   - Detailed identification of the Applicant and the Defendant Institution.
4. **Admissibility**
   - Locus Standi (Standing): Direct and individual concern (for non-privileged applicants).
   - Time-limit: Calculation showing the application is within the 2-month limit (+10 days for distance).
5. **Statement of Facts**
   - Numbered paragraphs detailing the chronological background.
6. **Pleas in Law and Main Arguments**
   - Specific grounds under Art 263 TFEU:
     - Lack of competence.
     - Infringement of an essential procedural requirement.
     - Infringement of the Treaties/rule of law.
     - Misuse of powers.
7. **Evidence Offered**
   - Summary list of annexed documents.
8. **Form of Order Sought**
   - Precise request: "Annul the contested decision," "Order the defendant to pay the costs."
9. **Signature** (by a lawyer authorized to practice in a Member State).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Paragraph Numbering**: Mandatory for every paragraph in the application.
- **Page Limits**: Maximum **30 pages** for the General Court (unless special authorization).
- **Formatting**: A4, 1.5 line spacing, Times New Roman 12pt, margins 2.5cm.
- **e-Curia**: Applications must be uploaded via e-Curia; physical signatures are only required if e-Curia is unavailable.
- **Language**: One of the 24 official languages becomes the "language of the case."

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The application... shall contain: (a) the name and address of the applicant; (b) the designation of the party against whom the application is made; (c) the subject-matter of the proceedings; (d) the form of order sought by the applicant; (e) a summary of the pleas in law on which the application is based; (f) where appropriate, the nature of any evidence offered." (Rules of Procedure of the General Court, Article 76).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary vehicle for challenging EU regulatory decisions (e.g., competition fines, state aid decisions, data protection adequacy findings). Solo advocates must follow these formal rules strictly to avoid immediate rejection by the Registry.
