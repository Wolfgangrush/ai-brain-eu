# ICT Third-Party Provider Contract — DORA (Regulation 2022/2554)

## Source URL

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable since 17 January 2025).

## Currency Warning
DORA applies from **17 January 2025** to financial entities (credit institutions · payment institutions · investment firms · CASPs · insurance · pension · etc.) and ICT third-party service providers. Critical ICT third-party service providers (CTPPs) designated by ESAs and subject to direct EU supervision.

## Template Type
Contract Template (Financial Entity ↔ ICT Service Provider)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties**
   - Financial Entity (FE) — regulated status + competent authority.
   - ICT Third-Party Service Provider (TPP) — establishment + CTPP status if applicable.
2. **Services Description (Art 30(2) DORA — Mandatory Contractual Elements)**
   - **Clear and complete description** of the ICT services.
   - Whether services are critical or important functions of the FE.
   - Locations where data is processed and stored (EU/EEA preferred).
3. **Service Levels**
   - Quantitative + qualitative performance targets.
   - Definitions of "incident" · "major incident" · "downtime".
   - SLAs measurable + auditable.
   - Remediation: service credits · step-in · termination triggers.
4. **Information and Reporting**
   - Provider's reporting obligations — performance · incidents · vulnerabilities.
   - Cooperation with the FE during testing of operational resilience.
5. **Security and Confidentiality**
   - Confidentiality of FE data + customer data.
   - Encryption standards (in transit + at rest).
   - Access controls + identity management.
   - Compliance with NIS2 where TPP is in NIS2 scope.
6. **Data Protection**
   - GDPR DPA Art 28 if personal data involved.
   - International transfer mechanism (SCCs).
   - Chapter VII Data Act safeguards for non-personal data.
7. **Right to Audit (Art 30(3))**
   - Pooled audits permitted between multiple FEs (cost-saving).
   - Audit by competent authorities directly.
   - Annual audit minimum + ad hoc audits triggered by incidents.
8. **Incident Reporting**
   - Major ICT-related incident — TPP must notify FE within agreed timeframe (typically 4-24 hours).
   - FE reports to competent authority per Art 19 DORA.
   - Joint root-cause analysis.
9. **Sub-contracting (Art 30(3)(f))**
   - Sub-outsourcing requires prior FE consent for ICT services supporting critical or important functions.
   - Sub-contractor must meet the same security and resilience requirements.
   - Chain-transparency — FE entitled to know the full sub-outsourcing chain.
10. **Termination Rights (Art 28)**
    - FE's mandatory exit rights where:
      - Material breach by TPP.
      - Circumstances impair TPP's performance.
      - TPP weaknesses identified by competent authority.
      - Inability to perform supervised testing.
    - Transition plan + reasonable transition period.
11. **Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery** — RPO / RTO targets · regular testing.
12. **Insurance and Liability**
    - Cyber liability insurance minimum.
    - Caps on liability + carve-outs (gross negligence · wilful misconduct · IP infringement · data breaches).
13. **CTPP-Specific Provisions** (where TPP designated critical)
    - ESAs supervisory access.
    - Recommendations from Lead Overseer — TPP must address.
    - FE's ability to terminate if Lead Overseer issues recommendations and TPP fails to remediate.
14. **Governing Law and Forum** — Rome I + Brussels Ia · arbitration alternative.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Art 30(2) mandatory contractual elements** — all 9 must be in the contract; otherwise FE breaches DORA.
- **CTPP designation** — verify TPP status with ESAs register.
- **Annual re-certification** of TPP's security posture.
- **Pooled audit clauses** — increasingly used by mid-tier FEs to share costs.
- **Step-in rights** — FE's right to take operational control on TPP failure.
- **Exit plan documentation** at contract inception — not at termination.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The rights and obligations of the financial entity and of the ICT third-party service provider shall be clearly allocated and set out in writing. The full contract shall include the service level agreements and be documented in one written document which shall be available to the parties on paper, in a downloadable and easily accessible document, or in another durable medium." (Regulation 2022/2554, Art 30(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
DORA significantly expanded the regulatory obligations on FE-TPP contracts. Solo practitioners advising FE-side (often payment institutions and CASPs) must audit existing ICT contracts against Art 30(2) elements; provider-side counsel must brace for pooled audits + ESAs direct supervision (CTPPs). Exit plans documented at inception are the differentiator between compliant and non-compliant agreements.
