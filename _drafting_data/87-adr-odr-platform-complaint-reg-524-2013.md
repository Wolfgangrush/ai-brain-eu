## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (ODR platform) + Directive (ADR bodies).

## Currency Warning
**ODR Regulation 524/2013 was repealed effective 20 July 2025 by Regulation 2024/3228** — the centralised EU ODR platform shut down. ADR Directive 2013/11/EU continues to apply; consumers must now approach certified national ADR bodies directly. This template retains the structural framework for ADR complaints to certified national entities. Practitioners must verify the current applicable national ADR entity for the specific sector.

## Template Type
Consumer Complaint / ADR Submission

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Certified ADR Entity Identified**
   - Name + sector competence + Member State.
   - Verify entity is on the national list pursuant to Directive 2013/11/EU.
2. **Parties**
   - Consumer: name · address · contact details · Member State of residence.
   - Trader: business name · registered address · website · Member State of establishment.
3. **Cross-Border Element**
   - Consumer's habitual residence vs trader's establishment.
   - Online or offline transaction.
4. **Contract Identification**
   - Order number / contract reference.
   - Date of contract.
   - Goods / services purchased.
   - Price + payment method.
5. **Statement of the Dispute**
   - Chronological narrative.
   - Specific consumer-protection provision allegedly breached (Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU · Sale of Goods Directive (EU) 2019/771 · Digital Content Directive (EU) 2019/770 · Unfair Commercial Practices Directive 2005/29/EC · Misleading Comparative Advertising Directive 2006/114/EC).
6. **Prior Communication with the Trader**
   - Date of complaint to trader.
   - Trader's response (or absence).
7. **Remedy Sought**
   - Refund / repair / replacement / price reduction / damages.
   - Quantified amount + currency.
8. **Annexes** — Order confirmation · invoice · correspondence · evidence of defect / non-conformity.
9. **Declaration** — Consumer's signature + date · authority to act if filed through representative.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Use the certified ADR entity's required form** — most have online portals.
- **Language** — the ADR entity must accept submissions in the consumer's language and the trader's language; binding outcomes typically in the trader's language.
- **Time-bar** — most ADR procedures require complaint within 12 months of the consumer's complaint to trader.
- **Cost** — free or nominal for the consumer in nearly all Member States.
- **Outcome** — recommendation (non-binding) or decision (binding subject to consumer's consent, depending on Member State).
- **Court fallback** — ADR does not preclude court proceedings under Brussels Ia / Rome I.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Regulation aims to contribute to the proper functioning of the internal market, in particular its digital dimension, by providing a European ODR platform... facilitating the independent, impartial, transparent, effective, fast and fair out-of-court resolution of disputes between consumers and traders online." (Regulation 524/2013, Art 1 — pre-repeal text retained for structural reference.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Post-ODR-platform repeal, solo practitioners advising consumer-side clients now route complaints through certified national ADR entities (FOS in some sectors · sector-specific ombudsmen). Trader-side counsel must ensure their websites still display ADR entity information per Directive 2013/11/EU Art 13 — failure remains a compliance risk under unfair commercial practices law.
