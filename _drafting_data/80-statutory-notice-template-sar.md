# GDPR — Subject Access Request (SAR) Response Template

## Source URL
- [GDPR Article 15](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32016R0679#d1e2501-1-1)
- [EDPB Guidelines 01/2022 on data subject rights - Right of access](https://edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-012022-data-subject-rights-right-access_en)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under GDPR Art 15 and EDPB Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. The response must be provided within **one month** of receipt, extendable by two months for complex cases.

## Template Type
Notice / Regulatory Communication

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction & Confirmation**
   - Statement confirming whether personal data is being processed.
   - Reference to the identity verification process completed.
2. **The Personal Data (The "Copy")**
   - Provision of the actual personal data in an intelligible form.
   - Explanation of any codes or technical logs included.
3. **Mandatory Processing Information (Art 15(1) a-h)**
   - Purposes of processing.
   - Categories of personal data concerned.
   - Recipients or categories of recipients (including those in third countries).
   - Envisaged retention period (or criteria for determination).
   - Data subject's rights (Rectification, Erasure, Restriction, Objection).
   - Right to lodge a complaint with a Supervisory Authority.
   - Source of the data (if not collected from the subject).
   - Existence of automated decision-making/profiling, including logic and consequences.
4. **International Transfer Safeguards (Art 15(2))**
   - Information on appropriate safeguards (e.g., SCCs) if data is transferred outside the EEA.
5. **Redactions and Limitations**
   - Justification for any redactions (e.g., to protect the rights and freedoms of others under Art 15(4)).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Human-Readability**: Information must be provided in a "concise, transparent, intelligible and easily accessible form."
- **Specific vs. General**: The response must be tailored to the individual requestor and their specific data, not just a link to the general privacy policy.
- **Form of Data**: If the request is made electronically, the information must be provided in a commonly used electronic format.
- **No Charge**: The first copy must be provided free of charge (reasonable fees only for further copies).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning him or her are being processed, and, where that is the case, access to the personal data and the following information... The controller shall provide a copy of the personal data undergoing processing." (GDPR Art 15).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "standard response" for any client-facing business. Solo advocates use this to ensure that a client's response to a SAR is legally complete, as incomplete SAR responses are one of the most common grounds for individual complaints to Data Protection Authorities.
