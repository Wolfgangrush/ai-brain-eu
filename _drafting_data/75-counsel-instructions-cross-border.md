# Counsel Instructions — Cross-Border / Member State Litigation

## Source URL
- [CCBE Code of Conduct for European Lawyers](https://www.ccbe.eu/documents/professional-regulations/code-of-conduct/)
- [Directive 77/249/EEC (lawyers' freedom to provide services)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:31977L0249)
- [Directive 98/5/EC (lawyers' establishment)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:31998L0005)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — CCBE Code of Conduct (binding via national bar implementation) + EU lawyers' services directives.

## Currency Warning
Where instructing a lawyer in a different Member State, CCBE Code Art 5.2 requires the instructing lawyer to verify the receiving lawyer's competence before entrusting the matter. Honoraria arrangements (Art 5.7) must be confirmed in writing at the outset.

## Template Type
Letter / Brief to Counsel

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header**
   - Instructing lawyer's firm letterhead (or chambers).
   - Date.
   - Receiving lawyer's firm + Member State Bar.
   - Re: short description of the matter.
2. **Conflict Check Request**
   - Names of all parties + connected entities.
   - Request that the receiving lawyer confirm no conflict before commencing work.
3. **Identification of the Client**
   - Client name · contact details · jurisdiction · regulatory status (if any).
   - Authority to instruct (resolution · power of attorney · engagement letter referenced).
4. **Matter Description**
   - Nature of the dispute or transaction.
   - Forum (Member State court · CJEU · arbitration · regulatory authority).
   - Key dates + deadlines (limitation · response deadlines · hearing dates).
5. **Specific Instructions**
   - Scope of mandate (advice · pleading · representation · hearing).
   - Permissions and limits (settlement authority · cost ceilings).
   - Documents to be drafted.
   - Documents to be reviewed.
6. **Materials Enclosed / Linked**
   - Schedule of attached evidence + previous correspondence + opinions.
   - Existing pleadings (if any).
   - Privileged-document log if disclosure is in scope.
7. **Fees and Disbursements**
   - Hourly rate · fixed fee · capped fee · success fee arrangement.
   - VAT treatment (cross-border B2B reverse charge).
   - Disbursement authorisation (court fees · expert fees · translation).
   - Billing cadence + payment terms.
8. **Communications and Reporting**
   - Reporting frequency.
   - Whether direct client contact is authorised.
   - Privileged communications confirmed under CCBE Code Art 5.3.
9. **Conflict-of-laws Note**
   - Governing law of the retainer.
   - Forum for any retainer dispute.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Written confirmation of instructions** is mandatory under CCBE Art 5.7.
- **Direct correspondence with the other side's lawyer** routed through receiving lawyer unless explicitly authorised.
- **CCBE professional-secrecy principle** (Art 2.3) — extended privilege applies between EU lawyers.
- **Honoraria-sharing** (Art 5.7) — permissible between EU lawyers; must be confirmed in writing.
- **Translation** — instructions and key documents should be in the working language of the receiving lawyer's bar.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A lawyer in one Member State has a right of free establishment and a right to provide services in another Member State; subject to the rules and ethical principles applicable in the host state, including those concerning the professional conduct of lawyers." (CCBE Code of Conduct, Art 1.4.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Solo practitioners frequently brief Member State counsel for litigation outside their home jurisdiction (e.g., a Spanish solo briefing German counsel for a competition case before the Bundesgerichtshof). The CCBE Code provides a binding cross-border ethical framework. Disputes most often arise over fee splits and reporting cadence — front-load both in the initial instruction letter.
