## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable in all Member States except Denmark).

## Currency Warning
The Recast Regulation applies to insolvency proceedings opened after 26 June 2017. The Regulation covers **main, secondary, and territorial proceedings**. Annex A lists the qualifying national procedures (administration · liquidation · restructuring · pre-insolvency frameworks under Directive (EU) 2019/1023).

## Template Type
Procedural Application / Cross-Border Coordination

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identification of the Debtor**
   - Name / corporate name + registered seat + COMI (Centre of Main Interests).
   - VAT number / company-house identifier.
2. **COMI Analysis (Art 3(1))**
   - Presumption: registered office for companies / habitual residence for individuals.
   - Rebuttal: where third parties can ascertain a different centre.
   - **3-month look-back** for company / **6 months** for self-employed / **6 months** for individual — to prevent forum shopping.
3. **Type of Proceedings Sought**
   - One of the procedures listed in Annex A.
   - For pre-insolvency restructuring (Directive 2019/1023 implementation), check Annex A inclusion.
4. **Universal Effect of Main Proceedings (Art 20)**
   - Effects extend to all Member States with no further formality.
   - Recognition without exequatur (Art 19).
5. **Insolvency Practitioner Appointed**
   - Powers under Art 21 to act in other Member States.
   - Registration of judgment in foreign land registers / commercial registers permitted.
6. **Creditor Information (Art 54)**
   - Standard Form (Annex II of Reg 2017/1105) for notifying foreign creditors.
   - 30-day minimum claim-filing period.
   - Language: official language of the proceedings + summary in EU language understood by creditor.
7. **Group Insolvency Coordination (Chapter V)**
   - Where multiple group entities are subject to proceedings in different Member States.
   - Coordination proceedings; coordinator's role; coordination plan.
8. **Public Register Entry (Art 24)**
   - Mandatory entry in the Member State's interconnected insolvency register.
9. **Annexes**
   - Standard Form Annex I — claim by foreign creditor.
   - Standard Form Annex II — notice of proceedings to creditors.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Annex I / Annex II standard forms** are mandatory for creditor claims + notifications.
- **COMI is the gateway** — opening of proceedings in the wrong Member State invalidates universality.
- **Translation** — standard forms available in all 24 official languages.
- **Interconnected insolvency registers** searchable via the e-Justice portal (Art 25).
- **Secondary proceedings** — see file 101.
- **Avoiding actions** — preserved under Art 16 (lex fori concursus governs, with carve-outs).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The courts of the Member State within the territory of which the centre of the debtor's main interests is situated shall have jurisdiction to open insolvency proceedings (main insolvency proceedings). The centre of main interests shall be the place where the debtor conducts the administration of its interests on a regular basis and which is ascertainable by third parties." (Regulation 2015/848, Art 3(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
COMI determination is the principal contested issue in cross-border insolvency. Solo practitioners representing creditors or directors must front-load COMI evidence (registered office address · place of management · creditor-facing communications · banking arrangements). Forum-shopping concerns (UK · NL · IE) drove the 3-month look-back; practitioners must track relocations.
