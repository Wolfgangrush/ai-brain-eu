# CJEU / General Court — Defence, Reply, and Rejoinder

- [Rules of Procedure of the General Court — Articles 81-83](https://curia.europa.eu/jcms/jcms/Jo2_7040/)
- [Practice Rules for the Implementation of the Rules of Procedure of the General Court](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32015H0520(02))

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — Rules of Procedure governing the written stage of proceedings.

## Currency Warning
Time-limits run from service of the preceding pleading via e-Curia: Defence — 2 months (+10 days for distance). Reply and Rejoinder are no longer automatic; the President of the chamber decides whether a second exchange is necessary.

## Template Type
Pleading / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
**Defence**
1. **Header** — Court designation · Defendant identification · authorised lawyer.
2. **Subject-Matter of the Dispute** — Brief restatement of the application.
3. **Plea of Inadmissibility (if any)** — Separate or with substance; may be raised at admissibility stage under Art 130.
4. **Statement of Facts** — Accept / contest each paragraph of the applicant's facts.
5. **Pleas in Law (Substance)** — Address each of the applicant's pleas; raise any counter-arguments.
6. **Form of Order Sought** — Dismiss the application + order applicant to pay costs.
7. **Evidence Offered** — Annexes.
8. **Signature**.

**Reply (applicant)**
- Confined to **new matters** raised in the Defence; cannot reopen pleas already pleaded.
- Address objections to admissibility.
- Address evidentiary points raised in defence.

**Rejoinder (defendant)**
- Confined to **new matters** raised in the Reply.
- Cannot enlarge the dispute.

## Drafting Conventions
- **25-page limit** for Defence (General Court); Reply and Rejoinder 15 pages each.
- **Paragraph numbering** continuous within each pleading.
- **e-Curia** filing for all three exchanges.
- **No new pleas** in Reply or Rejoinder unless based on matters of law or fact coming to light during proceedings (Art 84 Rules of Procedure).
- Reply / Rejoinder require leave of the President of the chamber where written procedure is being expedited.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The application may be supplemented by a reply from the applicant and a rejoinder from the defendant... The President shall set the time-limits within which those pleadings are to be lodged." (Rules of Procedure of the General Court, Art 83.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The Reply is the applicant's single chance to address admissibility plea or new evidentiary documents annexed by the Commission. Practitioners must triage which Defence points genuinely require Reply — over-replying risks the chamber refusing the exchange and converting strong points into "matters already pleaded."
