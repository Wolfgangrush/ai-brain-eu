# EU AI Act — Article 50 Transparency Notice Template

## Source URL
[EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) — Article 50](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32024R1689#d1e4348-1-1)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under the EU AI Act.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. These obligations apply to all providers and deployers of relevant AI systems in the EU from **August 2, 2026**.

## Template Type
Compliance / User Interface Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Chatbot / Interaction Disclosure**
   - Timing: At the latest at the time of the first interaction.
   - Content: "You are currently interacting with an AI system [Name/Model]."
   - Accessibility: Clearly visible, distinguishable from other content.
2. **Synthetic Content Marking (Technical Metadata)**
   - Requirement: Machine-readable marking (Watermarking/Metadata) detecting the content as artificially generated.
   - Standard: Interoperable and robust technical solution.
3. **Deepfake Disclosure (Image/Audio/Video)**
   - Content: "This content has been artificially generated or manipulated."
   - Placement: On the content itself or in immediate proximity (e.g., a permanent label).
   - Variation: Artistic/Satirical works may use less intrusive labeling that does not "unduly impair" the work.
4. **Public Interest Text Disclosure**
   - Content: Disclosure of artificial origin for text informing the public on matters of public interest.
   - Exception: Not required if the text has undergone human review/editorial control.
5. **Emotion Recognition / Biometric Categorisation**
   - Content: Disclosure to individuals exposed to such systems (as per Art 50(3)).

## Drafting Conventions
- **The "Obviousness" Test**: Disclosure is not required for chatbots if it is "obvious from the context" that the user is interacting with AI. However, for legal certainty, explicit labeling is recommended.
- **Machine-Readability**: Synthetic content must be marked in a way that other AI systems or search engines can detect (using open standards where possible).
- **Persistence**: For deepfakes, the disclosure must be "persistent" and difficult to remove.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Providers shall ensure that AI systems intended to interact with natural persons are designed and developed in such a way that the natural persons concerned are informed that they are interacting with an AI system... Deployers of an AI system that generates or manipulates image, audio or video content that appreciably resembles existing persons... shall disclose that the content has been artificially generated or manipulated." (AI Act Art 50).
