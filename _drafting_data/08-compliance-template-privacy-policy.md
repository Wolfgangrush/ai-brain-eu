# GDPR — Transparency Notice (Privacy Policy) Template

## Source URL
- [GDPR Articles 13 & 14](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32016R0679#d1e2335-1-1)
- [EDPB Guidelines on Transparency (WP260 rev.01)](https://edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-transparency-under-regulation-2016679_en)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under GDPR and authoritative EDPB Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. The information must be provided in a "concise, transparent, intelligible and easily accessible form." Layered notices are highly recommended for digital services.

## Template Type
Compliance / Disclosure Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identity and Contact Details**
   - Name and contact info of the Controller.
   - Contact details of the Data Protection Officer (DPO).
   - Details of the EU Representative (for non-EU controllers).
2. **Purposes and Legal Basis of Processing**
   - Specific description of each processing activity and its purpose.
   - Corresponding legal basis (Art 6 or Art 9).
   - If using "Legitimate Interests," a description of those interests.
3. **Categories of Personal Data (Art 14 Only)**
   - Description of the types of data obtained from third parties.
4. **Recipients of Personal Data**
   - Identification of recipients or categories of recipients (e.g., cloud providers, marketing partners).
5. **International Transfers**
   - Information on transfers to third countries and the specific safeguards (e.g., Adequacy, SCCs, BCRs).
6. **Retention Periods**
   - Specific time limits for storage or the criteria used to determine those limits.
7. **Data Subject Rights**
   - Right to Access, Rectification, Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"), Restriction, Objection, and Portability.
   - Right to withdraw consent at any time.
   - Right to lodge a complaint with a Supervisory Authority.
8. **Statutory/Contractual Requirement (Art 13 Only)**
   - Whether the provision of data is a statutory or contractual requirement and the consequences of failure to provide it.
9. **Automated Decision-Making and Profiling**
   - Existence of such systems, the logic involved, and the significance/consequences for the individual.
10. **Source of Data (Art 14 Only)**
    - Where the data originated if not collected directly from the individual.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Layered Approach**: Use a "First Layer" summary for key points and a "Second Layer" for full legal details.
- **Definitive Language**: Avoid "may" or "might"; use "we will" or "we do."
- **Just-in-Time Notices**: Provide specific snippets of the policy at the exact moment data is collected (e.g., on a sign-up form).
- **Multi-Device Optimization**: Ensure the policy is readable on mobile devices.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The controller shall take appropriate measures to provide any information referred to in Articles 13 and 14... in a concise, transparent, intelligible and easily accessible form, using clear and plain language... The information shall be provided in writing, or by other means, including, where appropriate, by electronic means." (GDPR Art 12(1)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The most visible compliance asset for any client. Solo advocates use this template to audit a client's website or app, ensuring it meets the high "User-Centric" transparency standards of the EDPB and avoids the common pitfall of using generic, non-specific "boilerplate" policies.
