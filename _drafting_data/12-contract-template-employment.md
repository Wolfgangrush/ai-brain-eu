# EU Employment Information — Standard Particulars Template

## Source URL
[Transparent and Predictable Working Conditions Directive (EU) 2019/1152](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32019L1152)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory directive governing the minimum information for all EU employment relationships.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. This Directive replaced the old 91/533/EEC Directive. Most Member States transposed this by August 2022. Key information must be provided within **7 days** of start of employment.

## Template Type
Contract / Employment

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identification of Parties**
   - Employer and Employee details.
2. **Place of Work**
   - Fixed location or statement of various locations/flexibility.
3. **Job Description**
   - Title, grade, nature or category of work, or a brief description.
4. **Dates and Duration**
   - Start date of employment.
   - End date (if fixed-term).
   - Probationary period (conditions and duration - generally max 6 months).
5. **Remuneration**
   - Initial basic amount and any other component elements (bonuses, allowances).
   - Method and frequency of payment.
6. **Working Time and Predictability**
   - Standard working day/week.
   - Overtime arrangements and remuneration.
   - For unpredictable schedules (e.g., on-call):
     - Guaranteed paid hours.
     - Reference hours and days.
     - Minimum notice period for assignments.
7. **Leave and Benefits**
   - Entitlement to paid leave (or calculation method).
   - Right to training provided by the employer.
8. **Termination Procedures**
   - Notice periods for both parties.
   - Formal requirements for termination.
9. **Social Security**
   - Identity of the social security institutions receiving contributions.

## Drafting Conventions
- **The 7-Day Rule**: Essential particulars (Parties, Place, Title, Start Date, Pay, Working Time) must be provided within 7 days. Remaining information within one month.
- **Parallel Employment**: Clauses prohibiting outside work are generally invalid unless justified by objective grounds (health, safety, business secrecy).
- **Electronic Form**: Information can be provided electronically if it is accessible, storable, and printable by the employee.
- **Transposition**: The Directive must be read alongside the specific national labor code (e.g., German Nachweisgesetz).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Member States shall ensure that employers are required to inform workers of the essential aspects of the employment relationship... (a) the identity of the parties... (b) the place of work... (c) the title, grade, nature or category of work... (d) the date of commencement... (e) in the case of a fixed-term employment relationship, the end date." (Directive 2019/1152, Article 4).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The foundational document for any employment relationship. Solo advocates use this template to draft compliant employment contracts for SMEs or to challenge "unpredictable" working conditions that lack the mandatory Art 4 disclosures (e.g., missing reference hours or notice periods for on-call work).
