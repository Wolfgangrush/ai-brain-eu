2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Directive (transposed in Member State law) + Regulation 1/2003.

## Currency Warning
The Directive establishes minimum standards; implementation varies by Member State (national court applies the transposing law). Leniency statements and settlement submissions remain on the **blacklist** — courts cannot order their disclosure (Art 6(6)). Documents on the **grey list** (information prepared specifically for proceedings, withdrawn settlement submissions) are disclosable only after the Commission's proceedings are closed.

## Template Type
Procedural Application / Pre-Trial Disclosure

## Structural Skeleton
1. **National Court Identified** — Member State court hearing the damages action.
2. **Parties** — Claimant + defendant (cartelist) + (where applicable) Commission as amicus.
3. **Categories of Evidence Sought**
   - From the defendant or third party (Art 5 of the Directive).
   - From the Commission file (Art 6) — subject to blacklist / greylist / whitelist categories.
4. **Necessity and Proportionality (Art 5(3))**
   - Plausibility of the claim.
   - Scope of disclosure proportionate to the case.
   - Specifically identified categories of evidence (not fishing expeditions).
   - Confidential information protections (in camera review · redaction · confidentiality rings).
5. **Cartelist's Joint and Several Liability (Art 11)** — Pleading framework.
6. **Presumption of Harm (Art 17)** — Cartel infringements presumed to cause harm; defendant bears rebuttal burden.
7. **Quantification of Damages**
   - Court may estimate where precise quantification is practically impossible.
   - Pass-on defence (Art 13) and indirect-purchaser claims (Art 14).
8. **Limitation Period (Art 10)** — Minimum 5 years from claimant's knowledge of infringement + identity of infringer + harm; suspended during Commission investigation.
9. **Annexes** — Commission decision (if finding-based action) · economic evidence.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Identify categories**, not individual documents — proportionality is key.
- **Confidentiality protections** must be pleaded — request a confidentiality ring of named counsel and approved experts.
- **In camera review** — court may inspect contested documents before ordering disclosure.
- **Penalties for non-compliance** — adverse inferences · monetary sanctions per national law.
- **Leniency-statement firewall** — never request leniency statements; they are absolutely protected.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Member States shall ensure that, in proceedings relating to an action for damages in the Union, upon request of a claimant who has presented a reasoned justification containing reasonably available facts and evidence sufficient to support the plausibility of its claim for damages, national courts are able to order the defendant or a third party to disclose relevant evidence which lies in their control..." (Directive 2014/104, Art 5(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical for follow-on damages actions after a Commission cartel decision (e.g., trucks cartel, air cargo cartel). Solo practitioners representing cartel victims must frame disclosure requests by category (pricing data · internal cost models · sales volumes) rather than document-by-document. The confidentiality ring is essential to give the court comfort to grant disclosure.
