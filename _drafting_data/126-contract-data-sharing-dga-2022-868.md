# Data Sharing Agreement — Data Governance Act (Reg 2022/868)

## Source URL
- [Regulation (EU) 2022/868 (Data Governance Act)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022R0868)
- [Commission guidance on Data Governance Act](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/data-governance-act)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable since 24 September 2023).

## Currency Warning
DGA establishes (i) re-use rules for protected data held by public sector bodies; (ii) notification regime for **data intermediation services**; (iii) framework for **data altruism organisations** registered with national competent authorities. Data Spaces sectoral regulations (Health Data Space · Mobility Data Space) layer on top.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Data Holder + Data Recipient + (if intermediated) Data Intermediation Service Provider.
2. **Recitals** — Data scope · purpose · regulatory basis (DGA · sector regulation · contract).
3. **Definitions** — "Data" (per DGA Art 2(1)) · "Data Holder" · "Data User" · "Data Subject" · "Re-use" · "Permitted Purposes" · "Excluded Purposes".
4. **Subject-Matter of the Sharing**
   - Datasets identified (categories · schema · temporal scope).
   - Personal vs non-personal data classification.
   - IP rights subsisting in the data (sui generis database right · trade secret · copyright).
5. **Permitted Purposes**
   - Specific use-case description.
   - Aggregation / analytics permitted.
   - Resale or onward-sharing restrictions.
6. **Intermediation (if applicable)**
   - Provider's notification status with national competent authority.
   - Independence requirements (Art 12 DGA) — intermediation service provider cannot use data for own commercial purposes.
   - Structural separation requirements.
7. **Data Altruism (where applicable)**
   - Registered organisation status (Art 18 DGA).
   - Data subject consent / data holder permission documented per Common European Data Altruism Consent Form.
8. **Personal Data Compliance**
   - GDPR roles (controller-controller · controller-processor · joint controllers).
   - Legal basis for processing.
   - SCCs for international transfers.
9. **Public-Sector Re-use (where data held by public sector body)**
   - Conditions imposed by public body (non-exclusive · proportionate · transparent · non-discriminatory).
   - Fees (cost-oriented).
   - Open formats and machine-readability.
10. **Security**
    - NIS2 compliance.
    - Pseudonymisation / anonymisation obligations.
    - Cyber-incident reporting.
11. **Audit Rights** — Data Holder's right to audit Data Recipient's compliance.
12. **International Transfers** — Mechanism for non-EU recipients (adequacy decisions · SCCs · BCRs); Chapter VII Data Act non-personal data transfer safeguards if relevant.
13. **Term · Termination · Effect of Termination (data return / destruction · audit log)**.
14. **Liability and Indemnification**.
15. **Boilerplate**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Intermediation service providers** subject to DGA notification + ongoing compliance (Chapter III).
- **Sector-specific overlays** — Health Data Space Regulation, when adopted, will impose additional obligations on health data sharing.
- **Data altruism** consent form template per Art 25 — must use the Common European form when available.
- **Open standard formats** — particularly for public sector re-use.
- **No exclusivity** for public-sector data unless required for service of general interest.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Regulation lays down: (a) conditions for the re-use, within the Union, of certain categories of data held by public sector bodies; (b) a notification and supervisory framework for the provision of data intermediation services; (c) a framework for voluntary registration of entities which collect and process data made available for altruistic purposes." (Regulation 2022/868, Art 1.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Data sharing is increasingly contract-intensive. Solo practitioners advising data-rich SMEs (health-tech · fintech · mobility) need to differentiate (a) pure B2B data licensing (contract-only), (b) DGA-regulated intermediation, and (c) DGA-regulated altruism. The compliance burden differs sharply across the three categories.
