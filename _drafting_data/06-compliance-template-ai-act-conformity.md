# AI Act Conformity Assessment — High-Risk System Technical Documentation

## Source URL
[EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) — Annex IV](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32024R1689#d1e134-1-1)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under EU AI Act Annex IV.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. High-risk AI systems must comply with these documentation requirements by **August 2, 2026**.

## Template Type
Compliance / Technical Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **General Description of the AI System**
   - Identity: Name, version, provider details.
   - Intended Purpose: Specific context, data subject categories.
   - Interactions: With other software, hardware, or AI systems.
   - Market Placement: API, standalone, or embedded.
2. **Detailed Description of Components**
   - Architecture: Logic, algorithms, and data flow diagrams.
   - Data Governance: Training/validation/testing datasets (provenance, representativeness, bias mitigation).
   - Design Choices: Rationale for technical decisions and trade-offs.
   - Third-Party Components: External models or libraries used.
3. **Monitoring, Functioning, and Control**
   - Capabilities and Limitations: Accuracy levels, performance benchmarks.
   - Human Oversight: Technical measures for human intervention (Art 14).
   - Traceability: Specifications for automatic event logging (Art 12).
4. **Performance Metrics**
   - Metrics for accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity.
   - Justification for the selected metrics.
5. **Risk Management System**
   - Risk Assessment Report (Art 9).
   - Mitigation Measures: Technical and organizational.
   - Residual Risk Analysis.
6. **Lifecycle and Change Management**
   - Procedures for significant modifications.
   - Software/firmware version control.
7. **Applicable Standards**
   - List of Harmonised Standards (e.g., ISO/IEC 42001) or common specifications applied.
8. **EU Declaration of Conformity** (Copy of the formal statement).
9. **Post-Market Monitoring Plan**
   - Strategy for real-world performance tracking.
   - Incident reporting procedures.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Traceability**: Every technical decision must be linked to the risk management system or performance metrics.
- **Explainability**: Documentation should be structured to allow authorities to assess the system's compliance with fundamental rights.
- **Data Provenance**: High emphasis on the "quality" of training data to prevent algorithmic bias.
- **Continuous Update**: The documentation must be updated throughout the system's lifecycle.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The technical documentation shall contain at least the following information... (a) a general description of the AI system including... its intended purpose... the categories of natural persons and groups on which the system is intended to be used... (b) a detailed description of the elements of the AI system and of the process for its development..." (AI Act Annex IV).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical for clients developing or deploying high-risk AI (e.g., HR screening, credit scoring, legal analysis tools). Solo advocates use this to audit a client's "Technical File" before market entry to ensure it meets the rigorous standards of the AI Act.
