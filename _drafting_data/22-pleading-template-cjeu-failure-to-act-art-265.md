# CJEU / General Court — Action for Failure to Act (Art 265 TFEU)

## Source URL
- [Article 265 TFEU](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:12016E265)
- [Rules of Procedure of the General Court — Title V, Chapter 1](https://curia.europa.eu/jcms/jcms/Jo2_7040/)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — Treaty provision (TFEU) + Rules of Procedure of the General Court.

## Currency Warning
Mandatory pre-litigation step: the institution must first be "called upon to act" (formal letter); only if it does not define its position within **2 months** of that call may the action be brought, and the action itself must then be lodged within a further **2 months**. e-Curia mandatory for filing since 2018.

## Template Type
Pleading / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header**
   - Designation of the Court (General Court of the EU OR Court of Justice if Member State / institution applicant).
   - Applicant name + address + Member State + lawyer's Bar.
   - Defendant institution (Commission · Council · Parliament · ECB).
2. **Subject-Matter**
   - Concise identification of the specific act the institution has failed to adopt.
3. **Admissibility**
   - Locus standi (privileged vs non-privileged applicant per Art 265(3)).
   - Evidence of the prior "call to act" (letter date + delivery proof).
   - Two-month silence period elapsed.
   - Two-month limitation for filing the action itself.
4. **Statement of Facts**
   - Chronological account of the legal obligation invoked.
   - Correspondence with the institution prior to the formal call.
5. **Pleas in Law**
   - Identification of the specific Treaty / regulation / directive provision creating the duty to act.
   - Demonstration that the institution has discretion bounded by a duty.
   - Distinction from Art 263 (action for annulment) — here, silence/inaction is the wrong, not a positive act.
6. **Form of Order Sought**
   - Declaration that the defendant's failure to act is contrary to the Treaty.
   - Order for costs.
7. **Evidence Offered**
   - List of annexes including the formal call to act and any response (or absence thereof).
8. **Signature** of a lawyer entitled to practise before a Member State court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **30-page limit** for the General Court (Practice Rules).
- **Paragraph numbering** mandatory throughout.
- **e-Curia** filing required.
- **Language of the case**: any official EU language; applicant chooses where applicant is not a Member State.
- **Cross-pleading**: an Art 265 action may be converted to or paired with Art 263 if the institution adopts an inadequate act after the proceedings begin.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Should the European Parliament, the European Council, the Council, the Commission or the European Central Bank, in infringement of the Treaties, fail to act, the Member States and the other institutions of the Union may bring an action before the Court of Justice of the European Union to have the infringement established." (Art 265 TFEU, first paragraph.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The vehicle to compel an EU institution to act when it has unlawfully remained passive — e.g. Commission failure to rule on a state-aid complaint, ECB failure to take a supervisory decision, Council failure to adopt a budget measure. Crucial for clients in regulated industries waiting on overdue Commission action.
