## Authority Tier
Primary — Commission Regulation + Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
TTBER 316/2014 applies to technology-transfer agreements between two undertakings (license of patents · know-how · software copyright · combinations) for the production of contract products. Market-share thresholds: 20% combined for agreements between competitors; 30% each for non-competitors. **TTBER expires 30 April 2026** unless extended — practitioners must track replacement regulation pre-execution.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Licensor + Licensee.
2. **Recitals** — Existing IP rights · contract products to be produced under the licensed technology.
3. **Definitions** — "Licensed Technology" · "Contract Products" · "Territory" · "Field of Use" · "Net Sales" · "Royalty Base".
4. **Grant of License**
   - Patents (registration numbers + states).
   - Know-how (substantial, secret, identified per TTBER Art 1(1)(i)).
   - Software copyright.
   - Combinations.
   - Exclusive · sole · non-exclusive.
   - Field-of-use limitation (acceptable under TTBER provided objective and proportionate).
5. **Territorial Scope** — Specific countries · EEA · worldwide.
6. **Royalty Structure**
   - Running royalty (% of net sales · per-unit · tiered).
   - Lump-sum + running.
   - Minimum royalty obligations.
   - Royalty base definition + audit rights.
7. **Improvements (Art 5 TTBER)**
   - **Permitted**: exclusive licensee's obligation to grant non-exclusive license back of severable improvements; obligation to grant exclusive license back of non-severable improvements.
   - **Hardcore (Art 4)**: obligation to assign back severable improvements; obligation not to challenge IP validity (with carve-out for termination right where licensee challenges).
8. **Restrictions on Competitors (Art 4(1) — Hardcore List)**
   - Fixing prices when selling to third parties.
   - Limitations of output (with exception: limits on contract products licensee can produce for itself).
   - Allocation of markets or customers (with carve-outs).
   - Restrictions on licensee's ability to exploit own technology.
9. **Restrictions Between Non-Competitors (Art 4(2))**
   - More permissive: passive sales restrictions to exclusive territory permitted for limited duration.
10. **Excluded Restrictions (Art 5)** — Provisions that lose block exemption but do not contaminate the whole agreement.
11. **IP Validity Challenges (Art 5(1)(b))**
    - Licensor may reserve right to terminate if licensee challenges validity.
    - No "no-challenge" obligation otherwise.
12. **Term and Termination**
    - License term (often life of last patent + know-how confidentiality period).
    - Termination for material breach · insolvency · change of control.
13. **Boilerplate** — Confidentiality · audit · governing law · forum.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Market share verification** at execution + ongoing.
- **Hardcore list** — Art 4 distinguishes competitors vs non-competitors; characterisation is the first analytical step.
- **Pool / patent-pool considerations** — Guidelines treat pools separately.
- **Royalty obligations beyond patent expiry** — permissible if license to know-how continues, but court will scrutinise where it operates as disguised post-expiry patent extension.
- **FRAND** — Standard-essential patents subject to additional fairness/reasonableness/non-discrimination overlay (Huawei v ZTE C-170/13).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Pursuant to Article 101(3) of the Treaty... Article 101(1) of the Treaty shall not apply to technology transfer agreements. This exemption shall apply to the extent that such agreements contain restrictions of competition falling within Article 101(1)." (Regulation 316/2014, Art 2(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Technology licensing is a high-value EU practice area for solo practitioners with patent / IT backgrounds. The most common drafting trap is allocating "improvements" — getting the Art 5 grant-back wrong removes block exemption. Practitioners should also pre-flag the TTBER 2026 expiry to clients negotiating long-term licenses.
