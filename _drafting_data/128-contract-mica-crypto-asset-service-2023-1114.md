## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable).

## Currency Warning
**MiCA application dates**: Titles III + IV (stablecoins — ARTs + EMTs) from 30 June 2024; remaining provisions (CASPs · other crypto-assets) from 30 December 2024. Transitional period for legacy CASPs operating under national law expires 1 July 2026 (with Member State option to shorten to 30 December 2025).

## Template Type
Contract Template (Crypto-Asset Service Provider ↔ Client)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties**
   - CASP — full identification + competent authority + authorisation reference (Art 59 MiCA).
   - Client — retail / professional client classification (per MiFID II Annex II analogously).
2. **Services Covered (Art 3(1)(16) MiCA)**
   - Custody · operation of trading platform · exchange (fiat ↔ crypto · crypto ↔ crypto) · execution of orders · placing · reception/transmission · advice · portfolio management · transfer services.
3. **Pre-Contractual Information (Art 66 MiCA + Annex III)**
   - White paper reference (for relevant crypto-assets).
   - Material risks · costs · conflicts of interest · complaints procedure.
   - Marketing communications compliance.
4. **Custody Provisions (Art 75 MiCA)**
   - Segregation of client crypto-assets from CASP's own.
   - Register kept of clients' positions.
   - Insolvency protection — client assets ring-fenced.
   - Acknowledgements from third-party custodians.
5. **Order Execution Policy (Art 78)**
   - Best-execution framework.
   - Venue list.
   - Client-consent regime.
6. **Conflicts of Interest (Art 72)** — Identification · prevention · disclosure of unavoidable conflicts.
7. **Complaints Handling (Art 71)** — Free of charge · timely · documented procedure.
8. **AML / CFT Compliance** — Travel Rule under TFR Reg 2023/1113 · KYC and ongoing customer due diligence.
9. **Market Abuse Prevention (Title VI MiCA)** — Insider list · suspicious-transaction reports · prohibition on market manipulation.
10. **Operational Resilience (DORA — see file 129)** — ICT risk management · incident reporting · third-party-provider register.
11. **Stablecoin-Specific Provisions** (where applicable)
    - ART / EMT classification.
    - Reserve assets backing.
    - Redemption rights — at par with the reference value.
12. **Termination** — Notice · effect on client positions · transfer of crypto-assets to designated wallet.
13. **Liability** — Operational failures · cyber-incidents · loss of crypto-assets.
14. **Compensation Schemes** — National investor / depositor protection extension where Member State elects.
15. **Choice of Law and Forum** — Rome I + Brussels Ia.

## Drafting Conventions
- **CASP authorisation** — Art 59 MiCA: single Member State authorisation grants passport across EEA.
- **Marketing communications** — must align with white paper · not misleading.
- **White paper** required for non-exempt crypto-asset offerings (Art 6); ESMA STS to publish.
- **Retail-investor protections** — cooling-off period (14 days for ART/EMT offers); right of withdrawal.
- **Sanctioned-list screening** — TFR + AMLD6 + EU restrictive measures regime.
- **Cross-border passporting** — notification to home and host competent authorities.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Crypto-asset service providers shall act honestly, fairly and professionally in the best interests of their clients and prospective clients, and shall provide clients or prospective clients with appropriate information." (Regulation 2023/1114, Art 66(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
MiCA created a new compliance burden across the EU. Solo practitioners advising fintech / crypto SMEs need to (i) audit existing client agreements against MiCA Art 66 disclosure requirements; (ii) update terms to reflect MiCA-specific protections (segregation · complaints · best execution); (iii) calendar the transitional period expiry to avoid loss of grandfathered status.
