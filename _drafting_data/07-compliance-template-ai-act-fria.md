# EU AI Act — Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA) Template

## Source URL
[EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) — Article 27](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32024R1689#d1e1672-1-1)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under the EU AI Act for certain high-risk AI deployers.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. This assessment must be completed **prior to the first use** of high-risk AI systems by public bodies, private entities providing public services, or those using AI for credit scoring/insurance pricing.

## Template Type
Compliance / Regulatory Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Description of the Deployer's Process**
   - Detailed context of how the high-risk AI system will be used.
   - Confirmation of alignment with the provider's "Intended Purpose."
2. **Temporal and Operational Scope**
   - Period of time and frequency of the intended use.
3. **Identification of Affected Persons**
   - Categories of natural persons and groups likely to be affected (e.g., job applicants, insurance policyholders, students).
4. **Specific Risks to Fundamental Rights**
   - Assessment of risks to rights protected by the EU Charter:
     - Human Dignity (Art 1).
     - Privacy and Personal Data (Arts 7 & 8).
     - Non-discrimination and Equality (Arts 21 & 23).
     - Rights of the Child (Art 24).
     - Right to an Effective Remedy (Art 47).
5. **Human Oversight Measures**
   - Description of the technical and organizational measures for human intervention (Art 14).
   - Identification of the individuals responsible for oversight.
6. **Mitigation and Internal Governance**
   - Procedures to be followed if risks materialize.
   - Complaint-handling and redress mechanisms for affected persons.
7. **Integration with DPIA**
   - Reference to the Data Protection Impact Assessment (Art 35 GDPR), ensuring a holistic view of risks.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Deployer Responsibility**: Unlike technical documentation (which is the provider's job), the FRIA is the **deployer's** responsibility.
- **Context-Specific**: The FRIA must evaluate the specific environment where the AI is deployed (e.g., a specific hospital or bank), not just the software's general properties.
- **Notification**: The results of the FRIA must be notified to the Market Surveillance Authority and, where applicable, submitted to the EU database.
- **Provider Cooperation**: Deployers should request relevant "Fundamental Rights" information from the AI provider to complete the assessment.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Prior to putting a high-risk AI system... into use, deployers... shall perform an assessment of the impact on fundamental rights that the use of such system may produce. For that purpose, deployers shall perform an assessment consisting of: (a) a description of the deployer’s processes... (b) the period of time and the frequency... (c) the categories of natural persons and groups likely to be affected..." (AI Act Art 27).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical for clients in the public sector or "essential services" (banks, insurance, education) deploying AI. Solo advocates use this to bridge the gap between "technical risk" and "legal risk," ensuring the client can justify the deployment of high-risk AI to regulators and the public.
