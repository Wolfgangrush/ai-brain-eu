Primary — Practice Rules + Rules of Procedure.

## Currency Warning
EU pleadings are written-stage-dominant; oral hearings are increasingly rare and short (often capped at 15-30 minutes per party). The written skeleton IS the case. Practice Rules require structural compliance — non-compliant pleadings may be returned for rectification.

## Template Type
Pleading Structural Reference

## Structural Skeleton
**Section 1 — Procedural Background (≤1 page)**
- Contested act / refusal / failure identified.
- Standing of applicant (privileged / non-privileged).
- Date of notification + calculated time-limit.

**Section 2 — Summary of Pleas (≤1 page)**
- Each plea named in one line. Required by Practice Rules.

**Section 3 — Statement of Facts (paragraph-numbered)**
- Chronological. Each material fact in its own paragraph.
- Cross-references to annexes by Annex number + page.

**Section 4 — Pleas in Law (heart of the pleading)**
- One section per plea.
- Within each plea:
  - Legal basis (Treaty article / regulation / directive cited).
  - Established case-law cited (CJEU + General Court).
  - Application to the contested act.
  - Why the contested act infringes the provision.
- Standard Art 263 grounds: lack of competence · infringement of essential procedural requirements · infringement of Treaties or rule of law · misuse of powers.

**Section 5 — Damages (if Art 268 / 340 paired action)**
- Causal link.
- Quantification.

**Section 6 — Form of Order Sought**
- Annul · declare failure · damages · costs.

**Section 7 — Evidence Offered**
- Schedule of annexes.

**Section 8 — Signature + Lawyer's Certification**

## Drafting Conventions
- **30-page limit** (General Court) · **25 pages** (Court of Justice on appeal).
- **A4 · 1.5 line spacing · Times New Roman 12pt · margins 2.5cm**.
- **Paragraph numbering** continuous throughout.
- **Annex pagination** — each annex paginated continuously; cross-references "Annex X, p. Y".
- **Footnotes** — only for cross-references; substantive content goes in the body.
- **e-Curia** submission · physical signatures only if e-Curia unavailable.
- **Cited case-law** — neutral CJEU citations (Case C-XXX/YY EU:C:YYYY:NNN).
- **No oral hearing presumption** — assume the chamber decides on papers; structure the skeleton to be self-sufficient.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Pleadings shall be drafted in a clear and precise manner. Pleas in law shall be developed and presented in such a way as to facilitate the reading and understanding of the Court's analysis." (Practice Rules of the General Court, point 116.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The CJEU skeleton structure is unforgiving — the chamber reads top-to-bottom and expects each plea to stand alone. Solo practitioners often misuse the "Summary of Pleas" section by leaving it generic; the chamber's first-pass reading is exactly that summary, so every plea must be named precisely there in one sharp line.
