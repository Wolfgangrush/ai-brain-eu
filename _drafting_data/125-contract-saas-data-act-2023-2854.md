# SaaS / Cloud Services Contract — Data Act 2023/2854 Compliance

## Source URL
- [Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 (Data Act)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32023R2854)
- [Commission guidance on Data Act implementation](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/data-act)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable).

## Currency Warning
Data Act in force from 11 January 2024; most provisions apply from **12 September 2025**. Mandatory contractual rules on cloud-services switching (Chapter VI) override conflicting contract terms. Chapter IV unfair-contract-term rules for B2B data-sharing apply where one party unilaterally imposes terms. **Switching charges** to be eliminated from 12 January 2027.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Provider + Customer.
2. **Definitions** — "Service" · "Data Processing Service" (DPS per Data Act Art 2(8)) · "Exportable Data" · "Switching" · "Functional Equivalence".
3. **Service Description**
   - SaaS / PaaS / IaaS classification.
   - Computing resources allocated.
   - Service Levels (uptime · response time · maintenance windows).
   - Geographic location of data (Member State or third country).
4. **Data Ownership and Rights**
   - Customer retains all rights in Customer Data.
   - Provider has limited license to process for Service provision.
   - Use of Customer Data for AI training / product improvement: prohibited absent specific consent.
5. **Switching and Portability (Chapter VI Data Act)**
   - Right to switch to another provider or in-house.
   - **Maximum switching period: 30 days** (post-notification) for completion of switching process.
   - **Mandatory transition assistance** — extraction of all exportable data + applications + digital assets in commonly used machine-readable structured format.
   - **Functional equivalence** — Provider's obligation to preserve functional equivalence post-switching for IaaS.
   - **Charges**: phased reduction, eliminated by 12 January 2027.
6. **Interoperability Standards**
   - Conformance to applicable interoperability specifications (Data Spaces · EU Cloud Rulebook).
7. **Switching Trigger Events**
   - At customer election with reasonable notice (30 days).
   - On Provider material change to service · pricing · governing law.
   - On insolvency · regulatory action against Provider.
8. **Confidentiality and Trade Secrets**
   - Provider's trade secrets and IP rights protected during switching.
   - Customer not entitled to extract Provider's proprietary algorithms.
9. **International Data Transfers (Chapter VII Data Act)**
   - Provider must implement safeguards against unauthorised non-EU government access to non-personal data.
   - Disclosure to non-EU authorities only via mutual-legal-assistance treaty or comparable instruments.
10. **B2B Unfair Terms Test (Chapter IV)**
    - Terms unilaterally imposed are void if they (a) exclude / limit Provider's liability for intent or gross negligence, (b) give Provider sole discretion to determine data conformity, (c) prevent Customer from using its own data.
11. **Security** — NIS2 compliance · Provider's security certification (ISO 27001 · EUCS where adopted).
12. **GDPR Annex** — DPA covering personal data within Customer Data (SCCs for non-EU transfers).
13. **Term · Termination · Boilerplate** — standard EU SaaS framework.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Switching obligations cannot be waived** — Data Act provisions are mandatory.
- **Functional equivalence** narrowly defined: applies to IaaS only.
- **Document switching plan** at contract execution — many disputes arise from absence of advance specification.
- **Charge transparency** — itemise transition-period charges separately.
- **Trade-secret carve-out** must be balanced against switching effectiveness; Commission will scrutinise abuse.
- **Pair with GDPR DPA** (existing file 01) where personal data is involved.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Providers of data processing services shall take the measures provided for in this Chapter to enable customers to switch to data processing services covering the same service type provided by a different provider of data processing services... or to switch to an on-premises ICT infrastructure." (Regulation 2023/2854, Art 23.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Cloud-contract negotiation has been reshaped by the Data Act. Solo practitioners advising SME customers should push for switching plans documented at execution + clear data extraction formats. Provider-side counsel must update standard SaaS templates to remove now-void switching restrictions and excessive lock-in pricing.
