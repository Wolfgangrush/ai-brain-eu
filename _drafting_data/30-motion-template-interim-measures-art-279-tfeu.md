## Authority Tier
Primary — Treaty provisions + Rules of Procedure.

## Currency Warning
The application for interim measures is decided by the **President** of the Court / General Court (or another judge designated for this purpose). The standard remains: prima facie case (*fumus boni juris*) + urgency + balance of interests. No automatic suspensive effect of the main action.

## Template Type
Motion / Interim Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header** — Separate document accompanying or following the main application · case number.
2. **Subject-Matter of the Interim Application**
   - Suspension of operation of the contested act (Art 278), OR
   - Other interim measures necessary to protect the applicant (Art 279).
3. **Conditions for Grant — Triple Test**
   - **Fumus boni juris**: the main pleas have at least a *prima facie* arguable basis. Cannot be a manifestly hopeless action.
   - **Urgency**: serious and irreparable damage will occur to the applicant before final judgment. Financial damage is rarely sufficient unless it endangers viability.
   - **Balance of interests**: harm to applicant from refusal vs harm to Union / third parties from grant.
4. **Statement of Facts** — Specific evidence of the threatened damage. Quantified where possible.
5. **Evidence of Urgency** — Sworn declarations · financial data · regulatory deadlines · medical evidence (for natural persons).
6. **Form of Order Sought**
   - Specific interim measure requested.
   - Costs reserved.
7. **Annexes** — Supporting documents.
8. **Signature**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **25-page limit** for the interim application.
- **Filed separately** from the main application via e-Curia (own case number, "R" suffix).
- **Without prejudice to the main action** — the interim decision does not prejudge the substance.
- **No appeal** against the President's interim order other than to a different formation in limited circumstances.
- **Counter-security** — the President may order the applicant to lodge security for damage caused by the interim measure if the main action ultimately fails.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court of Justice of the European Union may in any cases before it prescribe any necessary interim measures." (Art 279 TFEU.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical for clients facing imminent Commission decisions — antitrust fines · state-aid recovery orders · DMA designation · trade-defence duties. The urgency limb is often the failure point: practitioners must quantify financial loss against the company's viability threshold, not merely budget impact.
