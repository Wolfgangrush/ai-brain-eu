# European Enforcement Order (EEO) for Uncontested Claims

## Source URL
- [Regulation (EC) No 805/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32004R0805)
- [Commission Regulation (EC) No 1869/2005 — Annexes (Standard Forms)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32005R1869)
- [e-Justice EEO portal](https://e-justice.europa.eu/content_european_enforcement_order-376-en.do)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable in all Member States except Denmark).

## Currency Warning
EEO is available for **uncontested claims only**. Denmark is excluded. Since Brussels Ia Recast (Regulation 1215/2012) abolished the *exequatur* for most civil and commercial judgments, the EEO retains practical value mainly for authentic instruments and court settlements that do not benefit from automatic recognition under Brussels Ia.

## Template Type
Enforcement Certificate Application / EU Standard Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court of Origin** — Court that issued the underlying decision · case number · date.
2. **Type of Decision**
   - Judgment (where debtor did not contest the debt, or contested it then admitted, or did not appear).
   - Court settlement.
   - Authentic instrument (e.g., notarial deed enforceable in the Member State of origin).
3. **Identification of Parties** — Creditor + debtor (full identification per Art 8).
4. **Claim** — Principal · interest (rate and starting date) · costs awarded.
5. **Certification of Compliance with Minimum Standards (Arts 12-19)**
   - Service of the document instituting proceedings was effected with sufficient time + means.
   - Information on the claim provided.
   - Information on procedural steps to contest provided.
   - Review mechanism for exceptional cases.
6. **Standard Form Annex** — Annex I (judgment) · Annex II (court settlement) · Annex III (authentic instrument) of Regulation 1869/2005.
7. **Signature** of the court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Application to the court of origin** for certification — no application to the court of enforcement needed.
- **Certification is mandatory**, not discretionary, if minimum standards met.
- **Direct enforcement** — once certified, the EEO is enforced in any Member State without further declaration.
- **No appeal** against certification per se; only rectification under Art 10.
- **Translation** of the certificate into the enforcement-state language may be required by the enforcement authority.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The purpose of this Regulation is to create a European Enforcement Order for uncontested claims to permit, by laying down minimum standards, the free circulation of judgments, court settlements and authentic instruments throughout all Member States without any intermediate proceedings needing to be brought in the Member State of enforcement prior to recognition and enforcement." (Regulation 805/2004, Art 1.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Useful where the underlying obligation is a notarial deed or court settlement rather than a judgment (which now travels automatically under Brussels Ia). Civil-law jurisdictions (DE · FR · IT · ES · NL · PL) use notarial deeds extensively; the EEO converts them into directly enforceable instruments across the EU.
