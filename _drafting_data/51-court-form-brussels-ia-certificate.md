# Brussels Ia — Certificate of Enforcement (Annex I)

## Source URL
- [Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 (Brussels Ia) — Annex I](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32012R1215)
- [Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/281 (Updated Form)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32015R0281)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory form under Brussels Ia Regulation Article 53.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. The exequatur (declaration of enforceability) is abolished under this Regulation; the Annex I certificate + a copy of the judgment are sufficient for enforcement in another Member State.

## Template Type
Court Form / Cross-Border Enforcement

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court of Origin**
   - Name, address, and contact details of the court issuing the judgment.
   - Court reference/case number.
2. **The Parties**
   - Claimant(s): Full name, address, date of birth / company reg number.
   - Defendant(s): Full name, address, date of birth / company reg number.
3. **The Judgment**
   - Date of the judgment.
   - Type of judgment (Final, Provisional, Default).
   - Statement of Enforceability: Confirmation that the judgment is enforceable in the Member State of origin.
   - Service details (for default judgments): Date and method of service of the instituting document.
4. **The Award (Substantive Content)**
   - Monetary Orders: Principal amount, currency, interest rate, and calculation period.
   - Non-Monetary Orders: Description of the specific measure or injunction.
   - Costs and Expenses: Detailed breakdown of legal costs awarded.
5. **Authenticity**
   - Date and place of issue of the certificate.
   - Signature and/or stamp of the issuing court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Article 53 Request**: The certificate must be issued by the court of origin at the request of any interested party.
- **Abolition of Exequatur**: No separate "declaration of enforceability" is required in the Member State of enforcement.
- **Service on Debtor**: The certificate must be served on the defendant before the first enforcement measure is taken (Art 43).
- **Adaptation**: If the judgment contains an unknown measure in the Member State of enforcement, it may be adapted to one with equivalent effects (Art 54).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The court of origin shall, at the request of any interested party, issue the certificate using the form set out in Annex I... The certificate shall contain: (a) a summary of the judgment; (b) the amount for which the judgment is enforceable; (c) the interest rate... (d) the costs." (Brussels Ia Regulation, Article 53).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The essential tool for "chasing" debtors or enforcing injunctions across the EU. Solo advocates use this template to ensure the court of origin issues a compliant certificate, allowing for "automatic" enforcement in any other Member State without costly local exequatur proceedings.
