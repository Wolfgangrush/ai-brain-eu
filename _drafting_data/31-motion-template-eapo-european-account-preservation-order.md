# European Account Preservation Order (EAPO) — Cross-Border Freezing

## Source URL
- [Regulation (EU) No 655/2014](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32014R0655)
- [Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2016/1823 — Annex I (Application Form)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32016R1823)
- [e-Justice EAPO portal](https://e-justice.europa.eu/content_european_account_preservation_order-378-en.do)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable in all Member States except Denmark).

## Currency Warning
EAPO is **available only in cross-border cases** (debtor's bank account in a different Member State than the court seized). Denmark is excluded. The creditor may apply ex parte; the debtor learns of the order only when the bank freezes the account.

## Template Type
Motion / Interim Application / EU Standard Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Identification** — Court with jurisdiction over the main proceedings OR specifically designated EAPO court in the Member State (see Art 6).
2. **Parties** — Creditor (name · address · domicile · representative); Debtor (name · address · domicile).
3. **Stage of Proceedings**
   - **Before main proceedings**: creditor must initiate substantive proceedings within 30 days of applying (or 14 days of issue of EAPO, whichever later).
   - **After judgment / authentic instrument / settlement** — different evidentiary threshold.
4. **Claim Amount + Currency**
5. **Bank Account Details**
   - Account number IBAN if known.
   - If unknown: request for account information under Art 14 (court will query the designated information authority of the Member State of enforcement).
6. **Evidence of the Claim**
   - Substantiation of the merits sufficient to satisfy the court the creditor is likely to succeed.
7. **Evidence of Risk** (pre-judgment cases)
   - Real risk that without the measure the subsequent enforcement of a judgment will be frustrated.
8. **Security** (Art 12)
   - Court may require the creditor to provide security for damages to the debtor if the EAPO proves unjustified.
9. **Annexes** — Contract · invoices · correspondence · existing judgment (if any).
10. **Signature**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Annex I Application Form** of Implementing Regulation 2016/1823 is mandatory.
- **Ex parte procedure** — no notice to debtor before issue.
- **Decision within 10 working days** by the court (where evidence is sufficient).
- **Cap on freezing** — only up to the claim amount + interest + reasonable costs.
- **Translation**: required where service is to be effected in a Member State whose official language differs.
- **Liability for unjustified EAPO** — Art 13; the creditor bears strict-or-fault liability depending on Member State implementation.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Regulation establishes a Union procedure enabling a creditor to obtain a European Account Preservation Order which prevents the subsequent enforcement of the creditor's claim from being jeopardised through the transfer or withdrawal of funds up to the amount specified in the Order which are held by the debtor or on his behalf in a bank account maintained in a Member State." (Regulation 655/2014, Art 1(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The EU's most powerful cross-border collection tool — pre-judgment freezing without the debtor's prior knowledge. Critical for clients pursuing intra-EU debt recovery against debtors with bank accounts in multiple Member States. Practitioners must carefully evidence the "real risk" limb in pre-judgment applications.
