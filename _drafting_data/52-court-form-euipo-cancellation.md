# EUIPO — Trade Mark Cancellation Action Template

## Source URL
- [EU Trade Mark Regulation (EUTMR) (EU) 2017/1001 — Articles 58-60](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32017R1001)
- [EUIPO Trade Mark Guidelines (Part D: Cancellation)](https://euipo.europa.eu/ohimportal/en/trade-mark-guidelines)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory regulation and official EUIPO Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Cancellation includes both **Revocation** (post-registration issues) and **Invalidity** (flaws at the time of registration). A separate fee is required for each type of action.

## Template Type
Court Form / Intellectual Property

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Contested EUTM Details**
   - Registration number of the contested EUTM.
   - Name of the proprietor.
2. **Applicant Details**
   - Full name and address of the person/entity seeking cancellation.
   - Identification of the representative (if any).
3. **Type of Action (Selection Required)**
   - **Revocation** (Art 58): Non-use, genericism, or misleading use.
   - **Invalidity** (Art 59-60): Absolute grounds (bad faith, descriptive) or Relative grounds (earlier rights).
4. **Grounds of Cancellation (Legal Basis)**
   - Revocation Grounds:
     - 58(1)(a): Continuous non-use for 5 years.
     - 58(1)(b): Mark has become generic.
     - 58(1)(c): Mark has become misleading.
   - Invalidity Grounds:
     - 59: Absolute grounds (e.g., bad faith).
     - 60: Relative grounds (conflict with earlier trade marks or other rights).
5. **Extent of the Application**
   - Statement if the action is against all goods/services or only a specified partial list.
6. **Statement of Grounds**
   - Detailed reasoned statement of facts and arguments (often as an attachment).
7. **Language of the Action**
   - Selection of one of the two languages of the contested EUTM (must be an Office language).
8. **Payment of Fee**
   - Confirmation of payment (€630 as of 2026).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Single Action Rule**: A single application cannot combine Revocation and Invalidity unless two fees are paid and two separate files are opened.
- **Bad Faith (Art 59(1)(b))**: A common ground for absolute invalidity; requires evidence of the applicant's intent at the time of filing.
- **Genuine Use (Art 64(2))**: In relative invalidity cases, the proprietor can demand that the applicant prove genuine use of their earlier mark (if registered for >5 years).
- **Electronic Filing**: Mandatory for professional representatives via the EUIPO User Area.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"An application for revocation of the rights of the proprietor of an EU trade mark or for a declaration that the mark is invalid shall be filed with the Office... The application shall be filed in a written reasoned statement. It shall not be deemed to have been filed until the fee has been paid." (EUTMR Art 63(1)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The tool for "cleaning" the register of obstructive marks. Solo advocates use this template to challenge marks that haven't been used or were filed in bad faith to block a client's market entry.
