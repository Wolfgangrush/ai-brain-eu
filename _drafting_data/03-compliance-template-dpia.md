## Template Type
Compliance / Regulatory Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Section 0: Technical Sheet**
   - Identification of Controller and DPO
   - RACI Matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)
   - Trigger Analysis (Why a DPIA is required under Art 35(1) or national lists)
2. **Section 1: Description of Processing**
   - Nature, Scope, Context, and Purpose
   - Data lifecycle (Collection to Deletion)
   - Data Flow Diagrams (functional description)
   - Technical architecture (Hardware, Software, Networks)
3. **Section 2: Compliance Measures**
   - Legal basis (Art 6 and Art 9)
   - Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs) already in place
   - Adherence to Codes of Conduct or Certification
4. **Section 3: Necessity and Proportionality**
   - Justification of purpose
   - Assessment of data minimization (adequacy and relevance)
   - Storage limitation and deletion procedures
   - Mechanism for data subject rights (access, erasure, etc.)
5. **Section 4: Risk Assessment**
   - Perspective: Data subject’s rights and freedoms
   - Inherent Risk (Design Risk)
   - Operational Risk (Incident Risk)
   - Evaluation of Likelihood and Severity
6. **Section 5: Risk Treatment**
   - Mitigation actions for each identified risk
   - Residual Risk evaluation (Low, Medium, High)
7. **Section 6: Conclusion and Sign-off**
   - Formal opinion of the DPO
   - View of data subjects (where applicable)
   - Management decision on residual risks

## Drafting Conventions
- **Data Subject Perspective**: Risks must be assessed based on the potential harm to the individual, not the business.
- **Trigger Logic**: Use the "2+ Criteria" rule from WP248 (e.g., scoring + sensitive data = mandatory DPIA).
- **Residual Risk**: If residual risk remains "High" after mitigation, the controller MUST consult the Supervisory Authority (Art 36).
- **Living Document**: The DPIA must be reviewed whenever the processing operation changes.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The DPIA is a process designed to describe the processing, assess its necessity and proportionality and help manage the risks to the rights and freedoms of natural persons resulting from the processing of personal data... by assessing them and determining the measures to address them." (WP248, p. 4).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
A mandatory requirement for clients launching high-risk products (e.g., health-tech, AI-driven recruitment, large-scale monitoring). Solo advocates use this template to guide clients through the risk-mitigation process and document compliance for potential audits by DPAs.
