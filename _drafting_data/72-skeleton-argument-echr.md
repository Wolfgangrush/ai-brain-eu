## Currency Warning
**4-month time-limit** from the final domestic decision (reduced from 6 months by Protocol No 15, in force since 1 August 2022). Application must be on the official Rule 47 form with all required annexes; incomplete applications are not even registered. Single-judge formation may declare manifestly ill-founded applications inadmissible with no appeal.

## Template Type
Pleading Structural Reference

## Structural Skeleton
**Part A — Identification (Rule 47 form)**
- Applicant: name · DoB · nationality · address · occupation.
- High Contracting Party against which the application is directed.
- Representative (advocate): name · address · authority to act.

**Part B — Statement of Facts**
- Chronological. Concise. No legal argument here.
- Each domestic decision identified by date + court + outcome.

**Part C — Statement of Alleged Violations**
- Convention Article(s) invoked (Art 6 fair trial · Art 8 private life · Art 10 expression · Art 14 discrimination · Protocol 1 Art 1 property · etc.).
- For each Article: brief explanation of the violation alleged.

**Part D — Compliance with Admissibility Criteria**
- **Exhaustion of domestic remedies** (Art 35(1)) — chronicle of remedies pursued.
- **4-month time-limit** from final domestic decision.
- **Significant disadvantage** test (Art 35(3)(b)) — addressed.
- Not anonymous · not substantially the same as previously examined · not incompatible ratione materiae / personae / loci / temporis.

**Part E — Statement of the Object of the Application**
- What the applicant asks the Court to decide.
- Just satisfaction claimed (Art 41) — quantified.

**Part F — Other International Proceedings**
- Declaration whether the matter is or has been before another international procedure.

**Part G — List of Annexes**
- Copies of all domestic decisions · key procedural documents · evidence.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Rule 47 form mandatory** — completed in one of the two official languages (English or French).
- **Statement of Facts: maximum 4 pages**; Statement of Violations: maximum 4 pages.
- **Annexes** — copies only (not originals); paginated chronologically.
- **No new annexes** at the merits stage without leave.
- **Power of attorney** required where applicant is represented.
- **Legal aid** available at the merits / oral hearing stage (rare).
- **Friendly settlement** (Art 39) — Court may at any stage place itself at the parties' disposal to facilitate.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"In order to be able to lodge an application in accordance with Article 34 of the Convention, applicants must submit a duly completed application form and provide all information... Failure to comply with the requirements set out... will result in the application not being examined by the Court." (ECHR Rule 47(2).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The ECHR remains the last resort after exhausting EU and domestic remedies. Solo practitioners must front-load admissibility — a single-judge formation rejects ~95% of applications at this stage. Concrete, paragraph-numbered facts cross-referenced to specific Convention articles drastically improve registration prospects.
