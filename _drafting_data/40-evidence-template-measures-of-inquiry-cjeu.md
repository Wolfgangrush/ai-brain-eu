## Authority Tier
Primary — Rules of Procedure of the General Court.

## Currency Warning
Measures of inquiry are decided by the chamber to which the case has been assigned. The General Court may, by reasoned order, prescribe at any stage of the proceedings (including after written exchange) any measure it considers necessary for the proper determination of the case.

## Template Type
Procedural Application / Evidence Request

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identification of the Case** — Case number · parties · current procedural stage.
2. **Measure Requested** (Art 91 sets out the menu)
   - Personal appearance of the parties.
   - Request for information and production of documents.
   - Oral testimony of witnesses.
   - Expert opinions.
   - Inspection of the place or thing in question.
3. **Subject-Matter and Necessity**
   - Specific factual question the measure is intended to elucidate.
   - Demonstration that the question is contested and material to outcome.
   - Why existing documentary evidence is insufficient.
4. **Witness Identification (if witness testimony sought)**
   - Name · address · nature of relationship to facts.
   - Summary of expected testimony.
   - Reasons supporting credibility / relevance.
5. **Document Request (if production sought)**
   - Specific identification of documents.
   - Holder of the documents.
   - Reasoned ground showing they are likely to exist and to be relevant.
6. **Expert Brief (if expert opinion sought)**
   - Proposed question(s) to the expert.
   - Suggested expert / professional discipline.
   - Estimated cost.
7. **Procedural Effects** — Suspension of written stage timetable · costs of the measure.
8. **Form of Order Sought** — Specific order from the chamber.
9. **Annexes**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **No page-limit** specific to measures of inquiry; concise (under 10 pages) is conventional.
- **e-Curia filing** mandatory.
- **Specificity is key** — generic requests ("all internal Commission communications") are routinely refused; targeted, document-by-document requests are granted.
- **Cost allocation** — court may order the requesting party to bear advance costs of the measure.
- **Right of reply** — opposing party is heard before the chamber orders the measure.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Without prejudice to the measures of organisation of procedure... the General Court shall prescribe the measures of inquiry that it considers appropriate by means of an order setting out the facts to be proved. Before the General Court decides on the measures of inquiry referred to in Article 91(c) to (e), the parties shall be heard." (Rules of Procedure of the General Court, Art 92.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Underused tool in EU litigation. Solo practitioners challenging Commission decisions (state aid · antitrust) can compel production of internal Commission documents or compel hearing of Commission officials. The targeted-specificity rule is the difference between grant and refusal — practitioners must do the homework on the Commission's file before drafting.
