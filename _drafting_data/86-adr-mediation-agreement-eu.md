# Mediated Settlement Agreement — Cross-Border Enforceability

## Source URL
- [Directive 2008/52/EC — Article 6 (Enforceability)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32008L0052)
- [Singapore Convention on Mediation (UN Convention on International Settlement Agreements Resulting from Mediation, 2019)](https://uncitral.un.org/en/texts/mediation/conventions/international_settlement_agreements)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Directive (Art 6 enforceability) + Singapore Convention (where applicable).

## Currency Warning
The EU Directive guarantees enforceability mechanisms within the EU (court approval · notarial deed · authentic instrument under Brussels Ia / EEO). The Singapore Convention (in force August 2020) provides cross-border enforceability for international commercial settlements OUTSIDE the EU — only Belgium, Italy, Luxembourg, and a handful of other Member States are signatories; the EU itself has not ratified.

## Template Type
Settlement Contract / Deed

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties**
   - Full identification of each party + legal representatives.
2. **Recitals**
   - Existence of the underlying dispute.
   - Reference to the mediation conducted (date · mediator · location).
   - Without admission of liability.
3. **Definitions**
   - "Effective Date" · "Underlying Claims" · "Released Parties" · "Confidential Information".
4. **Settlement Terms**
   - **Payment(s)** — amount · currency · payment schedule · interest on late payment.
   - **Non-monetary obligations** — performance · transfer of assets · IP licensing.
   - **Conditions precedent** (regulatory approvals · board approvals).
5. **Mutual Releases**
   - Full and final settlement of all known and unknown claims arising from the subject-matter.
   - Carve-outs (criminal liability · regulatory penalties · fraud).
6. **Discontinuance of Proceedings**
   - Joint application to the court of seizing to discontinue or stay.
   - Tomlin-equivalent stay where Member State law provides.
7. **Confidentiality**
   - Existence and terms of the agreement.
   - Limited disclosure carve-outs (auditors · regulators · court enforcement).
8. **Governing Law and Forum**
   - Governing law (Rome I — freely chosen).
   - Forum for disputes arising from the agreement itself (court · arbitration).
9. **Enforceability Mechanism (Art 6 Directive)** — Choose ONE:
   - **Consent order** lodged with the court of seizing.
   - **Notarial deed** in a civil-law Member State (directly enforceable under EEO Reg 805/2004).
   - **Authentic instrument** under Brussels Ia Art 58.
10. **Boilerplate** — Entire agreement · variation in writing · severance · counterparts · electronic signature validity.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Bilingual or working-language drafting** for cross-border parties — designate the prevailing language.
- **Conditions precedent** explicit — many cross-border settlements fail at the regulatory-approval stage.
- **Enforceability mechanism is essential** — a bare settlement contract may be hard to enforce abroad; choose the EEO / notarial-deed / court-consent-order route.
- **Tax structuring** — withholding tax · transfer pricing implications · VAT.
- **No-claims-against-individuals** clause for representatives where appropriate.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Member States shall ensure that it is possible for the parties, or for one of them with the explicit consent of the others, to request that the content of a written agreement resulting from mediation be made enforceable." (Directive 2008/52/EC, Art 6(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The enforceability-mechanism choice is the single most important drafting decision. A settlement that is "binding" in one Member State but lacks an EU enforcement vehicle invites a second round of litigation. Solo practitioners default to court-consent-order where proceedings are on foot; notarial deed where pre-action.
