# Commercial Agency Agreement — Directive 86/653/EEC

## Source URL
- [Council Directive 86/653/EEC on self-employed commercial agents](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:31986L0653)
- [CJEU jurisprudence: Ingmar (C-381/98) · Unamar (C-184/12) · ERGO Insurance (C-359/14)](https://curia.europa.eu/)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Directive (transposed in all Member States; mandatory protective provisions).

## Currency Warning
**Mandatory rules** for the protection of commercial agents established in the EU apply even where the parties choose non-EU governing law (*Ingmar* doctrine confirmed and extended by *Unamar*). Key mandatory provisions: written-form right · termination notice · post-termination indemnity OR compensation (Art 17 · choice made at Member State level) · accounting rights · commission entitlement.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Principal + Commercial Agent (full identification; Agent must be self-employed natural or legal person).
2. **Recitals** — Authority to negotiate / conclude · territory · product or service category.
3. **Definitions** — "Products / Services" · "Territory" · "Customers" · "Commission" · "Termination Date".
4. **Agent's Duties (Art 3)**
   - Look after principal's interests in good faith.
   - Negotiate / conclude transactions entrusted.
   - Communicate necessary information.
   - Comply with reasonable instructions.
5. **Principal's Duties (Art 4)**
   - Provide necessary documentation.
   - Inform agent of acceptance / refusal / non-execution.
   - Inform agent within reasonable period of anticipated reduction in business volume.
6. **Territory / Exclusivity**
   - Exclusive · sole · non-exclusive.
   - Geographic scope (must comply with Vertical Block Exemption Regulation 2022/720 for territorial restrictions).
7. **Commission (Arts 6-12)**
   - Rate · base · timing (no later than end of next quarter).
   - Right to commission on transactions concluded after termination if (a) attributable to agent's efforts and (b) concluded within reasonable period after termination.
   - Statement of commission due — agent's right to demand (Art 12).
8. **Duration and Termination (Arts 14-15)**
   - Indefinite term: notice 1 month per year of agency, capped at 6 months.
   - Fixed term: not renewed automatically; if performance continues, deemed indefinite.
9. **Indemnity OR Compensation on Termination (Art 17) — MANDATORY**
   - Member State chooses model (most MS = indemnity; FR / IE / UK pre-Brexit = compensation).
   - Indemnity: max 1 year's average remuneration (last 5 years).
   - Compensation: aggregate of loss, calculated on principles of equity.
10. **Restraint of Trade (Art 20)**
    - Maximum 2 years post-termination.
    - Limited to territory + product category covered.
    - Must be in writing.
11. **Choice of Law and Forum**
    - Rome I governs; protective mandatory provisions of Member State of agent apply regardless.
12. **Boilerplate** — Confidentiality · IP · audit · severance · variation in writing.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Written form** — Art 13 right of either party to written confirmation of agreement terms (cannot be waived).
- **Mandatory provisions cannot be derogated from** to agent's detriment.
- **VBER 2022/720** — territorial / customer restrictions must comply (hardcore restrictions cause loss of block exemption).
- **Competition law** — exclusivity provisions must be reasonable.
- **Tax** — withholding tax · VAT (B2B place-of-supply rules); cross-border VAT registration considerations.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Member States shall take the measures necessary to ensure that, after termination of the agency contract, the commercial agent is indemnified... or compensated for damage. Member States shall... provide for one or other of these systems." (Directive 86/653/EEC, Art 17(1) + (2).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Cross-border commercial agency is one of the most litigated EU contract types because the *Ingmar* mandatory-provisions doctrine catches non-EU principals by surprise. Solo practitioners must advise non-EU clients (US tech, Asian manufacturers) that their EU-located agents enjoy non-waivable indemnity rights regardless of any choice-of-law clause selecting non-EU law.
