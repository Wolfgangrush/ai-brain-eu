# EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — Commission Decision 2021/914

## Source URL
[Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32021D0914)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - European Commission Implementing Decision under GDPR Art 46(2)(c).

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. These clauses replaced all previous versions of SCCs. Note that for transfers to the UK, the UK Addendum or IDTA must be used in conjunction with these clauses where applicable.

## Template Type
Contract / Regulatory Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Section I: General Clauses**
   - Clause 1: Purpose and scope
   - Clause 2: Effect and invariability of the Clauses
   - Clause 3: Third-party beneficiaries
   - Clause 4: Interpretation
   - Clause 5: Hierarchy
   - Clause 6: Description of the transfer(s)
   - Clause 7: Docking clause (Optional)
2. **Section II: Obligations of the Parties**
   - **Module 1: Controller to Controller (C2C)**
   - **Module 2: Controller to Processor (C2P)**
   - **Module 3: Processor to Processor (P2P)**
   - **Module 4: Processor to Controller (P2C)**
   - Clause 8: Data protection safeguards (Modulated)
   - Clause 9: Use of sub-processors (Modules 2 and 3 only)
   - Clause 10: Data subject rights (Modulated)
   - Clause 11: Redress
   - Clause 12: Liability
   - Clause 13: Supervision
3. **Section III: Local Laws and Government Access**
   - Clause 14: Local laws and practices affecting compliance with the Clauses (Transfer Impact Assessment - TIA)
   - Clause 15: Obligations of the data importer in case of access by public authorities
4. **Section IV: Final Provisions**
   - Clause 16: Non-compliance with the Clauses and termination
   - Clause 17: Governing law
   - Clause 18: Choice of forum and jurisdiction
5. **Annexes**
   - Annex I: A. List of Parties; B. Description of Transfer; C. Competent Supervisory Authority
   - Annex II: Technical and organizational measures (TOMs)
   - Annex III: List of sub-processors (Modules 2 & 3)

## Drafting Conventions
- **Module Selection**: Parties must select exactly one module per data flow relationship. In multi-party agreements, different modules may apply to different flows.
- **Invariability**: The text of the Clauses themselves (Sections I-IV) cannot be altered. Any changes must be made via Annexes or separate side-agreements that do not contradict the Clauses.
- **Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA)**: Under Clause 14, parties must document their assessment of the destination country's legal system (the "Schrems II" requirement).
- **Docking Clause**: If Clause 7 is included, new parties can join by executing Annex I.A.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The data exporter shall provide the data subject with a copy of these Clauses, including the Appendix as completed by the parties, free of charge, upon request. To the extent necessary to protect personal data or other confidential information... the data exporter may redact part of the text... prior to sharing a copy, but shall provide a meaningful summary if the data subject would otherwise not be able to understand its content or exercise his/her rights." (Clause 8.2(b)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "de facto" international standard for data transfers. Solo advocates advising SMEs on SaaS procurement or cross-border outsourcing will use this template to legalize the flow of personal data from the EU to non-adequate third countries (e.g., USA, India, Australia).
