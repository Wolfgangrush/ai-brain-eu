## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Insolvency Regulation Recast.

## Currency Warning
Secondary proceedings under the Recast Regulation are no longer limited to winding-up; they may also be restructuring-type (Art 3(3)). The main insolvency practitioner can give an **undertaking** under Art 36 to avoid the opening of secondary proceedings, by agreeing to treat local creditors as if secondary proceedings had been opened.

## Template Type
Procedural Application / Cross-Border Coordination

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identification of Debtor and Main Proceedings**
   - Debtor name + COMI Member State.
   - Main proceedings reference (court · case number · main insolvency practitioner).
2. **Establishment in Secondary Member State (Art 3(2))**
   - "Establishment" = any place of operations where the debtor carries out non-transitory economic activity with human means and assets.
   - Evidence of branch · subsidiary · permanent representative · operational premises.
3. **Local Creditors' Interest**
   - Identification of creditors locally established.
   - Why secondary proceedings would better protect them than main proceedings alone.
4. **Effect Limited to Assets in the Secondary Member State (Art 3(2))**
   - Secondary proceedings cover only the debtor's assets within the territory.
5. **Coordination with Main Proceedings**
   - **Duty to cooperate** between main and secondary insolvency practitioners (Art 41) + between courts (Art 42).
   - Stay of secondary proceedings (Art 46) — at the request of the main IP, the court may stay realisation of assets for up to 3 months (renewable).
   - **Conversion** (Art 51) — secondary proceedings of winding-up type may be converted to a different procedure listed in Annex A.
6. **Undertaking by Main IP (Art 36)** — Alternative
   - Written undertaking that local creditors will be treated as if a secondary proceeding had been opened.
   - Must be approved by local creditors (qualified majority).
   - If approved, court of secondary Member State refuses to open secondary proceedings.
7. **Recognition of Main Proceedings**
   - Automatic recognition under Art 19 — no exequatur.
   - But the law applicable to the secondary proceedings is the *lex fori concursus* of the secondary Member State (Art 7).
8. **Annexes** — Main-proceedings judgment · evidence of local establishment · creditor list · IP authorisation.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard forms** — same Annex I / II of Reg 2017/1105 for creditor claims / notifications.
- **Public register entry** in the secondary Member State (Art 24).
- **Group coordination** (Chapter V) — relevant where main and secondary proceedings concern affiliated entities.
- **Insolvency practitioner identification** — Annex B lists eligible IP categories per Member State.
- **Avoiding actions** — governed by law of the main proceedings except where the law of another Member State governs the specific transaction (Art 16).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Where the centre of the debtor's main interests is situated within the territory of a Member State, the courts of another Member State shall have jurisdiction to open insolvency proceedings against that debtor only if it possesses an establishment within the territory of that other Member State. The effects of those proceedings shall be restricted to the assets of the debtor situated in the territory of the latter Member State." (Regulation 2015/848, Art 3(2).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Secondary proceedings are the principal protective mechanism for local creditors against cross-border insolvency forum-shopping. Solo practitioners representing local creditors should consider whether opening secondary proceedings is worth the cost vs negotiating an Art 36 undertaking from the main IP — the latter is faster and avoids fragmentation of the estate.
