# CJEU Preliminary Reference — Request Template

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - CJEU Recommendations to national courts.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Note that as of October 1, 2024, the **General Court** has jurisdiction to hear preliminary ruling requests in certain specific areas (VAT, excise duties, customs, etc.). Requests should still be sent to the Court of Justice, which will decide on the transfer.

## Template Type
Court Form / Judicial Reference

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identification of the Referring Court**
   - Full name and location of the national court or tribunal.
2. **Subject-Matter of the Dispute**
   - Summary of the facts and the parties (with anonymisation where necessary).
3. **National Legal Framework**
   - Exact text of the national provisions applicable to the case.
   - Relevant national case-law.
4. **Relevant EU Law Provisions**
   - Specific Articles of the Treaties, Regulations, or Directives requiring interpretation.
5. **Reasons for the Reference**
   - Why the national court considers an interpretation or validity ruling is necessary.
   - The link between the national provisions and the EU law provisions.
6. **The Questions Referred**
   - A separate and clearly identified section.
   - Questions must be self-contained and numbered.
7. **The Referring Court’s View (Optional)**
   - A brief statement on the proposed answer (highly recommended for expedited/urgent procedures).
8. **Stay of Proceedings**
   - Formal order staying the national proceedings until the CJEU rules.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Anonymisation**: Mandatory for natural persons (use initials/letters) to comply with data protection rules before the CJEU.
- **Clarity and Brevity**: Ideally no more than **10 pages**. Avoid long quotes; provide summaries instead.
- **Separate Section for Questions**: Questions must be readable without reference to the rest of the document.
- **Language**: The request must be in the language of the national court; it will be translated by the CJEU into all official languages.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The request for a preliminary ruling should contain: (a) a summary of the subject matter of the dispute and the relevant findings of fact... (b) the tenor of any national provisions applicable in the case... (c) an account of the reasons which led the referring court or tribunal to raise the question of the interpretation or validity of certain provisions of EU law... and (d) the questions referred to the Court of Justice for a preliminary ruling." (Recommendations 2024/C/6008, Para 15).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Solo advocates appearing in national courts use this template to draft "Applications for Reference." While the judge ultimately signs the order, the advocate's draft often forms the basis of the reference. Successfully referring a point of EU law is a key strategy for challenging national measures that conflict with EU rights.
