## Authority Tier
Primary — Brussels Ia Recast (directly applicable in all Member States; UK now governed by post-Brexit arrangements separately).

## Currency Warning
**Brussels Ia abolished the exequatur** (Art 39). A judgment given in a Member State which is enforceable in that Member State is enforceable in the other Member States *without any declaration of enforceability being required*. Refusal grounds remain under Arts 45-46 but must be invoked by the debtor.

## Template Type
Enforcement Certificate Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Identification of the Judgment** — Court of origin · case number · date · operative part to be enforced.
2. **Annex I Certificate** — issued by the court of origin upon application by the interested party:
   - Section 1: Court of origin and parties.
   - Section 2: Type of decision (judgment · court settlement · authentic instrument).
   - Section 3: Operative provisions to be enforced (monetary or non-monetary).
   - Section 4: Provisional / protective measures status.
   - Section 5: Costs of the proceedings.
3. **Service on the Defendant** (Art 43) — Before enforcement, the certificate and the judgment must be served on the person against whom enforcement is sought. Translation requirements per Art 43(2).
4. **Application for Enforcement** — Direct application to the competent enforcement authority of the Member State of enforcement (no separate exequatur).
5. **Adaptation (Art 54)** — Where a judgment contains a measure not known in the enforcement state, the court adapts it to a measure with equivalent effects.
6. **Refusal of Recognition / Enforcement (Arts 45-46)** — Grounds available to the debtor:
   - Manifestly contrary to public policy of the enforcement state.
   - Default judgment without sufficient time to defend.
   - Irreconcilable with an earlier judgment between the same parties.
   - Lack of jurisdiction in insurance / consumer / employment / exclusive-jurisdiction matters.
7. **Stay of Enforcement** — Available where the judgment is challenged in the state of origin (Art 38).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Annex I Certificate** is the gateway document — without it the enforcement authority will not act.
- **Direct enforcement** — no national declaration of enforceability needed.
- **Service of the certificate + judgment** before any enforcement measure (other than protective measures under Art 40).
- **Translation** — required only on the debtor's request or where the enforcement authority demands it (limited to the certificate's operative provisions in most cases).
- **Authentic instruments and court settlements** are also enforceable under Brussels Ia (Arts 58-60) using Annexes II and III.
- **Provisional / protective measures** (Art 35) — courts may grant such measures available under their own law even if another Member State has jurisdiction on the substance.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A judgment given in a Member State which is enforceable in that Member State shall be enforceable in the other Member States without any declaration of enforceability being required." (Brussels Ia Recast, Art 39.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The default cross-border enforcement route for civil and commercial Member State judgments. Solo practitioners pursuing intra-EU collection should request the Annex I certificate from the court of origin at the time of obtaining judgment to avoid later delays. Adaptation under Art 54 is the practical bottleneck where the original measure (e.g., a UK-style search order, an Italian sequestro conservativo) has no direct equivalent.
