# EU NIS2 — Significant Incident Notification Template

## Source URL
[EU NIS2 Directive (Directive (EU) 2022/2555) — Article 23](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32022L2555#d1e1935-1-1)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under the NIS2 Directive.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. The 24-hour "Early Warning" is a strict deadline that must be met to avoid potential administrative fines for non-compliance with reporting obligations.

## Template Type
Compliance / Emergency Response Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Stage 1: Early Warning (Within 24 hours of awareness)**
   - Identification of the Entity and contact person.
   - Initial description of the incident (type, affected systems).
   - Indication if suspected to be caused by unlawful or malicious acts.
   - Preliminary assessment of cross-border impact (potential to affect other Member States).
2. **Stage 2: Incident Notification (Within 72 hours of awareness)**
   - Update to the early warning.
   - Evaluation of severity and impact (Operational, Financial, or Third-Party).
   - Identification of available Indicators of Compromise (IoCs).
   - Initial mitigation steps taken.
3. **Stage 3: Final Report (Within 1 month of resolution)**
   - Detailed description of the incident.
   - Root Cause Analysis (Technical or human factors).
   - Applied and planned mitigation measures.
   - Detailed assessment of cross-border and total impact.
4. **Notification to Service Recipients (Without undue delay)**
   - Clear and simple description of the incident.
   - Advice on measures the recipients can take to mitigate risks (Art 23(2)).

## Drafting Conventions
- **The "Clock" Rule**: The timeline starts from the moment of **awareness**, not completion of investigation.
- **Significant Incident Criteria**: Must meet the Art 23(3) threshold (severe operational disruption, financial loss, or considerable damage to others).
- **Parallel Reporting**: If the incident involves personal data, a separate GDPR breach notification must be filed with the DPA within 72 hours.
- **Malicious Act Flag**: Explicitly state if the incident appears to be a state-sponsored attack or criminal activity, as this triggers different responses from the CSIRT.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Member States shall ensure that essential and important entities notify, without undue delay, the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority... of any incident that has a significant impact on the provision of their services... The notification shall include: (a) an early warning, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours... (b) an incident notification... within 72 hours... (c) a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification." (NIS2 Directive, Article 23).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to build an "Incident Response Plan" for clients in critical sectors (e.g., energy, transport, digital infrastructure). Solo advocates help clients set up a "triage" system that ensures the 24-hour Early Warning is filed even if technical details are still emerging.
