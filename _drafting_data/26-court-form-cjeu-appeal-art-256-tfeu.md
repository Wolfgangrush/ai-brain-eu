## Authority Tier
Primary — Treaty provision + Statute of the Court + Rules of Procedure.

## Currency Warning
**Filtering mechanism (in force since 1 May 2019)**: appeals against decisions of independent EU appeal boards (EUIPO · CPVO · ECHA · EASA) require *prior allowance* by the Court under Article 58a of the Statute. The applicant must demonstrate that the appeal raises an issue significant to the unity, consistency, or development of EU law.

## Template Type
Pleading / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header** — Designation of the Court of Justice · case number of the General Court judgment · appellant + respondent details.
2. **Subject-Matter** — Identification of the General Court judgment appealed (date · case number · operative part contested).
3. **Admissibility**
   - Time-limit: appeal must be brought within **2 months + 10 days** of notification of the General Court's decision.
   - For Art 58a (appeal-board appeals): separate Request for Allowance to Proceed, max 7 pages.
4. **Pleas in Law on Appeal** — Points of law ONLY. The Court of Justice does not re-examine facts. Permissible grounds:
   - Lack of competence of the General Court.
   - Breach of procedure before it adversely affecting the appellant's interests.
   - Infringement of EU law by the General Court.
5. **Statement of Arguments** — For each plea, identify the contested paragraphs of the General Court judgment and the legal error.
6. **Form of Order Sought**
   - Set aside the General Court judgment in whole or in part.
   - Give final judgment (where the state of proceedings permits) OR refer back to the General Court.
   - Costs.
7. **Evidence Offered** — Limited; no new evidence on the facts. Documentary references to the General Court file.
8. **Signature** — Lawyer entitled to practise before a Member State court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **25-page limit** for appeals before the Court of Justice (15 pages for replies).
- **e-Curia** filing mandatory.
- **Cross-appeal** — respondent may lodge a cross-appeal in the response (separate from the response on the merits).
- **No new pleas** unless the General Court infringed procedural rules.
- **Request for Allowance to Proceed** under Art 58a — separate 7-page document filed with the appeal.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An appeal to the Court of Justice shall be limited to points of law. It shall lie on the grounds of lack of competence of the General Court, a breach of procedure before it which adversely affects the interests of the appellant as well as the infringement of Union law by the General Court." (Statute of the Court of Justice, Art 58.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
For clients who lost at the General Court level (state aid, antitrust, trade-defence cases) and need to escalate. The Art 58a filter means EUIPO trade-mark and design appeals now require a separate gateway request — practitioners often lose at the allowance stage. Drafting must be ruthlessly point-of-law-only; any factual re-argument will be struck.
