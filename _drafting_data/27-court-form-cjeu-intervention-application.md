## Authority Tier
Primary — Statute of the Court of Justice + Rules of Procedure.

## Currency Warning
The application to intervene must be lodged within **6 weeks** of publication of the notice of the action in the Official Journal. Late applications may still be admitted on showing of an interest but the intervener is then confined to supporting the arguments of the party already pleaded.

## Template Type
Pleading / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header** — Designation of the Court · case number and parties to the main action · prospective intervener details + lawyer.
2. **Identification of the Main Action** — Brief recital of the parties and subject-matter.
3. **Interest in the Result of the Case**
   - For Member States / EU institutions: automatic right; no interest needs to be shown.
   - For private parties: direct and existing interest in the outcome of the case must be substantiated (not merely an interest in the legal principles).
4. **Party to be Supported** — Which side (applicant or defendant) the intervener supports.
5. **Form of Order Sought by the Intervener** — Must mirror or be subsidiary to the supported party's form of order.
6. **Pleas in Law and Arguments** — May supplement but not enlarge the dispute beyond what the supported party has pleaded.
7. **Confidentiality Request (if any)** — Identification of items the intervener seeks to keep confidential vis-à-vis the other parties.
8. **Annexes** — Documents evidencing the interest claimed.
9. **Signature** — Lawyer entitled to practise before a Member State court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **10-page limit** for the application to intervene.
- **e-Curia** filing.
- **Statement in intervention** — once admitted, the intervener files a separate statement (max 20 pages) within the time-limit set by the President.
- **Costs** — Each party usually bears its own costs of intervention unless circumstances warrant otherwise.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Member States and institutions of the Union may intervene in cases before the Court of Justice. The same right shall be open to the bodies, offices and agencies of the Union and to any other person which can establish an interest in the result of a case submitted to the Court." (Statute of the Court of Justice, Art 40.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Frequently used by industry associations and competitors in state-aid, antitrust, and DMA cases. Practitioners should focus on what their client adds — economic data, sector-specific evidence, fundamental-rights perspective — rather than legal arguments already pleaded by the main parties.
