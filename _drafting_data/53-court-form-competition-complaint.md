# EU Competition Complaint — Form C Template

## Source URL
[Commission Regulation (EC) No 773/2004 — Annex (Form C)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32004R0773)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory form under Commission Regulation governing competition proceedings.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Note that while Form C is for formal complaints to the European Commission, many cases are now handled by National Competition Authorities (NCAs) under the ECN+ Directive.

## Template Type
Court Form / Regulatory Complaint

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Information Regarding the Complainant**
   - Identity: Name, address, legal status.
   - Business Activities: Concise overview of the complainant's position in the market.
   - Contact Person: Authorized representative details.
2. **Information Regarding the Undertaking(s) Complained Of**
   - Identification of the target company/companies.
   - Relationship: Complainant's role relative to the target (Competitor, Customer, etc.).
3. **Details of the Alleged Infringement**
   - The Facts: Chronological description of the conduct.
   - Legal Basis: Art 101 (Agreements/Cartels) or Art 102 (Abuse of Dominance) TFEU.
   - Products/Services Affected: Specific market definition.
4. **Evidence**
   - List of documents, witness statements, or data supporting the allegations.
5. **Finding Sought and Legitimate Interest**
   - Finding Sought: Specific request (e.g., "Find an infringement and order it to cease").
   - Legitimate Interest: Explanation of how the complainant is adversely affected.
6. **Proceedings before Other Authorities**
   - Disclosure of any parallel actions before NCAs or national courts.
7. **Confidentiality**
   - Identification of business secrets and provision of a non-confidential version.
8. **Declaration and Signature**
   - Statement of good faith.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Legitimate Interest**: This is a threshold requirement. The complainant must show they are "directly and adversely affected" to have standing for a formal decision.
- **Form C Rigor**: If the complaint does not follow the Form C structure, the Commission is not legally obliged to issue a formal rejection decision (limiting the complainant's right to appeal to the CJEU).
- **Non-Confidential Version**: Mandatory if any information is marked as secret.
- **Physical Submission**: Traditionally requires three paper copies plus electronic submission.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Complaints shall contain the information required by Form C, as set out in the Annex... The Commission may also require the complainant to provide a non-confidential version of the complaint... Complainants shall demonstrate a legitimate interest for the purposes of Article 7(2) of Regulation (EC) No 1/2003." (Regulation 773/2004, Article 5).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary tool for small companies or solo practitioners to "blow the whistle" on large market players engaging in anti-competitive behavior. Ensuring the complaint is a "Formal Form C Complaint" protects the client's procedural rights, including the right to be heard and the right to appeal a dismissal.
