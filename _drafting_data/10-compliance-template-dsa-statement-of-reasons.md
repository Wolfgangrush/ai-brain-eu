# DSA — Statement of Reasons Template (Art 17)

## Source URL
- [EU Digital Services Act (Regulation (EU) 2022/2065) — Article 17](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32022R2065#d1e3427-1-1)
- [Commission Guidance on the Statement of Reasons](https://ec.europa.eu/digital-building-blocks/sites/display/DIGITAL/DSA+Transparency+Database)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under DSA Article 17.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Online platforms must ensure these statements are provided on a "durable medium" and (for larger platforms) submitted to the Commission's Transparency Database.

## Template Type
Compliance / Communication Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Decision Summary**
   - Type of Restriction (Removal, disabling access, demotion, monetization restriction, or account suspension/termination).
   - Territorial Scope (Member State-specific or global).
   - Duration (Permanent or temporary).
2. **Facts and Circumstances**
   - Identification of the specific content or account.
   - Source of Detection: User notice (Art 16), Trusted Flagger, or voluntary investigation.
   - Use of Automation: Explicit statement if automated means were used for detection or decision-making.
3. **Legal or Contractual Grounds**
   - Illegal Content: Reference to specific national or EU law and explanation of why the content is illegal.
   - Terms of Service (ToS) Violation: Reference to the specific contractual clause and explanation of the incompatibility.
4. **Redress Mechanisms**
   - Internal Complaint-Handling: Procedures for appealing to the platform (Art 20).
   - Out-of-Court Dispute Settlement: Information on certified mediation bodies (Art 21).
   - Judicial Redress: Right to bring the case before a national court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Specificity**: The statement must be "clear and specific." Vague references to "community standards" without identifying the specific rule are insufficient.
- **Automation Transparency**: If an algorithm made the decision, the user must be informed (crucial for "shadow-banning" or automated takedowns).
- **Machine-Readability**: For platforms required to report to the Transparency Database, the statement should follow the JSON-based reporting schema.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The statement of reasons shall at least include the following information: (a) a decision as to whether the information is removed... (b) the facts and circumstances... including whether the decision was taken on the basis of a notice... or on the basis of voluntary own-initiative investigations... (c) where applicable, information on the use of automated means in taking the decision..." (DSA Art 17(3)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used by legal counsel for digital platforms to design automated compliance workflows or for advocates representing users to challenge unfair content moderation. A "boiler-plate" rejection without the Article 17 elements is a prima facie violation of the DSA.
