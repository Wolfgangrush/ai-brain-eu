# EUIPO — Trade Mark Application Template

## Source URL
- [EU Trade Mark Regulation (EUTMR) (EU) 2017/1001 — Article 31](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32017R1001)
- [Implementing Regulation (EUTMIR) (EU) 2018/626 — Article 2](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32018R0626)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory regulation and Implementing Regulation for EU Trade Marks.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. A filing date is only granted if the minimum requirements (Art 31 EUTMR) are met. The application fee must be paid within one month of filing.

## Template Type
Court Form / Intellectual Property

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Applicant Identification**
   - Full name, address, nationality, and legal seat.
   - Identification of the representative (mandatory for non-EU/EEA applicants).
2. **Representation of the Mark**
   - Selection of Mark Type (Word, Figurative, Shape, Position, Pattern, Sound, Motion, Multimedia, Hologram).
   - Technical file (JPEG, MP3, MP4) complying with Art 3 EUTMIR.
   - Indication of colors (if applicable).
   - Optional text description.
3. **List of Goods and Services**
   - Grouped by Nice Classification classes.
   - Terms must be "clear and precise" (using the Harmonised Database is recommended).
4. **Priority and Seniority Claims (Optional)**
   - Convention Priority (Art 34 EUTMR): Previous filing date, number, and country.
   - Seniority (Art 39 EUTMR): Previous identical national mark in an EU Member State.
5. **Language Selection**
   - First Language: Any of the 24 official EU languages.
   - Second Language: One of the five Office languages (EN, FR, DE, IT, ES).
6. **Collective or Certification Mark Declaration**
   - Indication if the mark is a collective or certification mark.

## Drafting Conventions
- **The "Clear and Precise" Rule**: Goods and services must be defined specifically. Vague terms like "all products in class 9" are rejected.
- **Language of the Case**: The second language serves as a potential language for opposition or cancellation proceedings.
- **Electronic Filing**: Highly recommended for faster processing and lower fees (€850 for electronic vs €1000 for paper for one class).
- **Fast Track**: Applications using only terms from the EUIPO's Harmonised Database qualify for accelerated "Fast Track" examination.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"An application for an EU trade mark shall contain: (a) a request for the registration... (b) information identifying the applicant; (c) a list of the goods or services... (d) a representation of the mark, which satisfies the requirements set out in Article 4(b)." (EUTMR Art 31).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The starting point for protecting a client's brand across the entire EU single market. Solo advocates use this template to ensure the initial filing secures a "filing date" (the priority date), which is the most critical asset in trademark law.
