# Distribution Agreement — VBER 2022/720 Compliance

## Source URL
- [Commission Regulation (EU) 2022/720 (VBER)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022R0720)
- [Vertical Guidelines 2022/C 248/01](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52022XC0630(01))

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — VBER + Vertical Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
Distribution agreements (exclusive · selective · non-exclusive) fall under VBER provided both parties' market share ≤ 30%. New Guidelines clarify online sales: dual pricing between online and offline channels is permissible in limited circumstances; outright prohibition on sales via online marketplaces requires careful justification.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Supplier + Distributor (independent buyer-reseller).
2. **Recitals** — Products · Territory · Distributor's reseller status (not agent).
3. **Definitions** — "Products" · "Territory" · "Customers" · "Net Sales" · "Marketing Plan".
4. **Distribution Model**
   - **Exclusive distribution**: only one distributor per territory; supplier may also sell in territory subject to dual-distribution rules.
   - **Selective distribution**: appointment of resellers meeting qualitative / quantitative criteria.
   - **Non-exclusive / open**: multiple distributors permitted.
5. **Supply Terms**
   - Pricing — supplier's list price; recommended resale price permitted, minimum / fixed RPM is hardcore.
   - Minimum purchase commitments (acceptable).
   - Delivery terms (Incoterms 2020).
   - Payment terms.
6. **Distributor Obligations**
   - Best efforts to promote and sell.
   - Maintain service / spare parts availability.
   - Provide market intelligence and sales reports.
   - Comply with supplier's quality standards.
7. **Territorial Restrictions (VBER Art 4)**
   - **Permitted**: restriction of active sales by the distributor into territories exclusively allocated to other distributors / reserved for supplier.
   - **Hardcore**: restriction of passive sales (unsolicited responses to customer requests) into other distributors' territories.
   - **Online sales**: cannot prohibit; restrictions on online marketplaces must be proportionate.
8. **Selective Distribution Criteria** (where applicable)
   - Qualitative: trained sales staff, after-sales service, store ambience for prestige goods.
   - Quantitative: number of distributors per territory.
   - Cross-supplies within the selective network must be permitted.
9. **IP and Marks**
   - Limited license to use Marks for resale and advertising.
   - Approval of marketing materials.
10. **Confidentiality + Competition Law Compliance**
    - No exchange of competitively sensitive information that goes beyond what is necessary for the vertical agreement.
11. **Term and Termination**
    - Term + renewal.
    - Termination for cause + convenience.
    - Post-termination obligations — return of materials · transfer of customer contracts · final settlement.
12. **Choice of Law / Forum** — Rome I + Brussels Ia.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Market share check** — VBER 30% threshold for both parties.
- **Hardcore restrictions** — even one disqualifies the whole agreement from block exemption.
- **Hub-and-spoke risk** — supplier as conduit for horizontal collusion between distributors (Eturas C-74/14).
- **MFN clauses** — wide MFN (across all sales channels) restricted; narrow MFN (price-parity within supplier's own direct online channel) permissible.
- **Sustainability targets** — permissible if proportionate; cannot be used as cover for cartel.
- **Online platform bans** — must be objectively justified (luxury goods · authorised reseller protection); a per-se ban likely fails proportionality after Coty (C-230/16).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Regulation shall not apply to vertical agreements... which contain any of the following restrictions, which, directly or indirectly, in isolation or in combination with other factors under the control of the parties, have as their object: (a) the restriction of the buyer's ability to determine its sale price..." (Regulation 2022/720, Art 4(a).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Distribution disputes are commercially valuable — solo practitioners frequently represent SMEs caught in cross-border distribution networks. The most common litigation triggers are (a) supplier termination of distributor without notice (compensation claims) and (b) Art 4 hardcore restrictions hidden in territorial or pricing clauses. Pressure-test every clause against the VBER hardcore list before signature.
