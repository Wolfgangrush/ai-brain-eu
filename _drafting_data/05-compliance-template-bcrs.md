# Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) — Group Data Transfer Template

## Source URL
- [EDPB Recommendations 1/2022 on the referential for Controller BCRs](https://edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/recommendations/recommendations-12022-referential-noble-elements-and_en)
- [EDPB Recommendations 1/2026 on the referential for Processor BCRs (Draft)](https://edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/recommendations/recommendations-12026-referential-noble-elements-and_en)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - EDPB Recommendations (approved referential for Art 47 GDPR).

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. All existing BCRs must be aligned with the 2023 update (Recommendations 1/2022) by their 2024 annual update. Processor BCRs are currently being aligned under the 1/2026 draft recommendations.

## Template Type
Compliance / Contractual Framework

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Part 1: Application Form**
   - Identification of the Group and the "Lead" Supervisory Authority.
   - Description of the group structure and the nature of data transfers.
2. **Part 2: Background Paper (Binding Nature)**
   - Mechanism for legal binding (e.g., Intra-Group Agreement - IGA).
   - Evidence of binding nature on employees and group members.
   - Description of the training and audit programs.
3. **Part 3: Table of Elements & Principles**
   - **Data Protection Principles**: Lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation.
   - **Rights of Data Subjects**: Access, rectification, erasure, objection, automated decision-making.
   - **Third-Party Beneficiary Rights**: Mechanism for individuals to enforce BCRs in EU courts.
   - **Liability and Burden of Proof**: EU group member accepts liability for violations by non-EU members.
   - **Complaint Handling Process**: Internal group mechanism + right to lodge DPA complaints.
   - **Security and Confidentiality**: Technical and organizational measures.
   - **Onward Transfers**: Rules for transferring data outside the BCR-bound group.
   - **Governance and Oversight**: DPO role, audit frequency, and reporting.
   - **Schrems II Compliance**: 
     - Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) protocol.
     - Government Access Request procedure (notification and challenge).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Uniformity**: BCRs must apply to all group members globally without exception.
- **Enforceability**: Must be legally binding under the law of an EU Member State.
- **Transparency**: The BCRs (or a meaningful summary) must be published on the group's website.
- **Reporting**: Any changes to the BCRs or the list of group members must be reported annually to the Lead Authority.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Binding corporate rules... shall at least specify: (a) the structure and contact details of the group... (b) the data transfers... (c) their legally binding nature, both internally and externally... (d) the application of the general data protection principles... (e) the rights of data subjects..." (GDPR Art 47(2)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used by large multinational clients (or group-structured law firms) as a "gold standard" for internal data flows. Solo advocates advise on the "Table of Elements" to ensure the group's internal policies meet the EDPB's strict 2023/2026 referential requirements.
