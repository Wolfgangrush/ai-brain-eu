## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Directives (transposed in each Member State; e-procurement obligatory since 2018).

## Currency Warning
**Financial thresholds** revised every 2 years (most recent: 1 January 2024 — Commission Implementing Regulations 2023/2495 + 2023/2497 + 2023/2496). 2024-2025 thresholds: works EUR 5,538,000 · supplies/services (central authorities) EUR 143,000 · supplies/services (sub-central) EUR 221,000 · concessions EUR 5,538,000. Below-threshold procurement still subject to general TFEU principles where cross-border interest exists.

## Template Type
Bid / Tender Submission

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Contract Identification**
   - Contracting authority name + Member State.
   - Tender reference (OJEU notice number · TED publication).
   - Subject-matter · estimated value · CPV code.
2. **Bidder Identification**
   - Legal entity (or consortium leader for joint bids).
   - Member State of registration.
   - VAT / EORI / business identifiers.
   - Authorised signatory.
3. **ESPD (European Single Procurement Document)**
   - Online declaration of compliance with exclusion grounds (Art 57 Directive).
   - Selection criteria self-declaration.
   - Reliance on capacities of other entities.
4. **Exclusion Grounds (Art 57)**
   - **Mandatory**: criminal convictions (corruption · fraud · terrorism · money laundering · child labour · tax / social-security debts where established).
   - **Discretionary**: serious professional misconduct · conflict of interest · prior contract termination for material breach.
   - Self-cleaning measures (Art 57(6)) — documented remediation.
5. **Selection Criteria (Art 58)**
   - Suitability to pursue the activity.
   - Economic and financial standing (turnover · insurance).
   - Technical and professional ability (reference projects · qualifications · machinery).
6. **Award Criteria (Art 67)**
   - **MEAT** (Most Economically Advantageous Tender) — required.
   - Price + quality factors weighted; sustainability / environmental / social factors permissible.
   - Lowest-price-only permitted only in narrow categories.
7. **Tender Documents**
   - Technical specifications response.
   - Implementation timeline + project management methodology.
   - Pricing schedule (fixed price · unit rates · cost-plus per RFP terms).
   - Reference projects + CVs of key personnel.
8. **Bid Security / Performance Security** — Per RFP terms (typically 1-3% of bid value as tender security; 5-10% performance security).
9. **Variants** (where permitted by RFP) — Alternative technical or commercial proposals.
10. **Sub-contracting Plan** — Identified sub-contractors per Art 71.
11. **Confidentiality** — Identification of business secrets within the bid.
12. **Validity Period** — Minimum bid validity (typically 90-180 days).

## Drafting Conventions
- **eIDAS-compliant electronic signature** where MS has implemented e-procurement.
- **TED filing** for bids above thresholds — bidder must monitor publication and clarification responses.
- **Standstill period** — 10-day minimum between award notification and contract conclusion (Remedies Directive Art 2a); 15 days for paper notifications.
- **Modification limits** post-award (Art 72) — material modifications require new procurement.
- **Remedies** — pre-contractual review · damages action · ineffectiveness declaration.
- **Conflict-of-interest declarations** mandatory.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Contracting authorities shall base the award of public contracts on the most economically advantageous tender. The most economically advantageous tender from the point of view of the contracting authority shall be identified on the basis of the price or cost, using a cost-effectiveness approach, such as life-cycle costing in accordance with Article 68, and may include the best price-quality ratio..." (Directive 2014/24/EU, Art 67(1) + (2).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Public-procurement practice splits between bidder-side support (drafting bids · responding to clarifications · post-award disputes) and contracting-authority-side advice. Solo practitioners frequently litigate procurement challenges in the 10-day standstill window. ESPD compliance is the most common bid-rejection ground — practitioners should walk clients through the form question-by-question, particularly the exclusion-grounds attestation.
