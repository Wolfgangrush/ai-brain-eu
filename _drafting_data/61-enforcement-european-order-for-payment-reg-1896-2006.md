# European Order for Payment Procedure (EOP)

## Source URL

- [Commission Regulation (EC) No 936/2012 — Standard Forms](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32012R0936)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable in all Member States except Denmark).

## Currency Warning
EOP is available for **uncontested pecuniary cross-border claims** of any value (Reg 936/2012 removed the previous EUR threshold). Denmark excluded. Online filing supported in most Member States via national portals; e-Justice provides the standard Annex I form.

## Template Type
Court Form / Standard EU Procedure

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Form A — Application for an EOP (Annex I)**
   - Court (jurisdiction per Brussels Ia, normally debtor's domicile).
   - Parties — claimant + defendant (full identification + cross-border element).
   - Claim — principal · interest (rate · starting date · whether contractual or statutory) · contractual penalty (if any) · costs.
   - Cause of action — facts giving rise to the claim · subject-matter description.
   - Evidence — list of evidence in support · type of evidence.
   - Cross-border nature — Member States of claimant and defendant must differ from the Member State of the court (or claim-element must cross a border).
2. **Form B — Request for the Claimant to Complete and/or Rectify** (issued by court if needed).
3. **Form C — Proposal to Modify** (court may propose partial issuance).
4. **Form D — Decision Rejecting the Application** (no appeal; ordinary national proceedings remain available).
5. **Form E — European Order for Payment** — issued where conditions met (typically within 30 days).
6. **Form F — Statement of Opposition** — defendant has 30 days from service to oppose; opposition automatically transfers the case to ordinary civil proceedings unless claimant has requested otherwise.
7. **Form G — Declaration of Enforceability** — issued where no opposition lodged.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard forms mandatory** — Annex I-G of Reg 1896/2006 (updated by Reg 936/2012).
- **Pecuniary claims only** — non-monetary claims excluded.
- **No examination of merits** by the issuing court — only formal / facial sufficiency check.
- **Translation** — standard forms available in all 24 official languages.
- **Direct enforcement** post-Form G — no exequatur needed.
- **Review in exceptional cases (Art 20)** — debtor may seek review where service was defective or claim was paid before service.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Regulation creates a European order for payment procedure for the simplification, speeding up and reduction of the costs of litigation in cross-border cases concerning uncontested pecuniary claims." (Regulation 1896/2006, Art 1.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The fastest EU cross-border collection route for liquidated debts where the debtor is unlikely to oppose — typically B2B invoice claims. Solo practitioners use this for clients trading across borders without the cost of full litigation. Be alert: 30-day opposition window converts the matter into full national proceedings, so factor that risk into client estimates.
