# Records of Processing Activities (ROPA) Template

## Source URL

- [CNIL ROPA Template (Exemplary SA Resource)](https://www.cnil.fr/en/record-processing-activities)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Statutory requirement under GDPR Art 30.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Note that the 250-employee exemption (Art 30(5)) is interpreted strictly by the EDPB; most organizations (including small law firms) must maintain a ROPA if their processing is not "occasional."

## Template Type
Compliance / Regulatory Template

## Structural Skeleton
### Part A: Controller Record (Art 30(1))
1. **Contact Details**
   - Controller name and contact info
   - Joint Controller(s) (if any)
   - Representative (if non-EU based)
   - Data Protection Officer (DPO)
2. **Purposes of Processing** (e.g., HR, Marketing, Client Matter Management)
3. **Categories of Data Subjects** (e.g., Employees, Clients, Potential Leads)
4. **Categories of Personal Data** (e.g., Identity, Financial, Special Category/Sensitive)
5. **Categories of Recipients** (Internal departments, Processors, Third parties)
6. **International Transfers**
   - Destination country
   - Transfer mechanism (SCCs, Adequacy, BCRs, etc.)
7. **Retention Periods** (Envisaged time limits for erasure for each category)
8. **Security Measures** (General description of TOMs)

### Part B: Processor Record (Art 30(2))
1. **Processor Details** (Name and contact info)
2. **Controller Details** (For each controller on whose behalf the processor acts)
3. **Categories of Processing** (Description of the service provided)
4. **International Transfers** (Performed on behalf of the controller)
5. **Security Measures** (General description of TOMs)

## Drafting Conventions
- **Activity-Based Recording**: Group processing operations by business function (e.g., "Payroll," "Litigation Support") rather than by individual file.
- **Granularity**: Descriptions should be specific enough for a regulator to understand the risk but can use "categories" (e.g., "contact details" instead of listing "email, phone, address").
- **Dynamic Maintenance**: The ROPA is a living document and must be updated whenever a new processing activity starts.
- **Availability**: Must be available to the Supervisory Authority upon request (Art 30(4)).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"Each controller... shall maintain a record of processing activities under its responsibility. That record shall contain all of the following information... (a) the name and contact details of the controller... (b) the purposes of the processing... (c) a description of the categories of data subjects and of the categories of personal data..." (GDPR Art 30(1)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
A foundational compliance document for the firm itself (acting as a controller of client/employee data) and for advising clients. It serves as the "map" for all other GDPR obligations (DPIAs, privacy notices, data subject rights).
