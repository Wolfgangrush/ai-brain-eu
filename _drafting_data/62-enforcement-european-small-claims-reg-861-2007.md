2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Regulation (directly applicable in all Member States except Denmark).

## Currency Warning
Threshold raised to **EUR 5,000** by Regulation 2015/2421 (in force 14 July 2017). Procedure is **written by default**; oral hearing only on request or where the court considers it indispensable, ordinarily via videoconference.

## Template Type
Court Form / Standard EU Procedure

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Form A — Claim Form (Annex I)**
   - Court with jurisdiction (Brussels Ia rules apply).
   - Parties — claimant + defendant.
   - Cross-border element — at least one party domiciled or habitually resident in a Member State other than the court's.
   - Claim — pecuniary or non-pecuniary; capped at EUR 5,000 (excluding interest, expenses, and costs).
   - Cause of action.
   - Evidence — schedule + indication of any oral evidence sought.
2. **Form B — Request to Complete or Rectify** (issued by court if Form A insufficient).
3. **Form C — Answer Form** — defendant served by court; 30 days to respond.
4. **Form D — Certificate Concerning a Judgment** — for cross-border enforcement.
5. **Court Conduct**
   - Court forwards Form A to defendant within 14 days.
   - Defendant's reply within 30 days.
   - Court may seek further information from either party (30-day deadline).
   - Judgment within 30 days of receipt of all evidence.
6. **Costs** — successful party may recover; principle of proportionality applies.
7. **Appeal** — governed by the procedural law of the Member State of the court of origin.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard forms mandatory** — Annex I-IV of Regulation 2017/1259.
- **EUR 5,000 cap** — claims above must use ordinary or EOP procedure.
- **Written procedure default** — practitioners should anticipate no oral hearing unless specifically warranted.
- **Direct enforcement** — Form D certificate enables cross-border enforcement without exequatur.
- **Translation** — only documents necessary for understanding the claim need be translated; cost-control mechanism.
- **Online filing** — available where the Member State permits.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Regulation establishes a European procedure for small claims (hereinafter referred to as the European Small Claims Procedure), intended to simplify and speed up litigation concerning small claims in cross-border cases, and to reduce costs. The European Small Claims Procedure shall be available to litigants as an alternative to the procedures existing under the laws of the Member States." (Regulation 861/2007, Art 1.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The ESCP is widely under-used. Solo practitioners running consumer-side practices (cross-border online purchases · short-stay rentals · low-value B2B disputes) find this the most cost-effective EU litigation tool. The written-only default keeps fees down; the EUR 5,000 cap covers most consumer disputes.
