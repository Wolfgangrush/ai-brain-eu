## Authority Tier
Primary — EUTMR + Delegated Regulation.

## Currency Warning
Appeals against EUIPO first-instance decisions (Examination · Opposition · Cancellation · Designs) are heard by the Boards of Appeal. Further appeal lies to the General Court under Art 263 TFEU and (subject to Art 58a allowance) to the Court of Justice. The **Grand Board** sits where uniformity is in issue or where the President refers a case.

## Template Type
Notice of Appeal / Statement of Grounds

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Notice of Appeal (filed within 2 months of contested decision)**
   - Identification of contested decision (number · date · operative part).
   - Appellant identification (name · address · representative · authority to act).
   - Statement that an appeal is being filed.
   - Appeal fee payment (EUR 720 for EUTM matters · designs lower).
2. **Statement of Grounds (filed within 4 months of contested decision)**
   - Subject-matter of the appeal.
   - Grounds in law and fact.
   - For each ground:
     - Legal basis (EUTMR article · Common Practice Communication).
     - Established Board / General Court / CJEU case-law.
     - Application to the contested decision.
   - **New evidence** — admissible at appeal stage subject to Art 27(4) Delegated Reg 2018/625 (only if relevant + reasoned justification + not raised at first instance).
3. **Form of Order Sought**
   - Annul / amend the contested decision.
   - Specific relief (registration · refusal · cancellation · cost reallocation).
4. **Procedural Requests**
   - Oral hearing (Art 96 EUTMR) — must be specifically requested.
   - Suspension pending parallel proceedings.
   - Restitutio in integrum (where the appellant missed a time-limit) under Art 104 EUTMR.
5. **Annexes**
   - Power of attorney.
   - Contested decision.
   - New evidence (where admissible).
   - Translations (working language: EN · DE · FR · ES · IT chosen by appellant from the EUIPO five languages).
6. **Signature** of professional representative authorised under Art 120 EUTMR.

## Drafting Conventions
- **e-filing via the EUIPO User Area** — mandatory.
- **Page limit** — no fixed limit, but Boards encourage concision (typically 20-30 pages).
- **Working language** — one of the 5 EUIPO languages; choice often dictated by the language of the contested decision.
- **Professional representation** — required for parties not domiciled in the EEA (Art 120 EUTMR); for EEA parties, voluntary but conventional.
- **Cross-appeal** — respondent may file with their response (within 2 months of service of statement of grounds).
- **Mediation** — EUIPO Mediation Centre may suspend appeal for negotiated outcome.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An appeal shall lie from decisions of any of the decision-making instances of the Office... Notice of appeal must be filed in writing at the Office within two months of the date of notification of the decision. The notice shall be deemed to have been filed only when the fee for appeal has been paid. A written statement setting out the grounds of appeal must be filed within four months of the date of notification of the decision." (EUTMR, Art 68.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
EUIPO appeals are the bread-and-butter of EU IP practice. Solo practitioners must rigorously front-load grounds in the 4-month statement — Boards routinely refuse late-raised arguments. The Art 27(4) new-evidence test is the principal contested procedural point on EUIPO appeals; practitioners must justify why the evidence could not have been adduced at first instance.
