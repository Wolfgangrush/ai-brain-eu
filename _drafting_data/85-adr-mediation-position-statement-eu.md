# Mediation Position Statement — Cross-Border (Directive 2008/52/EC)

## Source URL
- [Directive 2008/52/EC on certain aspects of mediation in civil and commercial matters](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32008L0052)
- [European Code of Conduct for Mediators](https://commission.europa.eu/system/files/2018-12/adr_ec_code_conduct_en.pdf)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## Authority Tier
Primary — EU Directive (transposed in all Member State civil-procedure codes for cross-border disputes; many Member States extend domestic application).

## Currency Warning
The Directive applies to **cross-border civil and commercial disputes** (Art 1(2)). Member States must ensure mediated settlement agreements are enforceable (Art 6) — typically by court approval or notarial deed. Confidentiality protection (Art 7) prohibits mediator giving evidence on mediation content in subsequent proceedings.

## Template Type
Pleading / Position Paper (without-prejudice)

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header**
   - Mediator's name + accreditation.
   - Parties (full identification + cross-border element).
   - Date of mediation session.
   - Marking: **"WITHOUT PREJUDICE — FOR MEDIATOR ONLY"** (Art 7).
2. **Subject-Matter of the Dispute**
   - Concise narrative of the underlying contractual / tortious / regulatory dispute.
3. **Procedural Posture**
   - Court / arbitration proceedings already on foot (court of seizing · case number).
   - Pre-action stage (no proceedings yet).
   - Any limitation period concerns (Art 8 — mediation suspends limitation in many Member States).
4. **Client's Position on the Merits**
   - Key facts asserted.
   - Legal basis (governing law + key Treaty / regulation / Member State law provisions).
   - Established case-law supporting client position.
5. **Quantum**
   - Claim valuation: principal · interest · costs.
   - Cost-of-litigation estimate (avoidable by settlement).
6. **Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement (BATNA)**
   - Likely outcome at trial / award.
   - Time to judgment.
   - Enforcement risk (cross-border — solvency · asset location).
7. **Settlement Parameters**
   - Aspirational outcome.
   - Walk-away threshold (confidential to mediator).
   - Non-monetary terms (apologies · ongoing relationship · NDA).
8. **Annexes** — Key contractual documents · correspondence · expert valuations.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Without-prejudice marking** mandatory under Art 7 — content inadmissible in subsequent proceedings.
- **Two versions** — one shared with the other side (filtered); one confidential to the mediator (full BATNA + walk-away).
- **Concise** — typically 10-15 pages; mediators do not read 100-page memos.
- **Schedule of disputed facts** — distinguished from agreed facts to focus the mediation.
- **CCBE confidentiality** — extends to mediation communications between EU lawyers.
- **Mediator's role** — facilitative not evaluative unless specifically engaged on evaluative model.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Member States shall ensure that, unless the parties agree otherwise, neither mediators nor those involved in the administration of the mediation process shall be compelled to give evidence in civil and commercial judicial proceedings or arbitration regarding information arising out of or in connection with a mediation process..." (Directive 2008/52/EC, Art 7(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Mediation is increasingly mandatory or mandatory-to-attempt for cross-border B2B disputes in many Member States (IT mandatory pre-action mediation · DE court-attached mediation · NL court-recommended). Solo practitioners often submit the same memo to mediator and counterparty; the two-version discipline is the differentiator between settlement and stalemate.
