# EUIPO Trade Mark Opposition — Notice of Opposition Template

## Source URL
- [Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2018/625 — Article 2](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32018R0625)
- [EUIPO Trade Mark Guidelines (Part C: Opposition)](https://euipo.europa.eu/ohimportal/en/trade-mark-guidelines)

## Last Verified
2026-05-20

## Authority Tier
Primary - Delegated Regulation and official EUIPO Guidelines.

## Currency Warning
Current as of 2026. Oppositions must be filed within the **3-month non-extendable period** following the publication of the contested EUTM application.

## Template Type
Court Form / Intellectual Property

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Contested Application Details**
   - File number of the contested EUTM application.
   - Name of the EUTM applicant.
2. **Opponent Details**
   - Full name and address of the opposing party.
   - Identification of the representative (if any).
3. **Earlier Rights (Basis of Opposition)**
   - Registration or application number of the earlier mark(s).
   - Filing/priority date and registration date.
   - Representation of the earlier mark.
   - Goods and services on which the opposition is based (full or partial).
4. **Grounds of Opposition (Legal Basis)**
   - Relative grounds under EUTMR Art 8:
     - 8(1)(a): Identity of marks and goods/services.
     - 8(1)(b): Likelihood of confusion.
     - 8(3): Unauthorised filing by agents.
     - 8(4): Non-registered marks.
     - 8(5): Reputation of the earlier mark.
     - 8(6): Geographical Indications.
5. **Language of the Proceedings**
   - Selection of one of the two languages of the contested application (must be an Office language).
6. **Payment of Opposition Fee**
   - Confirmation of payment (€320 as of 2026).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Admissibility**: The notice of opposition must contain all Section 1-4 elements to be admissible. Substantiation (evidence) is provided later.
- **Language Choice**: If the first language of the application is not an Office language, the second language MUST be used.
- **Electronic Filing**: Mandatory via the EUIPO "User Area" for professional representatives.
- **Indexing Evidence**: When submitting substantiation later (Art 7 EUTMDR), a clear, numbered index of evidence is required.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
"The notice of opposition shall contain: (a) the file number of the application against which opposition is entered; (b) a clear identification of the earlier mark or earlier right on which the opposition is based; (c) the grounds on which the opposition is based." (Delegated Regulation 2018/625, Article 2(2)).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The standard procedure for IP practitioners to protect their clients' trade mark rights at the EU level. Solo advocates use this template to ensure the initial notice is admissible, preserving the client's right to submit detailed observations and evidence during the adversarial phase.
